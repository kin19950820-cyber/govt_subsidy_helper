# Overview

> ⚠️ **重要：本計劃已於 2026 年 1 月 1 日起停止接受新申請。**
> The Community Care Fund Elderly Dental Assistance Programme (EDAP) **ceased to
> accept applications with effect from 1 January 2026** and has been supplanted
> by the Department of Health's **Community Dental Support Programme (CDSP)**
> (www.communitydental.gov.hk). [2][3]

「關愛基金長者牙科服務資助項目」（下稱「本計劃」）由**關愛基金（Community Care
Fund，CCF）**資助，於 2012 年 9 月推出，交由**香港牙醫學會（HKDA）**負責推行。計
劃為經濟有困難的長者提供**免費的活動假牙（活動義齒）及相關牙科服務**，幫助他們回
復咀嚼能力，改善口腔健康。長者接受服務時**無需向牙醫繳付任何費用**。[1][4][5]

簡單講：以前如果你是**領取「長者生活津貼」的長者**，或者正在使用**社署資助的長者
家居照顧服務**，而又不是綜援受助人、亦未曾參加過院舍外展牙科服務，就可以免費做假
牙、補牙、脫牙、洗牙、通牙根（根管治療）等。**但由 2026 年 1 月 1 日起，本計劃已
不再接受新申請**，改由衞生署的「社區牙科支援計劃（CDSP）」接手。[1][2][4][5]

# Official Purpose

The EDAP "aims to provide free removable dentures and related dental services to
low-income elderly who are users of home care services subvented by the Social
Welfare Department and recipients of the Old Age Living Allowances (OALA) aged 65
or above (i.e. all OALA recipients)." [4]

From 2 July 2024 the programme was enhanced so that the "essential requirement of
having removable dentures fitting" was revised — eligible elderly persons may
receive the covered dental services "even if they are unfit for denture fitting",
to encourage them to manage dental diseases early and to retain natural teeth as
far as possible. [1][4]

# Eligibility

**Objective:** Provide free removable dentures and related dental treatment to
low-income elderly to help improve chewing ability and retain natural teeth. [1][4]

**Who can apply (as at closure on 31 Dec 2025):** Low-income elderly persons who
are **either**:
- **(a)** current recipients of the **Old Age Living Allowance (OALA)** — because
  OALA is granted to persons aged 65 or above, this means **all OALA recipients
  (aged 65+)** are covered; [1][4] **or**
- **(b)** **users of home care services for the elderly under SWD subvention** —
  specifically Integrated Home Care Services (IHCS) or Enhanced Home and
  Community Care Services (EHCCS), where "low-income elderly persons charged at
  level one or two are eligible". [1][6]

…and who fulfil "other related eligibility criteria". [1]

**Who cannot apply:** Since the EDAP was launched in 2012, the beneficiaries have
"all along excluded the elderly persons who have benefited from the [Outreach
Dental Care Programme for the Elderly, ODCP] or those currently receiving
Comprehensive Social Security Assistance (CSSA) under the Social Welfare
Department. These requirements have been clearly stated in the EDAP promotional
pamphlets and the application forms." [5]

**Eligibility criteria (number of times):** Eligible elderly persons can receive
services under the EDAP **a maximum of two times**. To receive the service for
the **second time**, the elderly person **must have reached the age of 75 and
must not have received the dental service under the EDAP within the past five
years**. [1][5]

**Household definition:** Not applicable — eligibility is based on the
individual's status (OALA recipient or SWD home-care service user), not a
household means test administered by the EDAP itself. See glossary. [1][4]

**Income definition:** The EDAP does not run its own income test. It relies on the
means testing already performed for OALA (income/assets limits set by SWD) or on
the IHCS/EHCCS fee level (level 1 or 2), which SWD assigns by income. [1][6]
(For OALA income/asset limits see `knowledge/old_age_living_allowance.md`.)

**Assets definition:** As above — assessed via the underlying OALA or home-care
service, not separately by the EDAP. [4][6]

**Residency requirement:** Not separately stated in the EDAP sources read;
inherited from OALA / SWD home-care service eligibility. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review
(confirm on CCF programme page / application form). [7]

# Means Test

The EDAP is **not separately means-tested by its own office**. It "piggybacks"
on existing means-tested statuses:

- **OALA branch:** OALA is itself a means-tested allowance for persons aged 65 or
  above; all current OALA recipients qualify for the EDAP. [4]
- **Home-care branch:** Users of SWD-subvented IHCS/EHCCS pay a fee "according to
  the fee scale based on their income levels (divided into three levels)"; only
  those charged at **level one or level two** are eligible for the EDAP. [6]

There is **no full-grant / half-grant split** — where eligible, the covered
dental treatment is provided **free of charge to the elderly person** (see
Payment). [1]

# Application Flow

- **Application window:** The EDAP is **closed to new applications from 1 January
  2026**. The last applications had to be submitted **on or before 31 December
  2025**, with the **first consultation no later than 31 January 2026**. [2][3]
  (Before closure the programme accepted applications on a rolling/ongoing basis.) [1]
- **How to apply / submission channel (while open):**
  - **OALA recipients:** bring the required original documents (HK ID card,
    address proof, bank documents showing OALA deposits over the last three
    consecutive months) **in person to any of the 200-plus district service
    units** participating in the Programme (elderly centres, community centres
    and dental clinics). The district service units handle the application and
    arrange the first dental appointment. [1]
  - **Home-care service users:** apply **via their respective SWD-subvented
    service teams**. [1]
- **Review process:** After the district service unit processes the application
  and arranges the first dental appointment, the participating dentist examines
  the elderly person and provides the dental services under the EDAP "if
  considered necessary and suitable after examination by a dentist". [2]

# Approval Flow

- **Approval workflow:** Eligibility is verified by the district service unit /
  service team against the OALA-recipient or home-care-user status and the
  exclusions (no ODCP benefit, not on CSSA). The attending dentist then
  determines the necessary and suitable treatment on clinical examination. [2][5]
- **Additional information requests:** ⚠️ Not documented in the official sources
  read; confirm on the CCF programme page / application form. [7]
- **Appeal:** ⚠️ No appeal mechanism is described in the official sources read.
  Needs Manual Review (CCF page / HKDA Project Office; enquiry hotline 2525 8198). [1][7]

# Payment

- The subsidy is **not paid to the elderly person**. "Elderly persons will not be
  required to make any payments to the participating dentists after receiving
  necessary dental treatment services." [1]
- The **Hong Kong Dental Association (HKDA)**, as the implementing agent, "will
  disburse the subsidies direct to the dentists providing treatment services
  through its Project Office." Dentists must not charge participating elderly
  persons any fee. [1]
- Total programme spending was **close to $300 million in 2022-23**. [4] (No
  fixed per-person cash amount is paid to the elder — the benefit is the free
  service itself.)

# Renewal

- The EDAP is **not an annually-renewed cash allowance**. Each eligible elderly
  person can receive services **a maximum of two times** in total. [1][5]
- The **second** course of service is only available to elderly persons who have
  **reached age 75** and have **not received EDAP dental service within the past
  five years**. [1][5]
- **Cancellation / closure:** The entire EDAP **ceased to accept new applications
  from 1 January 2026** and is being supplanted by the CDSP. Existing applicants
  who applied on or before 31 December 2025 attend their first consultation no
  later than 31 January 2026 and may then receive services if assessed as
  necessary and suitable. [2][3]

# Exceptions

- **Mutually exclusive with CSSA and ODCP:** Elderly persons **currently
  receiving CSSA**, or who **have benefited from the Outreach Dental Care
  Programme for the Elderly (ODCP)**, are **excluded** from the EDAP. (CSSA
  recipients may instead apply for a **dental grant under the CSSA Scheme**; ODCP
  serves frail elders in residential care homes / day care centres.) [5]
- **Two-time cap:** See Renewal — maximum two courses of service, with the
  age-75 / 5-year rule for the second. [1][5]
- **Successor scheme:** From 1 January 2026 the **Community Dental Support
  Programme (CDSP)** (Department of Health, www.communitydental.gov.hk) supplants
  the EDAP. The CDSP uses a **co-payment arrangement** (participants may pay part
  of the cost, with fees waived in full for certain categories such as SWD
  IHCS(Frail)/EHCCS/Home Support Level-1/co-payment users, HA full medical-fee-
  waiver holders including OALA recipients aged 75+, and the homeless). CDSP
  denture fittings are limited to persons with fewer than 20 remaining teeth
  assessed as having eating/chewing difficulty. [2][3]

# FAQ

**Q：我 2026 年仲可唔可以申請長者牙科服務資助（EDAP）？**
**A:** 不可以。本計劃由 **2026 年 1 月 1 日起已停止接受新申請**，改由衞生署的「社
區牙科支援計劃（CDSP）」提供服務。詳情可瀏覽 www.communitydental.gov.hk。 [2][3]

**Q：EDAP 收唔收費？**
**A:** 合資格長者接受所需牙科治療後，**無需向參與計劃的牙醫繳付任何費用**；補助由
香港牙醫學會（HKDA）項目辦事處直接發放給牙醫。 [1]

**Q：邊啲人合資格（計劃仍接受申請時）？**
**A:** 領取「長者生活津貼（OALA）」的長者（即所有 65 歲或以上的 OALA 受助人），
**或**使用社署資助的長者家居照顧服務（IHCS／EHCCS，繳費第一或第二級別）的低收入長
者。**但**正領取綜援、或曾受惠於院舍外展牙科服務（ODCP）者除外。 [1][4][5][6]

**Q：包括哪些牙科服務？**
**A:** 由 2024 年 7 月 2 日起，包括牙科檢查、洗牙及磨光、脫牙、補牙、X 光檢查、拆
除牙橋或牙冠、根管治療（通牙根），以及免費活動假牙及相關服務——即使不適合裝配活動
假牙亦可接受上述服務。 [1]

**Q：可以做幾多次？**
**A:** 最多**兩次**。第二次須年滿 **75 歲**，且過去 **5 年**內未曾接受過 EDAP 牙科
服務。 [1][5]

**Q：查詢電話？**
**A:** EDAP 熱線 **2525 8198**（CDSP 熱線為 2111 3403）。 [1][2]

# Important Definitions

- **OALA (Old Age Living Allowance / 長者生活津貼):** A means-tested allowance for
  persons aged 65 or above. All current OALA recipients qualify for the EDAP.
  See `knowledge/old_age_living_allowance.md`. [4]
- **IHCS / EHCCS:** Integrated Home Care Services / Enhanced Home and Community
  Care Services — SWD-subvented home-care services; users charged at fee **level
  1 or level 2** are eligible for the EDAP. [6]
- **HKDA:** Hong Kong Dental Association — the EDAP's implementing agent; its
  Project Office disburses subsidies to dentists. [1]
- **CCF:** Community Care Fund — the funding source, providing assistance to
  people with financial difficulties, particularly those outside the social
  safety net. [5]
- **ODCP:** Outreach Dental Care Programme for the Elderly — a separate DH
  programme for frail elders in RCHEs/DEs; ODCP beneficiaries are **excluded**
  from the EDAP. [5]
- **CDSP:** Community Dental Support Programme — the DH successor programme that
  supplants the EDAP from 1 January 2026. [2][3]

# Important Dates

- **September 2012** — EDAP launched. [4]
- **Phase expansions by OALA age:** aged 80+ (Sept 2015, phase 1); 75+ (Oct 2016,
  phase 2); 70+ (Jul 2017, phase 3); 65+ (Feb 2019, phase 4 — all OALA
  recipients). [4][9]
- **1 July 2021** — scope expanded to include removal of bridges/crowns and root
  canal treatment (with free removable dentures). [8]
- **2 July 2024** — enhancement: covered services available even if unfit for
  denture fitting. [1]
- **31 December 2025** — last day to submit an EDAP application (through service
  unit); first consultation no later than **31 January 2026**. [2]
- **1 January 2026** — **EDAP ceases to accept new applications**; CDSP supplants
  it. [2][3]

# Examples

*Illustrative only — not a guarantee of eligibility. Based on the rules while the
EDAP was still accepting applications.*

- **Likely eligible (before closure):** Ms Chan, 68, receives the Old Age Living
  Allowance, is not on CSSA and has never used the ODCP. She could apply at a
  district service unit with her HK ID card, address proof and bank documents
  showing her OALA deposits over the last three months, and receive free
  dentures / covered treatment. [1][5]
- **Likely eligible (home-care branch):** Mr Wong, 72, uses SWD-subvented
  Integrated Home Care Services at fee level 2, not on CSSA, no ODCP. He could
  apply via his service team. [1][6]
- **Not eligible:** Mr Lee, 70, currently receives CSSA — excluded (he should use
  the CSSA dental grant instead). Also Ms Ho, 82, who already received her free
  outreach dental treatment under the ODCP — excluded. [5]
- **After 1 Jan 2026:** Any new applicant — the EDAP no longer accepts
  applications; they should use the **CDSP** instead. [2][3]

# Edge Cases

- **Second course of treatment:** Only elders aged 75+ who did not receive EDAP
  service in the past 5 years may receive a second (final) course. [1][5]
- **Applied by 31 Dec 2025 but not yet treated:** Must attend the first
  consultation no later than 31 January 2026; services then provided if the
  dentist assesses them as necessary and suitable. [2]
- **Unfit for dentures:** Since 2 July 2024, still eligible for the other covered
  services (check-up, scaling, extraction, filling, X-ray, removal of
  bridges/crowns, root canal). [1]
- **CSSA / ODCP overlap:** Excluded from the EDAP; alternative supports are the
  CSSA dental grant and the ODCP respectively. [5]
- **Transition to CDSP:** The CDSP's post-enhancement service scope is "in line
  with that of the EDAP", but the CDSP uses a co-payment model (free only for
  specified categories) and restricts denture fitting to elders with fewer than
  20 remaining teeth and assessed eating difficulty. [2][3]
