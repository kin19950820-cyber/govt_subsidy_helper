# Overview

上網費津貼計劃（SIA）由學生資助處（SFO）管理，幫助有需要的家庭支付子女**喺屋企做網上學習（e-learning）**嘅上網費，例如寬頻上網服務，或者電訊商提供嘅 SIM 卡上網費。[1][2]

呢個津貼係**以「一戶」為單位計**，通過入息審查嘅家庭會攞到**一筆固定金額（flat-rate）**嘅現金津貼，**唔理屋企有幾多個小朋友，一個學年只會出一次**。[2][9] 對象係全日制嘅中、小學生，以及全日制修讀職業訓練局（VTC）「應用教育文憑（DAE）」或同等課程嘅學生。[2][9]

Plain English: SIA is a means-tested, household-based flat-rate cash grant that
helps low-income families pay home internet charges so their children can learn
online. It is paid once per household per school year, regardless of how many
children are in the family. [1][2][9]

# Official Purpose

"The Subsidy Scheme for Internet Access Charges provides subsidy to needy
families whose children are full-time students receiving education at primary or
secondary level, or full-time students pursuing Diploma of Applied Education
(DAE) programmes or equivalent courses of the Vocational Training Council (VTC)
to meet the Internet access charges for e-learning at home for their children
(for example, the charges on broadband Internet service or SIM cards provided by
operators of fixed or mobile telecommunications services). The subsidy is
granted on a household basis. Eligible families will receive a flat-rate cash
grant, regardless of the number of children in the family." [2]

# Eligibility

**Objective:** Help needy families meet home internet access charges for their
children's e-learning. [2]

**Who can apply:** A parent or guardian (as recognised under the Guardianship of
Minors Ordinance, Cap. 13) of the student-applicant(s). If the applicant is not
the parent/guardian, they must explain in detail with proof why this cannot be
complied with. [3] The family must have children who are full-time students
receiving education at primary or secondary level, **or** full-time students
pursuing DAE programmes or equivalent VTC courses. [2][8]

**Who cannot apply / who is excluded:**
- Students holding **visitor visas, two-way exit permits, student visas only**,
  or who are **dependents of student-visa / visitor-visa holders**, are not
  eligible. [3][8]
- Families **currently receiving Comprehensive Social Security Assistance
  (CSSA)** should apply directly to the Social Welfare Department (SWD) for
  school-related assistance (including home internet access charges) and **do
  not** apply to SFO. A family cannot receive SIA from SWD and SFO concurrently
  in the same school year. [3][8] (See also — if children are not covered by
  CSSA and the family has not been disbursed the SIA by SWD in 2026/27, the
  family may apply to SFO. [8])
- Families / students already receiving **financial support for home internet
  access charges from any public or private organisation or school** are not
  eligible. [9]

**Eligibility criteria (all must be met):** [8][9]
1. The applicant must **pass the means test** (be issued an Eligibility
   Certificate for the "FULL" or "HALF" level of assistance). [4][9]
2. The student-applicant must be an **unmarried Hong Kong Resident** with the
   **right of abode, right to land, or valid permission to remain without any
   condition of stay** (other than the limit of stay) in Hong Kong, and provide
   a copy of the relevant supporting document. [3][8][9]
3. The family has children who are full-time primary/secondary students, or
   full-time DAE / equivalent VTC students. [2][8]
4. The family is **not in receipt of the same type of assistance under CSSA**,
   and is **not receiving home-internet support from any other public/private
   organisation or school**. [8][9]

**Household definition:** The subsidy is granted on a household basis. For the
means test, "the members of a family" normally refer to the applicant, his/her
spouse, unmarried child/children residing with the family, and the dependent
parent(s) supported by the applicant and/or spouse. [4] See
`glossary.md#household--家庭住戶`. For the definition of "Dependent Parent" refer
to the Notes (SFO 75B(2)). [4][10]

**Income definition:** Assessed via the "Adjusted Family Income" (AFI)
mechanism. Gross annual income of the family includes: the annual income of the
applicant and spouse; **30%** of the annual income of unmarried child/children
residing with the family (if applicable); and contributions from relatives /
friends (if applicable). The assessment period is **1 April 2025 to 31 March
2026** for the 2026/27 school year. [4][10]

**Assets definition:** No asset test is stated for this scheme; the SFO means
test for primary/secondary schemes uses the income-based **AFI** mechanism, not
an assets test. [4] ⚠️ Needs Manual Review — confirm that no separate assets
test applies to SIA.

**Residency requirement:** The student-applicant must be an unmarried Hong Kong
Resident with the right of abode, right to land, or valid permission to remain
without any condition of stay (other than the limit of stay) in Hong Kong.
Holders of visitor visas, two-way exit permits, student visas only, or
dependents of student-/visitor-visa holders are not eligible. [3][8][9]

# Means Test

Means-tested via the SFO's **Adjusted Family Income (AFI)** mechanism, which
determines both eligibility and the level of assistance (Full or Half). [4]

Formula: **AFI = Gross annual income of the family ÷ (Number of family members +
1).** For single-parent families of 2 to 3 members, the "plus 1 factor" in the
divisor is increased to 2. [4]

**2026/27 school-year AFI eligibility benchmarks** (note: AFI is not the average
monthly income of a family): [4]

| AFI between (HK$) | Level of assistance |
|---|---|
| 0 – 46,292 | Full* |
| 46,293 – 89,515 | Half |
| > 89,515 | Ineligible (application not successful) |

\* The Full-level AFI thresholds for **3-member and 4-member families** are
**$56,044** and **$51,561** respectively in 2026/27. For 2-member single-parent
families and 3-member single-parent families, they are regarded as 3-member and
4-member families respectively for determining the Full thresholds and for
calculating AFI. [4]

**Medical expense deduction:** For applicants meeting necessary medical expenses
of family member(s) who are chronically ill or permanently incapacitated, SFO
may deduct relevant medical expenses (with medical certificates and receipts for
1 April 2025 – 31 March 2026). The deduction ceiling is **$24,250 per family
member per year** in 2026/27. [4]

# Required Documents

Per para. 9.2 of the Notes (SFO 75B(2), 2026/27). Applicants must provide the
required supporting documents or SFO cannot process the application. [10]

**Required:**
- Copy of the student-applicant's **one-way permit / visa / permit to remain in
  Hong Kong / Hong Kong Birth Certificate**, if he/she is not a Hong Kong
  Permanent Identity Card holder (e.g. holds a one-way permit / dependent visa /
  other entry visa) or is under 11 years old. [10]
- Copy of **identity documents of the applicant and family members** listed in
  Part II (excluding the student-applicant but including dependent parent(s) if
  applicable) if they are not Hong Kong Identity Card holders. [10]
- Copy of the applicant's **bank statement / first page of bank book** (not
  required if the same, already-verified WFSFAA bank account is reused). [10]
- **Documentary proof of total income** for 1 April 2025 – 31 March 2026 (e.g.
  Inland Revenue Tax Demand Note, Employer's Return, salary statement, bank
  record showing salary; for self-employed, a Profit and Loss Account, etc.).
  [10]

**Supporting (if applicable):**
- (Single-parent families) Supporting documents for separation / divorce, or the
  spouse's Death Certificate. [10]
- Documentary proof of supporting dependent parents. [4][10]
- Documentary proof of unavoidable medical expenses for chronically ill /
  permanently incapacitated family members (1 April 2025 – 31 March 2026). [10]
- SFO may request other supporting document(s) during vetting; its decision is
  final in case of dispute. [10]

# Application Flow

- **Application window:** Fresh application every school year via the
  **"Household Application for Student Financial Assistance Schemes."** For
  2026/27, families are asked to return the completed form **preferably on or
  before 31 May 2026**; continuing applicants who submit completed applications
  and documents online on or before 31 May 2026 receive provisional TA and SIA
  earlier. Under normal circumstances SFO will **not accept applications for
  2026/27 submitted on or after 1 March 2027** (special consideration for
  exceptional cases). [5][6] ⚠️ Needs Manual Review — confirm the official
  opening date of the 2026/27 application window.
- **SIA-specific deadline note:** For applicants who submit the Household
  Application Form **on or after 1 February 2027** (latter half of 2026/27),
  **partial payment** of SIA may be made to successful applicants who pass the
  means test and meet the eligibility criteria. [6]
- **How to apply / submission channel:** Online **E-Form** (via "eWFSFAA" /
  E-Submission) using a computer or mobile device, or a **paper-based**
  application form. E-Form allows saving and retrieving unfinished forms.
  [5][8] See `glossary.md#sfo-e-link--學生資助處電子通`.
- **Review process:** SFO conducts the means test and eligibility assessment.
  During vetting, applicants may be asked to provide supplementary documents. If
  a statement of sworn declaration is required, SFO can provide declaration
  service by appointment at 5/F, Trade and Industry Tower, 3 Concorde Road, Kai
  Tak, Kowloon; or 12/F, One Mong Kok Road Commercial Centre, 1 Mong Kok Road,
  Kowloon. [5]

# Approval Flow

- **Approval workflow:** After completing the eligibility assessment, SFO issues
  **notification of result** to applicants **starting from around August 2026**
  (for the 2026/27 school year). Applicants who pass the means test are issued
  an Eligibility Certificate (EC) for "FULL" or "HALF" assistance. [7][9]
- **Additional-information requests:** During vetting, SFO may notify applicants
  to provide supplementary documents; SFO may require other supporting documents
  for assessment, and in case of disputes SFO's decision is final. [5][10]
- **Appeal:** ⚠️ Needs Manual Review — no explicit appeal / review mechanism for
  SIA was found on the official SIA / procedures / notification pages read; the
  SFO 24-hour enquiry hotline is 2802 2345. Confirm whether a formal appeal or
  review channel exists. [5][7]

# Payment

Payment is made **by direct credit / autopay to the applicant's nominated bank
account**. [7][9] The bank transaction legend is generally shown as
**"WFSFAA-SIA(SFO)"** for SIA (some banks may show different legends). [7]

Timing (2026/27 school year): [7]
- **Provisional TA and SIA** are normally disbursed in **July / August 2026** to
  applicants who successfully applied for textbook assistance in 2025/26 and
  submitted the household application with all necessary information and
  supporting documents **on or before 31 May 2026**, met the eligibility
  criteria and passed the means test.
- **First-time applicants** are normally disbursed TA and SIA from **late
  October / November 2026** after confirmation of eligibility.

**Subsidy rate (flat-rate, per household):**
- **2025/26 school year (confirmed):** Full rate **$1,700** per household; Half
  rate **$850** per household. [1][8]
- **2026/27 school year:** ⚠️ Needs Manual Review — the official FAQ states "The
  rates of subsidy for SIA in the 2026/27 school year will be announced later."
  Do not use the 2025/26 figures for 2026/27 until confirmed. [8]

# Renewal

**Fresh applications must be made every year** — there is no automatic renewal.
[5] Continuing applicants (who successfully applied for financial assistance in
2025/26 on or before 1 March 2026) who submit online on or before 31 May 2026
and remain eligible receive provisional TA and SIA earlier than paper
applicants. From around mid-March 2027, SFO issues notifications for continuing
applicants to access and submit their pre-filled E-form via "eWFSFAA." [5]

**Cancellation / change of circumstances:** Because SIA is a household-based,
once-per-year flat-rate grant, a family cannot receive SIA a second time in the
same school year due to a change of school or other reason. [8] ⚠️ Needs Manual
Review — confirm SIA-specific overpayment recovery / cancellation wording (the
STS overpayment-recovery rules do not necessarily apply to SIA).

# Exceptions

- **CSSA families:** Should apply to SWD (not SFO) for school-related assistance,
  including home internet charges. A family cannot receive SIA from SWD and SFO
  concurrently in the same school year. [3][8]
- **No double subsidy:** Families/students already receiving support for home
  internet access charges from any public/private organisation or school are not
  eligible. [9]
- **One grant per household per year:** Two children, or changing school
  mid-year, does not entitle a family to SIA twice. [8]
- **Families receiving Old Age Living Allowance, Old Age Allowance or Disability
  Allowance** may still submit the Household Application Form to SFO. [3]

# FAQ

**Q:** 上網費津貼一戶可以攞幾多次？屋企有兩個小朋友係咪出兩份？
**A:** No. The SIA is granted on a household basis; an eligible family receives a
flat-rate cash grant **once** per school year, regardless of the number of
children, and is not disbursed twice due to a change of school or other reasons.
[8]

**Q:** 上網費津貼可以點用？
**A:** Parents can use the SIA to meet the internet access charges for e-learning
at home for their children, e.g. broadband internet service, or SIM cards
provided by fixed/mobile telecommunications operators. [8]

**Q:** 2026/27 學年上網費津貼金額係幾多？
**A:** The full rate for **2025/26** is $1,700 per household and the half rate is
$850 per household; the rates for **2026/27 will be announced later**. [8] ⚠️
Needs Manual Review for the 2026/27 amount.

**Q:** 攞緊綜援仲使唔使向學資處申請上網費津貼？
**A:** No. CSSA families should apply directly to SWD for their children's home
internet access charges; they cannot receive the SIA from SWD and SFO in the
same school year. [8]

# Important Definitions

- **Adjusted Family Income (AFI):** SFO's means-test measure — see Means Test
  above and `glossary.md#means-test--入息審查資產審查`. [4]
- **Household basis / flat-rate grant:** One fixed grant per household per year,
  regardless of the number of children. [2][8]
- **Eligibility Certificate (EC) — "FULL" / "HALF":** Certifies the assistance
  level after passing the means test. [9] See `glossary.md` (Full/Half Grant).
- **Full-time DAE / equivalent VTC courses:** Diploma of Applied Education
  programmes or equivalent courses of the Vocational Training Council. [2][9]

# Examples

*Illustrative only — not a guarantee. Actual outcome depends on the means test
and eligibility check.*

- **Likely qualifies:** A 4-member family (two parents, two children in local
  primary/secondary day schools), both children unmarried HK permanent
  residents, not on CSSA, not receiving internet support elsewhere, with an AFI
  of $40,000 (below the 4-member Full threshold of $51,561) — likely qualifies
  for the **Full** flat-rate SIA grant (one grant for the household). [4]
- **Likely half grant:** The same family with an AFI of $70,000 (between $46,293
  and $89,515) — likely qualifies for the **Half** grant. [4]
- **Not eligible:** A family already receiving CSSA (should apply to SWD
  instead), or a family with AFI above $89,515, or one already receiving home
  internet support from another organisation/school. [3][8][9]

# Edge Cases

- **CSSA children partly outside CSSA:** If some children are not covered by CSSA
  and the family has not been disbursed the SIA by SWD in 2026/27, the family may
  apply to SFO — but the family can only receive SIA once in the school year and
  cannot receive it from SWD and SFO concurrently. [8]
- **Change of school mid-year:** Does not entitle the household to a second SIA
  grant in the same school year. [8]
- **Non-HKPIC student / under 11:** Must submit a copy of the one-way permit /
  visa / permit to remain / HK Birth Certificate. [10]
- **Single-parent families:** For 2–3 member single-parent families, the AFI
  divisor "plus 1 factor" is increased to 2, and they are treated as 3-/4-member
  families for the Full threshold. [4]
- **Applicant not the parent/guardian:** Must explain in detail with proof why
  the parent/guardian requirement cannot be met. [3]
- **Applicant not residing in Hong Kong:** Must provide a Hong Kong
  correspondence address for future correspondence. [10]
- **Full-time DAE / VTC students:** Apply for SIA when submitting the Household
  Application; ECs must be submitted to the institution within the time stated in
  the Guidance Notes (within one week of the school commencement date or two
  weeks from EC issue date, whichever is later). [9]
