# Overview

「平安鐘」（正式名稱之一為「緊急召援系統服務」，英文常稱 **Emergency Alarm System /
EAS** 或 **Personal Emergency Link Service**）係一種**24 小時緊急召援服務**：長者或
殘疾人士屋企裝一部召援裝置（或戴一個 SOS 掣），一有事（跌倒、身體不適等）撳掣就可以
即刻聯絡到營運中心嘅接線員求助。**呢個服務本身係由非政府機構（NGO）營運嘅**，當中最
主要嘅營運機構係**長者安居協會（Senior Citizen Home Safety Association, SCHSA）**——
**佢係一間非政府機構，唔係政府部門。** [8]

**本文重點係「政府嘅資助／支援角度」。** 政府（主要係社會福利署，SWD）並無自己直接
營運平安鐘，但透過幾條途徑**幫有需要嘅長者及殘疾人士畀平安鐘嘅費用**，主要有兩條：

1. **綜援「緊急召援系統特別津貼」（Special Grant for Emergency Alarm System）** —
   畀正領取綜援、合資格嘅長者／殘疾人士，用嚟報銷平安鐘嘅安裝費或月費。 [1][3]
2. **「緊急召援系統（平安鐘）服務資助計劃」** — 屬於政府「地區服務及關愛隊伍
   （關愛隊）——支援長者及照顧者計劃」嘅一部分：由**社會福利署資助**、**關愛隊（民政
   事務總署統籌）轉介**、**長者安居協會（NGO）營運**，為經轉介嘅合資格獨居／雙老長者
   及殘疾人士免費安裝及使用平安鐘。 [4][5][8]

The Personal Emergency Link Service ("Safety Bell") is a 24-hour emergency call
service run by NGOs (chiefly SCHSA — an NGO, not a government body). The
Government's role is to **subsidise the fees** for eligible needy elders/persons
with disabilities via two main channels: the **CSSA Special Grant for Emergency
Alarm System**, and the **Subsidy Scheme for Provision of Emergency Call System
Service** under the SWD/Care Teams "Scheme on Supporting Elderly and Carers". [1][3][4][5]

# Official Purpose

There is **no single "scheme" document**; the Government supports the service
through separate subsidy channels:

- **CSSA Special Grant for Emergency Alarm System (EAS Grant):** the SWD's stated
  purpose is that "recipients aged 65 or above who are single or lack family care
  support" may apply for a special grant to cover the **one-off installation fee**
  **or** the **monthly service charges** of an emergency alarm system, so that
  needy elders can obtain any EAS available on the market to meet their needs. [1][3]
- **Subsidy Scheme for Provision of Emergency Call System Service (under the
  "District Services and Community Care Teams – Scheme on Supporting Elderly and
  Carers"):** Care Teams identify and refer in-need singleton/doubleton elderly and
  persons with disabilities, and refer eligible individuals for **SWD-subsidised**
  installation and use of the emergency alarm (call) system, "so that they can
  receive more comprehensive support and care." [4][5][8]

# Eligibility

Because there are **two distinct government support channels**, eligibility
differs. Read each separately.

**Objective:** Enable needy elders / persons with disabilities who live alone or
lack home care to obtain a 24-hour emergency call ("Safety Bell") service, with
the fee met by government subsidy rather than out of pocket. [1][3][4]

**Who can apply / be covered:**

- **CSSA EAS Grant:** CSSA recipients **aged 65 or above who are single, or who
  live in a family whose other members cannot take care of them**; and — per the
  Guide's eligibility note — persons **aged 60 to 64 certified to be more than 50%
  disabled**, or suffering from medical conditions which in a public hospital/clinic
  doctor's opinion may develop into life-threatening conditions requiring immediate
  attention. [1][3]
- **Subsidy Scheme (Care Teams):** In-need **singleton/doubleton elderly** and
  **persons with disabilities** identified and **referred by a Care Team**, in the
  covered districts. Beneficiaries are enrolled **by referral**, not by open
  self-application to SWD. [4][5][8] The detailed applicant criteria published by
  the operator (e.g. singleton elderly aged 65+, all household members aged 65+, or
  singleton person with disability below 65; not already receiving another EAS
  financial allowance; receiving Old Age Living Allowance or income within a
  specified limit) appear only on the **NGO (SCHSA) page** and are **not confirmed
  on a `.gov.hk` page**. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review [8]

**Who cannot apply:**

- For the **CSSA EAS Grant** — persons who are **not** CSSA recipients, or who do
  not meet the age/care-support/disability criteria in the eligibility note. [1]
- For the **Subsidy Scheme** — persons **not referred by a Care Team**, or outside
  the covered districts, or who do not meet the (NGO-published) applicant criteria.
  ⚠️ Needs Manual Review on the precise exclusion rules (NGO source only). [8]

**Eligibility criteria:** See "Who can apply" above; the CSSA criteria are the only
ones fully sourced from `.gov.hk`. [1][3]

**Household definition:** For the CSSA EAS Grant, assessment follows the general
**CSSA household** basis (the applicant must be a CSSA recipient). For the Subsidy
Scheme, the referral targets **singleton (one) or doubleton (two) elderly
households** and persons with disabilities. [4][5] See `glossary.md#household`.

**Income definition:** For the CSSA EAS Grant, the applicant must already be within
the CSSA net (i.e. have passed the CSSA income test). [1][2] For the Subsidy Scheme,
an income limit / Old Age Living Allowance receipt is described only on the NGO page.
⚠️ Needs Manual Review [8]

**Assets definition:** Not separately stated for either channel beyond the
underlying CSSA asset test for the CSSA EAS Grant. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review whether
the Subsidy Scheme applies any asset test. [8]

**Residency requirement:** Not separately stated in the EAS/Subsidy sources
reviewed; for the CSSA EAS Grant it is inherited from CSSA residency rules.
⚠️ Needs Manual Review [1]

# Means Test

- **CSSA EAS Grant:** Effectively means-tested **through CSSA** — only existing CSSA
  recipients (who have already passed the CSSA income and asset tests) and who meet
  the age/care/disability note can receive it. The grant is paid **on a
  reimbursement / special-grant basis** to cover actual EAS costs up to the caps. [1][3]
- **Subsidy Scheme (Care Teams):** The Government describes the Care Teams outreach
  as reaching in-need households "**regardless of their background and financial
  conditions**" for identification purposes [4]; however, the actual **subsidised
  EAS** is provided to referred eligible individuals, and the operator's page states
  an income limit / OALA condition. Whether a formal means test applies to the
  subsidy itself is **not confirmed on a `.gov.hk` page**. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review [8]

**Current government figures (CSSA EAS Grant), per the 1 April 2026 LegCo reply:**
- **Installation fee:** up to a maximum of **$2,500** on a one-off basis; **or** [1][3]
- **Monthly service charges:** up to a maximum of **$100 per month**. [1][3]
- **Beneficiaries:** 26,653 (2023-24); 25,543 (2024-25); 25,053 (2025-26, as at end
  December 2025). [3]

⚠️ Needs Manual Review: the **NGO service fee** (i.e. the standard, non-subsidised
price a member pays SCHSA for the Care-on-Call / Personal Emergency Link service)
is an **NGO figure, not a government figure** — do not present it as a government
subsidy amount; confirm on the operator's own materials. [8]

# Required Documents

- **CSSA EAS Grant:** No separate application scheme — it is a **special grant under
  CSSA**. A CSSA recipient requests the grant from their SWD Social Security Field
  Unit; reimbursement is against the actual EAS installation/service cost. ⚠️ Needs
  Manual Review: confirm the exact documents (e.g. EAS invoice/receipt, medical
  certification for the 60–64 disability route) from the current CSSA guidance. [1]

- **Subsidy Scheme (Care Teams):** Enrolment is **by Care Team referral**; the
  beneficiary does not submit a competitive application to SWD. Documents requested
  at intake by the operator are **not listed on a `.gov.hk` page**. ⚠️ Needs Manual
  Review [4][8]

# Application Flow

- **Application window:** No fixed annual window for either channel. The CSSA EAS
  Grant is available on an ongoing basis to eligible CSSA recipients [1]; the Subsidy
  Scheme operates on a rolling **referral** basis while the Care Teams scheme runs. [4][5]
- **How to apply / submission channel:**
  - **CSSA EAS Grant:** Through the applicant's **CSSA case at the SWD** (Social
    Security Field Unit); the recipient acquires an EAS on the market and claims the
    special grant. [1][3]
  - **Subsidy Scheme:** **Not self-apply.** A **Care Team** (Home Affairs
    Department-coordinated district body) identifies in-need singleton/doubleton
    elderly and persons with disabilities through visits/contacts and **refers**
    eligible individuals to **SCHSA (NGO)**, whose service is **subsidised by SWD**,
    for installation and use of the emergency call system. Residents can also
    approach **Care Team booths** in their community. [4][5][6][8]
- **Review process:**
  - **CSSA EAS Grant:** SWD verifies the recipient meets the age/care/disability
    note and approves the special grant. [1]
  - **Subsidy Scheme:** Care Team screens/identifies; the operator processes the
    referral and installs the device. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review on the exact
    verification steps (NGO/SWD split). [8]

# Approval Flow

- **Approval workflow:**
  - **CSSA EAS Grant:** Approved by the SWD as part of CSSA case management. [1]
  - **Subsidy Scheme:** Care Team referral → SWD-subsidised installation by the NGO
    operator. [4][8]
- **Additional information requests:** Not specified in the government sources
  reviewed. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review [1][8]
- **Appeal:** For CSSA, decisions are subject to the **SWD Social Security Appeal
  Board** mechanism that applies to CSSA generally. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review whether
  an EAS-grant refusal is separately appealable. For the Subsidy Scheme, no appeal
  mechanism is stated in the sources reviewed. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review [2][8]

# Payment

The Government does **not pay cash to the elder**; it meets the **service cost**:

- **CSSA EAS Grant:** Paid as a **special grant / reimbursement** covering the actual
  EAS installation fee (one-off, up to $2,500) **or** monthly service charges (up to
  $100/month), added to the CSSA payment mechanism. [1][3]
- **Subsidy Scheme:** SWD **subsidises the operator (SCHSA)** to provide the emergency
  call system service to the referred beneficiary; the beneficiary receives the
  device/service, generally at no charge to them. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review on the exact
  subsidy amount/duration paid to the operator (not published on a `.gov.hk` page). [4][8]

# Renewal

- **CSSA EAS Grant:** Continues while the recipient remains an eligible CSSA
  recipient; the monthly-service-charge option recurs monthly (up to $100). [1][3]
- **Subsidy Scheme:** The Government stated it will **continue** the "District
  Services and Community Care Teams – Scheme on Supporting Elderly and Carers" for
  **three years**, assisting households referred by Care Teams in installing and using
  the emergency alarm system as necessary. [5] ⚠️ Needs Manual Review on the length of
  the subsidised service term per beneficiary and any renewal/re-referral rule. [8]
- **Cancellation:** ⚠️ Needs Manual Review — confirm what happens if the beneficiary
  stops meeting eligibility (e.g. CSSA case closes, or the elder no longer lives
  alone), and the operator's service-termination terms. [1][8]

# Exceptions

- **Two channels may not stack:** A person receiving the CSSA EAS Grant would
  typically already have their EAS fee met; the Subsidy Scheme's operator criteria
  (NGO page) reportedly exclude those already receiving another EAS financial
  allowance. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review on the exact anti-double-subsidy rule. [8]
- **Other government routes to an EAS fee:** Public Housing tenants may be dealt with
  under the Housing Authority's own EAS policy; the **Community Care Fund** and other
  SWD/community-care programmes have at times supported emergency alarm/support
  services. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review to confirm whether a **current** Community Care
  Fund programme subsidises this service and its terms. [3]
- **Intelligent accident-detection pilot (separate):** A two-year pilot from March
  2026 installs **intelligent accident detection systems for 300+ high-risk
  households** (singleton/doubleton elderly and households with persons with
  disabilities), **sponsored by the HKEX Foundation, with no participant fees** — a
  **distinct** initiative from the platform-based "Safety Bell". Do not conflate. [3][5]
- **Cancellation / overpayment:** For the CSSA EAS Grant, standard CSSA overpayment-
  recovery rules apply. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review on specifics. [1]

# FAQ

**Q:** 平安鐘係咪政府部門營運？
**A:** 唔係。平安鐘（緊急召援服務）係由**非政府機構（NGO）**營運，最主要係**長者安居
協會（SCHSA）**。政府（社會福利署）嘅角色係**資助有需要嘅長者／殘疾人士畀費用**，或
經**關愛隊**轉介合資格人士去接受資助服務。 [4][8]

**Q:** 我攞緊綜援，政府會唔會幫我畀平安鐘錢？
**A:** 如果你係**綜援受助人、65 歲或以上而獨居**（或家人無法照顧你），你可申請綜援嘅
「**緊急召援系統特別津貼**」——安裝費一次過最多 **$2,500**，或月費每月最多 **$100**。
60 至 64 歲、經證明超過 50% 殘疾或有可能危及生命嘅病況者亦可能合資格。 [1][3]

**Q:** 我唔係攞綜援，仲有冇其他政府資助？
**A:** 有可能。政府「地區服務及關愛隊伍——支援長者及照顧者計劃」下嘅「**緊急召援系統
（平安鐘）服務資助計劃**」，由**社署資助、關愛隊轉介、長者安居協會營運**，為經轉介嘅
合資格獨居／雙老長者及殘疾人士提供資助平安鐘服務。詳細申請條件請向關愛隊或營運機構
查詢。 [4][5][8]（⚠️ 部分申請條件只見於機構網頁，需人手覆核。）

**Q:** 政府資助金額係幾多？
**A:** 綜援特別津貼：安裝費最多 **$2,500**（一次過）或月費最多 **$100**。 [1][3] 資助
計劃對營運機構嘅資助金額**未有公開於 `.gov.hk`**，需人手覆核。 [8]

**Q:** 我要去邊度搵關愛隊？
**A:** 關愛隊由**民政事務總署**統籌，全港 18 區都有；你可去社區內嘅關愛隊街站或按民政
事務總署網頁嘅聯絡途徑查詢。 [5][6]

# Important Definitions

- **平安鐘 / Emergency Alarm System (EAS) / Personal Emergency Link Service** — a
  24-hour emergency call service: a home device or wearable SOS button connects the
  user to an operator's call centre for help in an emergency. Operated by NGOs (chiefly
  SCHSA). [7][8]
- **CSSA Special Grant for Emergency Alarm System (EAS Grant)** — a special grant under
  the CSSA Scheme covering an eligible recipient's EAS installation fee or monthly
  charge. Introduced by SWD in May 1996. [1][3]
- **Subsidy Scheme for Provision of Emergency Call System Service** — SWD-funded
  subsidy under the Care Teams "Scheme on Supporting Elderly and Carers", operated by
  SCHSA, for Care-Team-referred eligible elders/PWD. [4][8]
- **Care Teams (District Services and Community Care Teams) / 關愛隊** — district bodies
  coordinated by the **Home Affairs Department** that conduct visits, identify in-need
  singleton/doubleton elderly and carers, and make referrals (including for subsidised
  EAS). [5][6]
- **SCHSA (Senior Citizen Home Safety Association) / 長者安居協會** — an **NGO** and the
  main operator of the "Safety Bell" service; **not a government department**. [8]
- See `glossary.md` for CSSA / household / means-test terms.

# Important Dates

- **May 1996** — SWD introduced the "Special Grant for Emergency Alarm System" for
  eligible elderly CSSA recipients (reimbursement basis). [3]
- **Late March 2024** — Government launched the "District Services and Community Care
  Teams – Scheme on Supporting Elderly and Carers" pilot in **Tsuen Wan and Southern
  districts (36 sub-districts)**, including subsidised emergency alarm systems. [4]
- **1 April 2024** — SCHSA (NGO) began accepting Care Team referrals under the Subsidy
  Scheme for Provision of Emergency Call System Service (SWD-funded). ⚠️ Confirm on a
  `.gov.hk` page. [8]
- **April 2025 (Q2 2025)** — Scheme **extended to all 18 districts** across the
  territory. [5]
- **As at end August 2025** — Over **1,500 households** had been referred by Care Teams
  and **received subsidy for installation of the emergency alarm system**, involving
  over **1,970 elderly persons and persons with disabilities**. [5]
- **1 April 2026** — LegCo reply: CSSA EAS Grant amounts confirmed ($2,500 one-off /
  $100 per month); beneficiaries 26,653 (2023-24), 25,543 (2024-25), 25,053 (2025-26 to
  end Dec 2025); intelligent accident-detection pilot (300+ households, from March 2026,
  HKEX Foundation-sponsored). [3]

# Examples

*Illustrative only — not a guarantee of eligibility; each channel decides
separately.*

- **Likely covered (CSSA route):** A 72-year-old CSSA recipient living alone applies to
  her SWD Social Security Field Unit for the EAS special grant; SWD reimburses her EAS
  monthly service charge up to $100/month. [1][3]
- **Likely covered (Subsidy Scheme route):** An 80-year-old couple (both 65+) living
  alone in the Southern District are visited by a Care Team, assessed as in need, and
  referred to SCHSA for a SWD-subsidised emergency call device installed at no charge to
  them. [4][8] (Exact income criteria ⚠️ Needs Manual Review [8].)
- **Likely not covered by these government subsidies:** A 68-year-old with adequate
  income, not on CSSA and not referred by a Care Team, who buys an EAS privately — he
  pays the NGO's standard service fee himself. [8]

# Edge Cases

- **Person aged 60–64:** May qualify for the **CSSA EAS Grant** only if certified more
  than 50% disabled or with a medical condition that may become life-threatening. [1]
- **Living with family:** A CSSA recipient 65+ can still qualify if the family members
  present **cannot take care of** him/her. [1]
- **Public housing tenants:** May be handled under Housing Authority EAS policy;
  ⚠️ Needs Manual Review. [3]
- **Already subsidised:** A person already receiving an EAS financial allowance may be
  excluded from the Subsidy Scheme (per NGO page). ⚠️ Needs Manual Review. [8]
- **Community Care Fund:** Whether a current CCF programme subsidises this service is
  ⚠️ Needs Manual Review. [3]
