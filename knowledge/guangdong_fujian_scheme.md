# Overview

廣東計劃同福建計劃係社會福利署「公共福利金計劃（社會保障津貼／SSA）」下的
安排，等**已經移居廣東／福建**的合資格香港長者，可以**繼續**領取**高齡津貼
（生果金／OAA）**或**長者生活津貼（長生津／OALA）**，而**唔使每年返香港**。 [1][2][3]

簡單講：如果你符合資格（夠年齡、住夠香港年期），但你選擇長住廣東或福建，就可以
透過呢兩個計劃**照出津貼**——廣東計劃畀住廣東的長者，福建計劃畀住福建的長者。
兩個計劃的條件、金額基本一樣，只係居住的省份唔同。同一般（香港）的高齡津貼／
長生津最大分別係：**攞津貼期間你要住喺廣東／福建**（一個「付款年」內至少住滿
**60日**），而唔係住喺香港。 [3]

Both schemes cover OAA (age 70+, non-means-tested) and OALA (age 65+,
means-tested). Current monthly amounts are **OAA HK$1,675** and **OALA
HK$4,345**, with effect from **1 February 2026**. [6][1][2]

# Official Purpose

"The Guangdong Scheme and Fujian Scheme under the Social Security Allowance
Scheme comprise Old Age Allowance and Old Age Living Allowance to provide monthly
allowance for eligible Hong Kong residents who choose to reside in Guangdong
(only applicable to Guangdong Scheme) or Fujian (only applicable to Fujian
Scheme)." [3]

"Old Age Allowance is designed to provide a monthly allowance on a
non-means-tested basis to Hong Kong residents who are 70 years of age or above to
meet their special needs arising from old age. Old Age Living Allowance is to
provide a special allowance per month to supplement the living expenses of Hong
Kong residents aged 65 or above who are in need of financial support and having
income and assets not exceeding the prescribed limits." [3][1][2]

The schemes let eligible Hong Kong elderly persons who choose to reside in
Guangdong or Fujian receive OAA / OALA **without having to return to Hong Kong
each year**. The Guangdong Scheme and Fujian Scheme are **non-contributory**. [1][2][3]

# Eligibility

**Objective:** Enable eligible Hong Kong elderly persons who **choose to reside
in Guangdong or Fujian** to receive OAA (age 70+, non-means-tested) or OALA (age
65+, means-tested) **without returning to Hong Kong each year**. [1][2][3]

**Who can apply:** An applicant is eligible for an allowance under the Guangdong
Scheme / Fujian Scheme if he/she: [3]
- **reaches the qualifying age** — **OALA: aged 65 or above; OAA: aged 70 or
  above**; [3][4]
- satisfies the **residence requirement** (see below): (1) has been a **Hong Kong
  resident for at least seven (7) years**; **and** (2) has resided in Hong Kong
  **continuously for at least one (1) year immediately before the date of
  application** (absence from Hong Kong up to a maximum of **90 days** during the
  one-year period is treated as residence in Hong Kong); [3]
- **continues to reside in Guangdong (Guangdong Scheme) / Fujian (Fujian Scheme)
  during receipt of allowance** (subject to the permissible-absence rule — see
  Means Test / Payment); [3][4]
- is **not** in receipt of other allowances under the Social Security Allowance
  Scheme **or** assistance under the Comprehensive Social Security Assistance
  (CSSA) Scheme; [3]
- is **not** being detained in legal custody or admitted to a penal institution; [3]
- **is required to surrender his/her public rental housing (PRH) unit or delete
  his/her name from the tenancy before leaving Hong Kong** if he/she is an
  authorised PRH tenant; [3]
- for **OALA only**: has **income and assets below the prescribed limits** (see
  Means Test). This condition is **not applicable** to OAA. [3]

**Who cannot apply / cannot continue:** A person below the qualifying age; a
person whose presence in Hong Kong is unlawful or who is permitted to stay for a
purpose other than residence (e.g. imported workers, visitors); a person who has
not satisfied the 7-year / continuous-1-year residence rule; a person already
receiving another SSA allowance or CSSA; a person detained in legal custody or in
a penal institution; a recipient who does not continue to reside in Guangdong /
Fujian (subject to the permissible-absence rule). For OALA, a person whose
income/assets (and the spouse's/cohabiting partner's, where applicable) exceed
the prescribed limits. [3]

**Eligibility criteria:** Qualifying age **and** the HK residence rule (7 years +
continuous 1 year before application) **and** continuing residence in Guangdong /
Fujian while receiving payment **and** not concurrently on another SSA allowance
/ CSSA **and** not in legal custody / a penal institution **and** (if a PRH
tenant) surrender of the PRH unit **and** (OALA only) income + assets within the
prescribed limits. [3]

**Household definition:** OAA is assessed on the **individual applicant only**
(non-means-tested — no household financial assessment). For OALA, where the
applicant's marital status is **"Married" or "Cohabiting"**, the applicant must
also provide the spouse's / cohabiting partner's personal particulars, income and
assets, and the couple is assessed against the **"Married couples"** limits;
otherwise the **"Single person"** limits apply. "Cohabiting" applies only where
the applicant (i) currently lives with the cohabiting partner in the same
household, (ii) lives on shared resources with the partner, and (iii) agrees to
provide the partner's personal and financial information to SWD. [3]
See `glossary.md#household`.

**Income definition (OALA only):** Same as ordinary OALA. "Income" includes wages
from employment; income from handiwork, business, etc. (salaries, wages, monthly
commissions or bonuses, and monthly income from self-employment); retirement
benefits/pensions; net income on rentals collected; and payout from annuity
scheme(s). **Excluded:** contributions from family members, relatives or friends,
and monthly payments received under the Reverse Mortgage Programme (only where the
collateral is an owner-occupied property) and the Policy Reverse Mortgage
Programme — but any unspent, accumulated savings/cash is treated as an **asset**. [3]

**Assets definition (OALA only):** Same as ordinary OALA. "Assets" include land
and non-owner-occupied properties; cash in hand; bank savings; investments in
stocks and shares (including bonds, trust funds, interests in sole
proprietorships/partnerships/firms/limited companies, and accrued retirement
benefits); vehicles for commercial use/investment and their business licence; and
gold bars and gold coins. **Excluded:** one owner-occupied residential property
that is the **principal place of residence in Guangdong / Fujian** and one
self-use parking space; a columbarium niche for future self-use; and the cash
value of insurance schemes. For a spouse/cohabiting partner **aged below 65**,
accrued MPF benefits (mandatory + tax-deductible voluntary contributions) are
disregarded as assets (and monthly mandatory MPF contributions disregarded as
income); withdrawn accrued benefits count as assets. [3]

**Residency requirement:** (a) **HK resident for ≥ 7 years**, and **continuous
residence in Hong Kong for ≥ 1 year immediately before the date of application**,
with up to **90 days** absence within that year treated as residence; and (b)
**during receipt of payment the recipient must reside in Guangdong / Fujian**,
satisfying a **minimum residence of 60 days in that province in a payment year**
(see Payment). Absences arising from **paid work outside Hong Kong** during the
pre-application year may be disregarded with sufficient documentary proof; where
absence exceeded 90 days because of **medical treatment outside Hong Kong**, the
Director of Social Welfare may exercise discretion to disregard the excess. [3][4]

# Means Test

**OAA under these schemes is NOT means-tested** — income and assets are not
assessed for OAA applicants. [3][1][2]

**OALA under these schemes IS means-tested** on both income and assets. The
prescribed limits are the **same** as ordinary OALA in Hong Kong: [3][7]

| Category | Monthly Income Limit (HK$) | Asset Limit (HK$) |
|---|---|---|
| Single person | **$10,900** | **$415,000** |
| Married couples | **$16,680** | **$630,000** |

Both figures are stated **with effect from 1 February 2026**. [7][3]

"Single person" limits apply to marital status "Never Married", "Separated",
"Divorced" or "Widowed"; "Married couples" limits apply to "Married" or
"Cohabiting". There is **no partial/half rate** — each allowance is a single flat
amount. [3][6]

> Note on residence: unlike ordinary OAA/OALA (which requires residence **in Hong
> Kong** during payment), a Guangdong/Fujian Scheme recipient must reside **in
> Guangdong / Fujian** — at least **60 days in a payment year** — to keep the full
> year's allowance. [3][4]

# Required Documents

Return the completed **"Guangdong Scheme and Fujian Scheme Application Form"
(SWD307 GD/FJ)** by post or in person, together with **two recent photos** of the
applicant and photocopies of the following, then bring the originals to the
interview: [3][4][5]

**Required (both OAA and OALA):**
- **Any documents confirming the applicant's identity, age and residence in Hong
  Kong** (e.g. Hong Kong Identity Card, Birth Certificate) **and residential
  address in Guangdong / Fujian** (e.g. rental receipts, utilities bills). [3]
- **Document of the applicant's bank account** (e.g. first page of bank passbook
  or a bank statement) clearly showing the applicant's name and account number.
  **Only a sole account is acceptable** for payment; a **joint account is not
  applicable**. [3][4]
- **All travel documents of the applicant** — the pages showing personal
  particulars (e.g. Passports, HKSAR Document of Identity for Visa Purposes,
  travel documents issued by other countries) and the absence record; brought as
  originals at the interview. [3]

**Supporting (if applicable):**
- **Identity document of the applicant's spouse / cohabiting partner** — OALA
  only, where marital status is "Married" or "Cohabiting". (Not applicable to
  OAA.) [3]
- **All documents on the monthly income and assets** of the applicant (and the
  spouse / cohabiting partner where "Married" or "Cohabiting"), including all bank
  account documents — OALA only. (Not applicable to OAA.) [3]
- For an applicant already settled in Guangdong / Fujian who may be unable to
  travel to Hong Kong for health reasons: **medical proof(s) issued by a public
  hospital / clinic** on the health condition. [3]
- If the application is made by an **appointee**: the appointee's own identity
  document and the bank-account document for receiving the applicant's allowance
  (joint bank account not accepted). [3]

# Application Flow

- **Application window:** Accepted **year-round**. SWD accepts an application
  **within one month prior** to the applicant reaching the respective qualifying
  age (the **65th birthday for OALA / 70th birthday for OAA**); under this
  arrangement the date of reaching the qualifying age is regarded as the date of
  application, and payment is calculated from the date the applicant fulfils the
  qualifying age and satisfies all eligibility criteria. [3][4]
- **How to apply / submission channel:** Applicants **must complete the
  application procedures in person in Hong Kong**. First return the completed
  application form with two recent photos and photocopies of supporting documents
  to the **Social Security Field Unit (Guangdong Scheme and Fujian Scheme)
  [SSFU(GDS & FJS)]** — by post or in person — **or apply by online form**
  (https://www.sssof.swd.gov.hk/eApplyGDFJ/Start). The form and Guidance Notes can
  be downloaded from the Guangdong Scheme site (www.swd.gov.hk/gds) or Fujian
  Scheme site (www.swd.gov.hk/fjs), or obtained from any social security field
  unit or from SSFU(GDS & FJS) by phone or post. [3][4]
- **Review process:** On receipt of an application SWD arranges (by prior
  appointment) an **interview** with the applicant at SSFU(GDS & FJS); the
  applicant brings the original documents. For an applicant **already settled in
  Guangdong / Fujian** who can produce public hospital/clinic medical proof that
  they may be unable to travel to Hong Kong, SWD requests its **agent** to assist
  in completing the application procedures. After the investigation, a
  **notification letter** is issued. [3][4]

# Approval Flow

- **Approval workflow:** SWD verifies the qualifying age, the 7-year /
  continuous-1-year HK residence rule (via travel documents), the Guangdong /
  Fujian residential address, non-concurrency with other SSA allowances / CSSA,
  that the applicant is not in legal custody, and (if a PRH tenant) surrender of
  the PRH unit. For OALA it also assesses the means test (income + assets against
  the single/couple limits). Subject to meeting all eligibility criteria, payment
  is arranged. [3][4]
- **Additional information requests:** SWD may ask for supplementary information;
  providing complete information and all supporting documents speeds up
  processing. SWD conducts **data matching** periodically / on a need basis with
  other government departments, banks and organisations (Immigration Department,
  Treasury, Correctional Services Department, Land Registry, Companies Registry,
  Transport Department, HKMC Annuity Limited, etc.) and, through its agents,
  reviews of targeted cases by postal review or home visit. [3]
- **Appeal:** An applicant who disagrees with SWD's decision may lodge an appeal
  with the **Social Security Appeal Board** (an independent body of non-officials
  appointed by the Chief Executive). An appeal must be lodged **within four (4)
  weeks** immediately following the date of notification of SWD's decision.
  Enquiries about appeal procedures can be made with SSFU(GDS & FJS). [4][8]

# Payment

- **Amount:** **OAA HK$1,675** per month; **OALA HK$4,345** per month — both
  **with effect from 1 February 2026** (flat rate; same as ordinary OAA/OALA in
  Hong Kong). [6][1][2]
- **Payment method:** OAA/OALA is paid **monthly**. The allowance is credited to
  the applicant's / appointee's **designated bank account** — a **sole account is
  required; a joint bank account is not accepted**. The applicant/appointee is
  responsible for arranging collection of the allowance (e.g. arranging with the
  bank to remit payments to him/her in Guangdong / Fujian, or withdrawing via a
  Hong Kong ATM card on the Mainland); any expenses are borne by the recipient
  (some banks offer fee-waiving arrangements). Alternatively the recipient may
  choose to receive the allowance from SWD **directly into a bank account
  designated by SWD on the Mainland, with no service fee**. [4][3]
- **Cross-border remittance (from 6 July 2026):** Hong Kong elderly persons
  applying for or receiving OAA/OALA under the Guangdong Scheme and Fujian Scheme
  may choose to receive their assistance payments in **Hong Kong Dollars (HKD)**,
  remitted by SWD directly into their **HKD ("Type I") accounts at designated
  Mainland banks (Bank of China / ICBC branches in Guangdong or Fujian) free of
  charge**. [1][2]
- **Effective date of payment:** Calculated from the **date of receipt of the
  application by SWD** (or the date of application / referral if referred by
  another organisation) **or the date on which the applicant satisfies the
  eligibility criteria, whichever is later**. Where the application is made within
  one month before the qualifying-age birthday, payment is calculated from the
  date the applicant fulfils the qualifying age and satisfies all eligibility
  criteria. [3][4]
- **Permissible absence from Guangdong / Fujian while receiving payment:** In a
  **payment year** (the 12-month period from the date eligibility is met), a
  recipient who has resided in Guangdong / Fujian for **not less than 60 days**
  keeps the **full year's allowance**, provided total absence from that province
  (leaving for other Mainland provinces, Hong Kong, Macao or overseas) does **not
  exceed 305 days (306 days in a leap year)**. A recipient who resided in the
  province for **less than 60 days** in a payment year is entitled to allowance
  **only for the periods actually resident** there; any overpayment must be
  refunded. Absences arising from **paid work outside the province** may be
  disregarded with sufficient documentary proof. [3][4]

# Renewal

No fixed annual re-application; recipients continue to receive the allowance so
long as they remain eligible (including continuing to reside in Guangdong /
Fujian and satisfying the 60-day residence rule). SWD, **through its agents,
carries out an annual review of all OAA and OALA cases** under the schemes by
**postal review or spot check (home visit)**. On receiving a postal review form,
a recipient must complete it, have the declaration **witnessed by a relative or
friend aged 18 or above** (or by SWD/agent staff if in Hong Kong), and return it
within the time specified; **failure to complete the postal review on time may
lead SWD to stop payment** as continued eligibility cannot be established. As long
as the recipient resides in the province for **≥ 60 days** in a payment year, he
/ she is **not required to report** temporary absences (regardless of reason). [4]

# Exceptions

- **Cannot be held together with any other SSA allowance or CSSA.** A person
  receiving another SSA allowance or CSSA is not eligible. [3]
- **PRH surrender:** An authorised PRH tenant must surrender the PRH unit or
  delete his/her name from the tenancy **before leaving Hong Kong**. [3]
- **SWD's agents:** SWD has appointed the **New Home Association** (Guangdong
  Scheme) and the **International Social Service Hong Kong Branch** (Fujian
  Scheme) as agents to help applicants/recipients complete applications and
  reviews and, for the Guangdong Scheme, to arrange escort service where a frail
  recipient has genuine need to travel between Guangdong and Hong Kong (recipient
  bears transport costs). [4]
- **Returning to Hong Kong / moving between provinces:** A recipient who decides
  to leave Guangdong / Fujian and return to settle in Hong Kong must report and
  complete formalities with SSFU(GDS & FJS) to arrange another SSA allowance
  (subject to meeting its criteria). A recipient wishing to change permanent
  residence between Guangdong and Fujian (and convert to the other scheme) must
  report to SSFU(GDS & FJS) or the SWD agent and **obtain SWD's approval before
  changing residence**. [4]
- **Special One-Off Arrangement conversions:** A recipient who benefited from the
  Special One-Off Arrangement and wishes to convert to another SSA allowance in
  Hong Kong, or between the Guangdong and Fujian Schemes, **must either** have
  received OAA/OALA under the scheme for **not less than one year** (a payment
  break of not more than 10 days is allowed) **or** have resided in Hong Kong
  continuously for one year immediately before applying for the other SSA
  allowance — in addition to meeting that allowance's eligibility criteria (e.g.
  OALA income/asset limits). [4]
- **Cancellation / change of circumstances:** The applicant/appointee must report
  immediately any change — e.g. change of address, absence from Guangdong /
  Fujian exceeding the permissible limit, income/assets exceeding the limits after
  the specified date, change of marital status (OALA), being imprisoned or
  detained in legal custody for more than 29 days, or allocation of a PRH unit /
  tenancy right — so the allowance can be adjusted or the case closed. [3]
- **Overpayment recovery / offence:** Any overpaid allowance must be refunded to
  SWD. A person who knowingly or wilfully provides a false statement or withholds
  information to obtain the allowance by deception, or deliberately fails to report
  changes, **commits a criminal offence** and may be liable to prosecution. [3]

# FAQ

**Q:** 廣東計劃／福建計劃係咪要每年返香港先出到糧？
**A:** 唔使。呢兩個計劃就係畀已移居廣東／福建的合資格長者，**唔使每年返港**都可以
繼續出高齡津貼或長生津。 [1][2][3]

**Q:** 幾多歲先申請到？
**A:** **長者生活津貼：65歲或以上；高齡津貼：70歲或以上**。（同一般香港的
OALA／OAA一樣。） [3][4]

**Q:** 攞津貼期間要住喺邊度？離開幾耐會停？
**A:** 要住喺**廣東（廣東計劃）／福建（福建計劃）**。喺一個「付款年」內，只要在該
省份住滿**60日**，離省（去內地其他省、香港、澳門或海外）總日數不超過**305日
（閏年306日）**，可獲全年津貼；若住少於60日，只可就實際居於該省期間獲發津貼，
多付的須退還。 [3][4]

**Q:** 兩個計劃包唔包長者生活津貼（長生津）？
**A:** **包。** 廣東計劃同福建計劃都同時涵蓋**高齡津貼（OAA）同長者生活津貼
（OALA）**。 [3][1][2]

**Q:** 每月攞幾多錢？
**A:** 由**2026年2月1日**起，**高齡津貼每月$1,675，長者生活津貼每月$4,345**
（劃一金額，同香港一樣）。 [6][1][2]

**Q:** 我住緊公屋，可唔可以申請？
**A:** 如你係公屋認可租戶，**離開香港前必須交回公屋單位或從租約中刪除姓名**。 [3]

# Important Definitions

- **Guangdong Scheme / Fujian Scheme（廣東計劃／福建計劃）** — SSA arrangements
  paying OAA/OALA to eligible HK elderly who choose to reside in Guangdong /
  Fujian, without returning to HK each year. [1][2][3]
- **Qualifying age（合資格年齡）** — OALA 65+; OAA 70+. [3][4]
- **Payment year（付款年）** — the 12-month period from the date the recipient meets
  the eligibility criteria; used for the 60-day residence / permissible-absence
  calculation. [3][4]
- **60-day minimum residence（60日最低居留期）** — residence of ≥ 60 days in
  Guangdong / Fujian in a payment year to keep the full year's allowance
  (permissible absence ≤ 305 / 306 days). [3][4]
- **Special One-Off Arrangement（特別一次性安排）** — a one-off arrangement affecting
  later conversion between schemes / return to HK (one-year rule). [4]
- OAA (Old Age Allowance), OALA (Old Age Living Allowance), CSSA, PRH — see
  `glossary.md`. Cross-reference `old_age_allowance.md` and
  `old_age_living_allowance.md`.

# Important Dates

- **Current monthly amounts effective:** **1 February 2026** — OAA **$1,675**,
  OALA **$4,345**. [6][1][2]
- **OALA income & asset limits effective:** **1 February 2026** — Single: income
  $10,900 / assets $415,000; Married couples: income $16,680 / assets $630,000. [7][3]
- **HK residence rule:** HK resident ≥ 7 years; continuous residence ≥ 1 year
  before application; up to **90 days** absence in that year treated as residence. [3]
- **In-province residence rule (during payment):** ≥ **60 days** in Guangdong /
  Fujian per payment year; total absence ≤ 305 (306 in a leap year) days for
  full-year allowance. [3][4]
- **Cross-border HKD remittance available from:** **6 July 2026** (direct HKD
  remittance into designated Mainland bank accounts, free of charge). [1][2]
- **Applications accepted year-round**; may apply within **one month** before the
  qualifying-age birthday (65 for OALA / 70 for OAA). [3][4]
- **Appeal deadline:** within **4 weeks** of notification of SWD's decision. [4][8]

# Examples

*Illustrative only — not a guarantee of eligibility.*

- **Likely qualifying (OAA, Guangdong Scheme):** A 72-year-old who has been a HK
  resident for 50 years, resided continuously in Hong Kong in the year before
  applying (absent ≤ 90 days), has moved to Guangdong to live, is not on any other
  SSA allowance or CSSA, and (if a former PRH tenant) has surrendered the PRH unit
  — qualifies for OAA regardless of income/assets, and can receive $1,675/month
  while living in Guangdong, provided he/she resides there ≥ 60 days in each
  payment year. [3][6]
- **Likely qualifying (OALA, Fujian Scheme):** A 67-year-old single person, HK
  resident for 40 years, living in Fujian, with monthly income $3,000 and total
  assets $200,000 (within the single-person limits of $10,900 / $415,000), not on
  OAA/DA/CSSA — qualifies for OALA at $4,345/month, subject to the 60-day Fujian
  residence rule. [3][7]
- **Not qualifying (in-province residence):** A Guangdong Scheme OAA recipient who
  spent only 40 days in Guangdong in a payment year (below 60 days) is entitled to
  allowance only for the days actually resident in Guangdong; the excess is an
  overpayment to be refunded. [3][4]

# Edge Cases

- **Already settled in Guangdong / Fujian and unable to travel to HK:** Where the
  applicant can produce public hospital/clinic medical proof of a health condition
  preventing travel to Hong Kong, SWD's agent assists in completing the
  application in the province (otherwise the applicant must complete procedures in
  person in Hong Kong). [3][4]
- **PRH tenants:** Must surrender the PRH unit / delete name from tenancy before
  leaving Hong Kong; later allocation of a PRH unit or tenancy right must be
  reported. [3]
- **Switching between schemes / returning to HK:** Permanent moves between
  Guangdong and Fujian (and scheme conversion) need SWD's prior approval;
  returning to settle in HK requires reporting to SSFU(GDS & FJS) to arrange
  another SSA allowance. The one-year (or continuous-HK-residence) rule applies to
  conversions under the Special One-Off Arrangement. [4]
- **Person unable to apply personally:** If medically certified unfit to make a
  statement (verified by SWD), the Director of Social Welfare appoints an
  appointee, who must give the reasons for the applicant's having to settle in
  Guangdong / Fujian. [3][4]
- **Applicants who currently reside in HK:** May apply through the Guangdong /
  Fujian Scheme in preparation for moving; existing SSA/CSSA recipients who choose
  to convert must also complete the GD/FJ application form providing the Guangdong
  / Fujian residential address and (OALA) updated income/asset information. [3]
