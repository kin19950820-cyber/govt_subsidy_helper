# Overview

高齡津貼（高津，OAA），俗稱「生果金」，是社會福利署「公共福利金計劃（社會保障
津貼／SSA）」下的一項**按月**津貼，用來幫補**70歲或以上**的香港長者因年老而
引起的特別需要。 [1][3][6]

簡單講：只要**夠70歲**、**住夠香港年期**（住滿7年、申請前連續住滿1年），而且
**唔係同時領取其他社會保障津貼（例如長者生活津貼／傷殘津貼）或綜援**，就可以每月
拎一筆津貼。最重要的一點：高齡津貼**唔使查你嘅入息同資產（免入息及資產審查）**，
即係唔理你有幾多錢、有幾多身家都可以申請。 [3][6][7][8]

The current monthly payment of OAA is **$1,675** (with effect from 1 February
2026). [8]

# Official Purpose

"Old Age Allowance … are designed to provide a monthly allowance on a
non-means-tested basis to Hong Kong residents who are 70 years of age or above …
to meet their special needs arising from old age …" [7][6]

The objective of the SSA Scheme is "to provide a monthly allowance to Hong Kong
residents who are severely disabled or who are 65 years of age or above to meet
special needs arising from disability or old age." [1]

# Eligibility

**Objective:** Provide a monthly, **non-means-tested** allowance to Hong Kong
residents **aged 70 or above** to meet special needs arising from old age. [7][6]

**Who can apply:** A person is eligible for OAA if he/she: [2][3][6][7]
- is **aged 70 or above**; [3][6][7]
- has been a **Hong Kong resident for at least seven (7) years**, **and** has
  resided in Hong Kong **continuously for at least one (1) year immediately
  before the date of application** (absence from Hong Kong up to a maximum of
  **90 days** during the one-year period is treated as residence in Hong Kong); [2][7]
- **continues to reside in Hong Kong during receipt of payment**; [2][7]
- is **not** in receipt of any other allowance under the SSA Scheme (e.g. Old Age
  Living Allowance, Disability Allowance) **or** assistance under the
  Comprehensive Social Security Assistance (CSSA) Scheme; and [2][6][7][8]
- is **not** being detained in legal custody or admitted to a penal institution. [2][6][7]

**Who cannot apply / cannot continue:** A person under 70; a person whose presence
in Hong Kong is unlawful or who is permitted to stay for a purpose other than
residence (e.g. imported workers, visitors); a person who has not satisfied the
7-year / continuous-1-year residence rule; a person receiving another SSA
allowance or CSSA; a person detained in legal custody or in a penal institution;
a recipient who does not continue to reside in Hong Kong. [2][6][7]

**Eligibility criteria:** Age 70+ **and** residence rule **and** continuing HK
residence **and** not concurrently on another SSA allowance / CSSA **and** not in
legal custody / a penal institution. **No income or asset test applies.** [2][3][6][7]

**Household definition:** OAA is assessed on the **individual applicant only**.
Because OAA is non-means-tested, there is **no** spouse/cohabiting-partner or
household financial assessment (unlike OALA). [7][8]
See `glossary.md#household`.

**Income definition:** **Not applicable.** OAA applicants "are not required to go
through a means test", so income is not assessed. [1][6][7]

**Assets definition:** **Not applicable.** OAA is non-means-tested, so assets are
not assessed. [1][6][7]

**Residency requirement:** HK resident for **≥ 7 years**, and continuous
residence for **≥ 1 year immediately before the date of application**, with up to
**90 days** absence within that year treated as residence. Absence from Hong Kong
means leaving for the Mainland, Macao or overseas countries/territories. Where an
applicant has been absent for more than 90 days during the one-year period
because of the need to receive **medical treatment outside Hong Kong**, the
Director of Social Welfare may exercise discretion to disregard the excess
absence (subject to reason and evidence); absences arising from **paid work
outside Hong Kong** may also be disregarded with sufficient documentary proof.
The recipient must **continue to reside in Hong Kong** while receiving payment. [2][7]

# Means Test

**OAA is NOT means-tested.** "Applicants of Disability Allowance and Old Age
Allowance … under the Scheme are **not required to go through a means test**." [1][6]
"The scheme is non-means-tested." — i.e. income and asset limits do **not**
apply. [8]

There are therefore **no income or asset cut-offs** and **no partial/half rate**
for OAA. Every eligible applicant receives the same flat-rate monthly payment. [4][7][8]

> Contrast: the sibling scheme **Old Age Living Allowance (OALA)** for those aged
> 65+ **is** means-tested (see `old_age_living_allowance.md`). A person aged 70+
> whose income/assets exceed the OALA limits can instead apply for the
> non-means-tested OAA. [8]

# Required Documents

Documents required for an OAA application (photocopies to be returned with the
application form; originals brought for verification at interview): [6][7]

**Required:**
- **Any documents confirming the applicant's identity, age and residence** in
  Hong Kong (e.g. Hong Kong Identity Card, Birth Certificate). [7]
- **Document of the applicant's bank account** (e.g. first page of bank passbook
  or a bank statement) clearly showing the applicant's name and account number.
  **Only a sole account is acceptable** for payment of the allowance; a **joint
  account is not applicable**. [7]
- **All the applicant's travel documents** — the pages showing personal
  particulars (e.g. Passports, HKSAR Document of Identity for Visa Purposes,
  travel documents issued by other countries) **and** the pages showing the
  **absence record during the one-year period immediately before the
  application**. [7]

**Supporting (if applicable):**
- Where the applicant cannot make the application himself/herself, the identity
  document and bank-account document of the **appointee/agent** and proof of
  relationship. [7]
- Original identity and travel documents (e.g. Passports, HKSAR Re-entry Permit,
  Home Visit Permit, One-way Permit, etc.) for verification during processing. [7]

> Note: because OAA is non-means-tested, **no income or asset documents are
> required** (unlike OALA, which additionally requires spouse/partner ID and
> financial evidence). [7]

# Application Flow

- **Application window:** Applications are accepted **year-round**. SWD accepts an
  OAA application **within one month prior** to the applicant reaching the
  qualifying age (the **70th birthday**); under this arrangement the date the
  applicant reaches age 70 is regarded as the date of application, and payment is
  calculated from the date the applicant fulfils the qualifying age and satisfies
  all eligibility criteria. [2][5][7]
- **How to apply / submission channel:** The applicant (or a relative/friend, or
  an appointee/agent) may apply directly to the **social security field unit** for
  the applicant's place of residence — **by phone, by fax, by e-mail, by post, by
  online form, or in person** — or through a **referral** to SWD by another
  government department or a non-governmental organisation. The "Social Security
  Allowance Scheme Application Form" (SWD307) and "Application for Social Security
  Allowance Guidance Notes" (SWD675) can be downloaded from the SWD website
  (https://www.swd.gov.hk) or obtained from social security field units, and
  returned with photocopies of supporting documents by post or in person. [5][6][7]
- **Review process:** On receipt of an application, an officer of SWD **arranges
  an interview** with the applicant to verify eligibility. All application
  procedures are processed in Hong Kong. After completion of investigation, a
  **formal notification letter** is sent to the applicant. [6][7]

# Approval Flow

- **Approval workflow:** SWD verifies age, the 7-year / continuous-1-year
  residence requirement (via travel documents and absence records),
  non-concurrency with other SSA allowances / CSSA, and that the applicant is not
  in legal custody, following the interview. **No means test is conducted.**
  Payment is arranged once all eligibility criteria are met. [6][7]
- **Additional information requests:** SWD may, where necessary, ask the applicant
  for supplementary information; providing complete information and all necessary
  supporting documents speeds up processing. SWD also conducts **data matching**
  (with the Immigration Department, Treasury, Correctional Services Department,
  Land Registry, Companies Registry, Hospital Authority, Transport Department,
  HKMC Annuity Limited, etc.) and **case reviews** (including home visits or by
  post) to cross-check information. [7]
- **Appeal:** An applicant who disagrees with SWD's decision may lodge an appeal
  with the **Social Security Appeal Board** (an independent body of non-officials
  appointed by the Chief Executive). An appeal must be lodged **within four (4)
  weeks** immediately following the date of notification of the Director of Social
  Welfare's decision. [6][9]

# Payment

- **Amount:** The current monthly payment of OAA is **$1,675** (with effect from
  **1 February 2026**). [8]
- **Rate structure:** Paid at a **flat rate** to all eligible applicants; there is
  no full/half distinction. [4]
- **Payment method:** The allowance is **credited monthly** into the applicant's
  (or guardian's/appointee's) designated bank account in Hong Kong. A **sole
  account is required — a joint bank account is not accepted**. Under exceptional
  circumstances, special delivery of cash directly to the applicant can be
  arranged. [6][7]
- **Effective date of payment:** Payment is calculated from the **date of receipt
  of the application** by SWD (or the date of application/referral if referred by
  another organisation) **or the date of eligibility, whichever is later**. Where
  the application is made within one month before the 70th birthday, payment is
  calculated from the date the applicant fulfils the qualifying age and satisfies
  all eligibility criteria. [5][6][7]
- **Permissible absence while receiving payment:** In a **payment year** (the
  12-month period from the date eligibility is met), a recipient who has resided
  in Hong Kong for **not less than 60 days** keeps the full year's allowance,
  provided total absence does not exceed **305 days (306 days in a leap year)**.
  A recipient who resided in Hong Kong for **less than 60 days** in a payment year
  is entitled to allowance only for the periods actually resident in Hong Kong;
  any overpayment must be refunded. Absences arising from **paid work outside
  Hong Kong** may be disregarded with sufficient documentary proof. [6][7]

# Renewal

OAA does not require a fixed annual re-application; recipients continue to receive
the allowance so long as they remain eligible (including continuing to reside in
Hong Kong and not taking up another SSA allowance or CSSA). SWD conducts periodic
**case reviews** and **data matching**. A person previously found ineligible may
re-apply at any time — by phone, fax, e-mail, post, online form, referral, or in
person to the field unit — submitting a completed application form with supporting
documents; SWD will again interview the applicant. [6][7]

# Exceptions

- **Cannot be held together with any other SSA allowance or CSSA.** A person
  receiving Old Age Living Allowance, Disability Allowance (Normal or Higher)
  under the SSA Scheme, or CSSA is **not** eligible for OAA. [2][6][7][8]
- **Cancellation / change of circumstances:** The applicant/guardian/appointee
  must report immediately any change — e.g. change of address, imprisonment or
  detention in legal custody, or absence from Hong Kong exceeding the permissible
  limit — so that the amount can be adjusted or the case closed. [7]
- **Overpayment recovery:** Any overpaid allowance must be **refunded to SWD**. A
  person who knowingly or wilfully provides a false statement or withholds
  information to obtain the allowance by deception **commits a criminal offence**
  and may be liable to prosecution. [7]
- **Detention / leaving Hong Kong:** A person detained in legal custody or in a
  penal institution is not eligible; a recipient must continue to reside in Hong
  Kong (subject to the permissible absence limits above). [2][6][7]
- **Other welfare needs:** If, during enquiries, it becomes apparent the applicant
  has other welfare needs (e.g. financial assistance), SWD will look into these
  and render appropriate assistance (e.g. CSSA or rehabilitation services). [6]

# FAQ

**Q:** 高齡津貼（生果金）使唔使查我嘅入息同資產？
**A:** **唔使。** 高齡津貼是**免入息及資產審查（non-means-tested）**的，唔理你有
幾多收入或身家都可以申請，只要夠70歲、符合居港年期同其他條件就得。 [1][6][8]

**Q:** 幾多歲先可以申請生果金？
**A:** **年滿70歲或以上。** （70歲以下、有經濟需要的長者，可考慮申請65歲起、需要
審查的**長者生活津貼／OALA**。） [3][6][8]

**Q:** 攞緊長者生活津貼（長生津）、傷殘津貼或綜援，可唔可以同時攞生果金？
**A:** **唔可以。** 領取任何其他社會保障津貼（例如長生津、傷殘津貼）或綜援嘅人士，
都**唔符合**高齡津貼資格。 [2][6][7][8]

**Q:** 每月可以攞幾多錢？
**A:** 由**2026年2月1日**起，每月**$1,675**（劃一金額）。 [8]

**Q:** 攞津貼期間離開香港，會唔會影響出糧？
**A:** 在一個「付款年」內，只要在港住滿**60日**，且離港總日數不超過**305日
（閏年306日）**，可獲全年津貼；若在港少於60日，只可就實際居港期間獲發津貼，多付
的須退還。 [6][7]

# Important Definitions

- **Non-means-tested（免入息及資產審查）** — for OAA, income and assets are **not**
  assessed; no financial limits apply. [1][6][8]
- **Qualifying age（合資格年齡）** — for OAA this is **70 years or above**. [3][7]
- **Payment year（付款年）** — the 12-month period from the date the recipient meets
  the eligibility criteria; used for the permissible-absence calculation. [6][7]
- **Permissible limit of absence（准許離港期限）** — must reside in HK ≥ 60 days in a
  payment year; total absence ≤ 305 days (306 in a leap year) for full-year
  allowance. [6][7]
- OALA (Old Age Living Allowance), DA (Disability Allowance), CSSA — see
  `glossary.md`.

# Important Dates

- **Current monthly amount effective:** **1 February 2026** — **$1,675**. [8]
- **Applications accepted year-round**; may apply within **one month** before the
  70th birthday (date of reaching 70 is treated as the date of application). [5][7]
- **Residence rule:** HK resident ≥ 7 years; continuous residence ≥ 1 year before
  application; up to **90 days** absence in that year treated as residence. [2][7]
- **Appeal deadline:** within **4 weeks** of notification of SWD's decision. [6][9]

# Examples

*Illustrative only — not a guarantee of eligibility.*

- **Likely qualifying:** A 72-year-old, HK resident for 50 years, who did not
  leave Hong Kong (or left for fewer than 90 days) in the year before applying,
  not receiving OALA / DA / CSSA, and not in custody — qualifies for OAA
  regardless of income or assets, because OAA is non-means-tested. [3][6][7][8]
- **Likely not qualifying (concurrency):** A 71-year-old already receiving Old Age
  Living Allowance cannot also receive OAA — a person may hold only one SSA
  allowance (and not CSSA) at a time. [2][6][7]
- **Not yet qualifying (age):** A 68-year-old cannot apply for OAA (under 70). If
  in financial need, he/she may instead consider the means-tested OALA (age 65+).
  [3][8]

# Edge Cases

- **Wealthy but aged 70+:** Because OAA is non-means-tested, an applicant with high
  income or substantial assets is still eligible, provided all other criteria are
  met. [1][6][8]
- **Turning 70 soon:** May apply up to one month before the 70th birthday; payment
  starts from when age 70 and all criteria are met. [5][7]
- **New arrivals / short residence:** A person not a HK resident for ≥ 7 years, or
  who has not resided continuously for the year before application (more than 90
  days absent, other than disregarded medical/paid-work absences), does not meet
  the residence rule. [2][7]
- **Extended absence after grant:** Frequent or long stays outside Hong Kong can
  reduce or suspend payment — full-year allowance requires ≥ 60 days' residence
  and ≤ 305 (306 in a leap year) days' absence in a payment year; excess absence
  must be reported and any overpayment refunded. [6][7]
- **Switching from OALA to OAA:** A person aged 70+ whose income/assets exceed the
  OALA limits (so ineligible for OALA) may apply for the non-means-tested OAA
  instead — but cannot hold both at once. [2][8]
- **Cannot apply personally:** For an adult aged 18+ medically certified as unable
  to make a statement, the Director of Social Welfare may appoint an appointee to
  act on his/her behalf. [6][7]
