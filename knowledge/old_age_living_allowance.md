# Overview

長者生活津貼（長生津，OALA）是社會福利署「公共福利金計劃（社會保障津貼）」下的
一項按月津貼，用來幫補**65歲或以上、有經濟需要**的香港長者的生活開支。申請人的
入息同資產必須不超過政府定明的上限（即需要經過「入息及資產審查」）。 [1][2]

簡單講：夠65歲、住夠香港年期、而且錢（入息）同身家（資產）唔超過上限，就可以每月
拎一筆津貼。但**唔可以同時領取高齡津貼（生果金／OAA）、傷殘津貼（DA）或綜援
（CSSA）**。 [1]

The current monthly payment of OALA is **$4,345**. [1]

# Official Purpose

"Old Age Living Allowance (OALA) under the Social Security Allowance Scheme is
designed to provide a special monthly allowance to supplement the living
expenses of Hong Kong elderly people aged 65 or above who are in need of
financial support." [1]

# Eligibility

**Objective:** Supplement the living expenses of HK elderly aged 65 or above who
are in need of financial support. [1]

**Who can apply:** A person is eligible for OALA if he/she: [1]
- is **aged 65 or above**;
- has been a **Hong Kong resident for at least seven (7) years** and has resided
  in Hong Kong **continuously for at least one (1) year immediately before the
  date of application** (absence from Hong Kong up to a maximum of **90 days**
  during the one-year period is treated as residence in Hong Kong);
- has **income and assets not exceeding the prescribed limits** (see Means Test); and
- is **not** in receipt of Old Age Allowance (OAA) or Disability Allowance (DA)
  under the Social Security Allowance Scheme, **or** assistance under the
  Comprehensive Social Security Assistance (CSSA) Scheme.

**Who cannot apply / cannot continue:** A person who is receiving OAA, DA or
CSSA; a person whose income and/or assets (and those of the spouse/cohabiting
partner, if applicable) exceed the prescribed limits; a person **detained in
legal custody or admitted to a penal institution**; a recipient who does **not
continue to reside in Hong Kong** during receipt of payment. [1]

**Eligibility criteria:** Age 65+ **and** residence rule **and** means test
(income + assets) **and** not concurrently on OAA/DA/CSSA. [1]

**Household definition:** Assessment is on the individual, **except** where the
applicant's marital status is **"Married" or "Cohabiting"** — then the
applicant must also provide the spouse's/cohabiting partner's personal
particulars, income and assets, and the couple is assessed against the
**"Married couple"** limits. "Cohabiting" applies only where the applicant
(i) currently lives with the cohabiting partner in the same household, (ii)
lives on shared resources with the partner, and (iii) agrees to provide the
partner's personal and financial information to SWD. Applicants whose status is
"Never Married", "Separated", "Divorced" or "Widowed" provide only their own
particulars and are assessed against the **"Single person"** limits. [1]
See `glossary.md#household`.

**Income definition:** "Income" includes wages from employment; income from
handiwork, business, etc. (salaries, wages, monthly commissions or bonuses, and
monthly income from self-employment); retirement benefits/pensions; net income
on rentals collected; and payout from annuity scheme(s) (regular payouts,
apportioned to a monthly figure). **Excluded** from income: contributions from
family members, relatives or friends, and monthly payments received under the
Reverse Mortgage Programme (RMP, only where the collateral is an owner-occupied
property) and the Policy Reverse Mortgage Programme — but any unspent and
accumulated savings/cash generated is treated as an **asset**. [1]

**Assets definition:** "Assets" include land and non-owner-occupied properties
(real estate and parking space of any use, in or outside Hong Kong); cash in
hand; bank savings; investments in stocks and shares (including bonds, trust
funds, interests in sole proprietorships/partnerships/firms/limited companies,
and accrued retirement benefits); vehicles for commercial use/investment (e.g.
taxi, public light bus) and their business licence; and gold bars and gold
coins. **Excluded** from assets: one owner-occupied residential property that is
the principal place of residence in Hong Kong and one parking space for
self-use; a columbarium niche for future self-use; and the cash value of
insurance schemes. For a spouse/cohabiting partner **aged below 65**, accrued
MPF benefits from mandatory and tax-deductible voluntary contributions are
disregarded under the asset test (and monthly mandatory MPF contributions are
disregarded under the income test); however, accrued retirement benefits once
**withdrawn** are treated as assets. [1]

**Residency requirement:** HK resident for **≥ 7 years**, and continuous
residence for **≥ 1 year immediately before the date of application**, with up
to **90 days** absence within that year treated as residence (relaxed from 56 to
90 days starting **1 September 2023**). The recipient must **continue to reside
in Hong Kong** while receiving payment. [1]

# Means Test

OALA is **means-tested** on both **income** and **assets**. The prescribed
limits (including OALA under the Guangdong Scheme and Fujian Scheme) are: [3][1]

| Category | Monthly Income Limit (HK$) | Asset Limit (HK$) |
|---|---|---|
| Single person | **$10,900** | **$415,000** |
| Married couple | **$16,680** | **$630,000** |

Both figures are stated **with effect from 1 February 2026**. [3]

There is **no partial/half rate** — OALA is a single flat allowance. Applicants
who pass the means test and meet all criteria receive the standard monthly
payment of **$4,345**. [1]

"Single person" limits apply to marital status "Never Married", "Separated",
"Divorced" or "Widowed"; "Married couple" limits apply to "Married" or
"Cohabiting". [1]

# Required Documents

Supporting documents required at application/interview: [4][1]

**Required:**
- Any documents confirming the applicant's **identity, age and residence** in
  Hong Kong (e.g. Hong Kong Identity Card, Birth Certificate).
- **All the applicant's travel documents** — the pages showing personal
  particulars (e.g. Passports, HKSAR Document of Identity for Visa Purposes,
  travel documents issued by other countries) **and** the pages showing the
  **absence record during the one-year period immediately before the
  application**.
- **Document of the applicant's bank account** (e.g. first page of bank passbook
  or a bank statement) clearly showing the applicant's name and account number;
  **only a sole account is acceptable** for payment of the allowance.

**Supporting (if applicable):**
- **Identity document of the applicant's spouse or cohabiting partner** — only
  where marital status is "Married" or "Cohabiting". [4][1]
- Photocopies of relevant supporting documents on income and assets to
  substantiate the means test. [1]

# Application Flow

- **Application window:** Applications are accepted **year-round**. SWD accepts
  an OALA application **within one month prior** to the applicant reaching the
  qualifying age (65th birthday); under this arrangement the date of reaching age
  65 is regarded as the date of application. [5]
- **How to apply / submission channel:** The applicant (or a relative/friend, or
  an authorised person) may apply directly to the **social security field unit**
  for the applicant's place of residence — **by phone, fax, e-mail, post, online
  form, or in person** — or through a **referral** by another government
  department or NGO. The "Social Security Allowance Application Form" and
  "Application for Social Security Allowance Guidance Notes" can be downloaded
  from the SWD website (https://www.swd.gov.hk) and returned, with photocopies of
  supporting documents, to the field unit by post or in person. [1][5]
- **Review process:** On receipt of an application, SWD arranges for staff to
  **interview the applicant** to verify eligibility and process the application.
  If someone applies on the applicant's behalf, SWD still interviews the
  applicant to verify eligibility. [1]

# Approval Flow

- **Approval workflow:** SWD assesses the means test (income + assets against the
  applicable single/couple limits) and the age, residence and non-concurrency
  criteria, following the interview and verification of documents. [1]
- **Additional information requests:** Applicants must provide supporting
  documents (identity, travel documents/absence record, bank account, and
  spouse/partner information where applicable); making a **false or incorrect
  declaration** of income/assets makes any allowance paid an **overpayment**
  recoverable by SWD. [1][4]
- **Appeal:** An applicant/recipient dissatisfied with SWD's decision on
  eligibility or payment may appeal to the **independent Social Security Appeal
  Board**. An appeal must be lodged **within 4 weeks** from the date of
  notification of SWD's decision, using an appeal form (available at field units,
  downloadable, or via online form). The Board's decisions are final. [7]

# Payment

- **Amount:** The current monthly payment of OALA is **$4,345**. [1]
- **Payment method:** The allowance is paid into the applicant's **sole bank
  account** (a sole account is required for payment of the allowance). [4]
- **Effective date of payment:** Payment is calculated from the **date of
  receipt of the application** by SWD (or the date of application/referral if
  referred by another organisation) **or the date of eligibility, whichever is
  later**. Where the application is made within one month before the 65th
  birthday, payment is calculated from the date the applicant **fulfils the
  qualifying age and satisfies all eligibility criteria**. [5]
- **"Grace period":** For the **12 full months** counting from the date of
  eligibility, the case status is assumed unchanged — subsequent increases in the
  recipient's (and spouse's/partner's) income and/or assets that would otherwise
  affect eligibility are **disregarded**, and payments are not affected. This
  does **not** apply where a false/incorrect declaration was made at application
  (any allowance paid is then treated as overpayment). Example given: if eligible
  on 1 September 2022, the grace period runs 1 September 2022 to 31 August 2023
  inclusive. [1]

# Renewal

OALA does not require a fixed annual re-application; recipients continue to
receive the allowance so long as they remain eligible (including continuing to
reside in Hong Kong). SWD conducts **case reviews** of recipients' circumstances
(income/assets). An applicant previously found ineligible because income/assets
exceeded the limits may **re-apply** at any time — by fax, e-mail, post, online
form, referral, or in person to the field unit, submitting a completed signed
application form with supporting documents; SWD will again interview the
applicant. [1]

# Exceptions

- **Cannot be held together with OAA, DA or CSSA.** A person receiving Old Age
  Allowance, Disability Allowance (under the Social Security Allowance Scheme) or
  CSSA is **not** eligible for OALA. [1]
- **Married/cohabiting couples and OAA:** If a couple is assessed on the
  "Married couple" limits and their (joint or individual) income/assets exceed
  the limits, they are not eligible for OALA; however, if **aged 70 or above**
  they may apply for the **non-means-tested Old Age Allowance (OAA)**. [1]
- **Detention / leaving Hong Kong:** A person detained in legal custody or in a
  penal institution is not eligible; a recipient must continue to reside in Hong
  Kong during receipt of payment. [1]
- **Overpayment recovery:** Allowance paid on the basis of a false or incorrect
  declaration is treated as overpayment and must be duly returned to SWD. [1]

# FAQ

**Q:** 攞緊「生果金」（高齡津貼／OAA）、傷殘津貼或綜援，可唔可以同時攞長生津？
**A:** 唔可以。領取 OAA、傷殘津貼或綜援嘅人士，都**唔符合**長生津資格。 [1]

**Q:** 夫妻其中一個入息／資產超咗上限，可唔可以一個攞長生津、一個攞高齡津貼？
**A:** 已婚／同居人士必須按「夫婦」的入息及資產上限評估；如夫婦（或其中一方）入息／
資產超過上限，就唔符合長生津資格。但如年滿**70歲**，可以申請**免入息及資產審查**
嘅高齡津貼（OAA）。 [1]

**Q:** 攞咗長生津之後，如果之後收入或資產多咗，會唔會即刻停？
**A:** 由符合資格當日起計嘅 **12 個月「寬限期」**內，其後收入／資產增加令超出上限，
可以獲豁免，津貼照出。但如申請時作出虛假／不正確申報，已發放嘅津貼會當作多付款項
追回。 [1]

**Q:** 邊啲嘢當作「入息」／「資產」？自住樓計唔計？
**A:** 一個作為香港主要居所嘅**自住住宅物業**同一個**自用車位**唔計入資產；保險
現金價值、自用骨灰龕位亦唔計。家人／親友嘅資助唔當入息（但未用完嘅積蓄會當資產）。
[1]

# Important Definitions

- **Single person limits** — apply to "Never Married", "Separated", "Divorced",
  "Widowed" applicants. [1]
- **Married couple limits** — apply to "Married" or "Cohabiting" applicants;
  spouse/partner particulars, income and assets must be provided. [1]
- **"Grace period"** — the 12 full months from the date of eligibility during
  which subsequent increases in income/assets are disregarded. [1]
- **Owner-occupied property** — one principal HK residence and one self-use
  parking space, disregarded under the asset test. [1]
- OAA (Old Age Allowance), DA (Disability Allowance), CSSA — see `glossary.md`.

# Important Dates

- **Income and asset limits effective:** **1 February 2026** — Single: income
  $10,900 / assets $415,000; Married couple: income $16,680 / assets $630,000. [3]
- **Absence limit relaxed:** From **56 to 90 days** effective **1 September
  2023** (within the pre-application one-year continuous residence period). [1]
- **Applications accepted year-round**; may apply within one month before the
  65th birthday. [5]
- **Appeal deadline:** within **4 weeks** of notification of SWD's decision. [7]

# Examples

*Illustrative only — not a guarantee of eligibility.*

- **Likely qualifying (single):** A 68-year-old widow, HK resident for 40 years,
  who did not leave HK in the past year, with a monthly income of $3,000 and
  total assets of $200,000, and not on OAA/DA/CSSA — is within the single-person
  limits ($10,900 income / $415,000 assets) and meets age and residence rules. [3][1]
- **Likely not qualifying (couple):** A married couple both aged 66 with combined
  assets of $700,000 exceed the married-couple asset limit of $630,000, so
  neither qualifies for OALA. As they are under 70, they cannot yet apply for the
  non-means-tested OAA either. [3][1]

# Edge Cases

- **New arrivals / short residence:** A person who has not been a HK resident for
  ≥ 7 years, or who has not resided continuously for the year before application
  (more than 90 days absent), does not meet the residence rule. [1]
- **Cohabiting couples:** Assessed on married-couple limits only where the three
  cohabitation conditions (same household, shared resources, consent to disclose
  partner's information) are met. [1]
- **Reverse mortgage / annuity recipients:** RMP monthly payments and family
  contributions are excluded from income, but unspent accumulated amounts become
  assets; regular annuity payouts count as income while the pre-surrender value
  is disregarded (surrender value, if any, counts as assets). [1]
- **Spouse/partner under 65:** Their MPF accrued benefits (mandatory + tax-
  deductible voluntary contributions) are disregarded as assets, and monthly
  mandatory MPF contributions disregarded as income; withdrawn benefits count as
  assets. [1]
- **Turning 65 soon:** May apply up to one month before the 65th birthday; payment
  starts from when age and all criteria are met. [5]
