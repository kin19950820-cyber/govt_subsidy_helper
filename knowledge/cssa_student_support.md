# Overview

綜合社會保障援助（綜援 / CSSA）是社會福利署（社署）的一個安全網，為入息及資產都
低於規定水平、無法自己維持基本生活的個人及家庭提供現金援助。綜援本身不是「學生津貼」，
但**綜援家庭的在學子女**可以透過綜援中的「特別津貼」，領取**學校費用**及**就學相關開支**
的資助（例如學費、上學車費、午膳津貼、公開試考試費，以及書簿、文具、校服等一筆過的
「選定就學開支項目津貼」）。[1][3][4]

簡單講：如果一家人已經攞緊綜援，仔女讀書要用的錢，係向**社署**直接申請，唔使再另外
去學生資助處（SFO）申請學生資助。[6]

# Official Purpose

The CSSA Scheme provides financial assistance to bring the income of needy
individuals and families up to a prescribed level to meet their basic needs. [3]
Within the recognized needs, **special grants** are payable to meet the specific
needs of an applicant or family, and these include **school fees and
school-related expenses** for students in the household. [3][4]

# Eligibility

**Objective:** Bring a needy family's income up to a prescribed level for basic
needs; school-related special grants cover the school costs of children in a
CSSA household. [3][4]

**Who can apply:** A Hong Kong resident who satisfies the residence requirement
and passes both the income and asset tests. Applications must be made on a
**household basis** if the applicant lives with other family members — the total
income and assets of all members in the same household are counted. [3] The
school-related grants apply to **students in the household** (from child-care
centre up to upper-secondary level for the flat-rate school-expenses grant). [3]

**Who cannot apply:**
- Persons whose presence in Hong Kong is unlawful, and persons admitted for a
  purpose other than residence (e.g. imported workers, visitors under Immigration
  Regulation Cap. 115A). [3]
- Families **already receiving CSSA should NOT also apply to the Student Finance
  Office (SFO)** for the corresponding student financial assistance — school
  needs are met through CSSA instead (see Exceptions). [6]

**Eligibility criteria (CSSA itself):**
1. Residence requirement (see below). [3]
2. Financial tests — pass **both** income and asset tests. [3]
3. For an able-bodied adult aged 15–59 in normal health, one of the additional
   criteria in Section 3C of the Guide (e.g. **studying**, caring for young/sick
   family members, meeting the earnings/hours threshold, or joining the Support
   for Self-reliance Scheme). Note: a person who is **studying** can be "not
   available for work for reasons acceptable to the Department". [3]

**Household definition:** If the applicant lives with other family members the
application is made on a household basis; the income and assets of all family
members in the same household are aggregated. See `glossary.md#household`. [3]

**Income definition:** Total assessable monthly household income = earnings from
employment plus other income (e.g. rent collected, contributions from
relatives/friends, regular free meals from employers, maintenance payments), less
disregarded earnings / disregarded training allowance where applicable. [3]

**Assets definition:** Total value of capital assets in Hong Kong, Macao, the
Mainland or overseas — including land/properties, cash, stored value on Stored
Value Facilities (e.g. Octopus, e-wallets), bank savings, cash value of insurance
policies, pre-surrender/surrender value of annuities, stocks and shares, deposits
in betting accounts, and other readily realizable assets — must not exceed the
prescribed asset limit. Non-owner-occupied residential property is treated as an
asset; the owner-occupied home is treated under special rules (see Means Test).
[3][5]

**Residency requirement:** The applicant must (a) be a Hong Kong resident; (b)
have held Hong Kong resident status for **not less than one year**; and (c) have
**resided in Hong Kong for at least one year** (need not be continuous or
immediately before the application; absences up to a maximum of **56 days** are
treated as residence). **Hong Kong residents aged below 18 are exempted from
requirements (b) and (c).** In exceptional circumstances the Director of Social
Welfare may exercise discretion. [2][3]

# Means Test

CSSA is **non-contributory but means-tested** — the applicant/family must pass
both the income test and the asset test. [3][5]

**Income test:** The family is eligible if total assessable monthly household
income is **insufficient to meet their total monthly recognized needs** under the
Scheme. Recognized needs = standard rates + supplements + special grants (the
school-related grants are part of recognized needs). Assistance payable =
Recognized needs − Assessable income. [3]

**Asset test (limits per October 2023 Guide):** [3]

Single-person cases:
- Able-bodied adult: **$35,000**
- Child / elderly / disabled / person medically certified in ill-health: **$52,000**

Family cases — cases involving any able-bodied adult (able-bodied adults/children):
1 member **$23,000**; 2 **$46,000**; 3 **$69,000**; 4 or above **$92,000**.
For elderly/disabled/ill-health members: 1 **$52,000**; 2 **$78,000**; 3
**$104,000**; 4 **$130,000**; 5 **$156,000**; 6 **$182,000**. Family limits are
the sum of the two components. [3]

Owner-occupied residential property: value totally disregarded where any member
is old/disabled/ill-health or where there is no able-bodied adult aged below 50;
otherwise included after a 12-month grace period (extendable at the Director's
discretion for single parents with a youngest child below 15). [3]

**Disregarded earnings:** For cases on CSSA for not less than two months, monthly
earnings are partially disregarded up to a maximum of **$4,000/month** (first
$1,200 at 100%, next $5,600 at 50%); the first two months' income from a new job
can be totally disregarded (once every two years). [3]

⚠️ **Needs Manual Review:** CSSA standard rates, supplement amounts, asset limits
and the school-related grant amounts are **adjusted (usually annually, with
effect from 1 February)**. The figures above are quoted from the **October 2023**
Guide (2023/24 school year for school grants). Confirm the **current-year
(2025/26 / 2026/27)** amounts against the latest SWD leaflet before relying on
any dollar figure. [3][5]

# Required Documents

**Required (as requested by SWD staff, depending on the case):** [3]
- Identity documents (HK Identity Card for those aged 11+, Birth Certificate for
  under-11, Certificate of Exemption, etc.).
- Marriage certificate / divorce documents (if applicable).
- Proofs of accommodation expenses (rent receipts, rent card, tenancy agreement,
  water & sewage receipts, rates demands, management-fee receipts).
- Proofs of assets (bank passbooks/statements for the past year, time-deposit
  receipts, insurance policy statements, annuity documents, stocks, property/land
  ownership, etc.).
- **Proofs of educational expenses (e.g. receipts of school fee and school-bus
  fee) and student handbooks.** [3]
- Travel documents (passport, Re-entry Permit, One-way Permit, Home Visit Permit,
  etc., with printed e-Visa if applicable).
- Proofs of income (MPF pay-records, pay slips, employer's statement).
- Follow-up appointment slips in public hospitals/clinics (if applicable).
- Proofs of financial relationship with other family members.
- Other relevant documents as required by the Department.

**Supporting (for a school-related special-grant reimbursement claim):** [3]
- **All relevant receipts** for the school expense being claimed (school fee,
  school-bus/transport, HKDSE examination fee, JUPAS application fee, etc.).
- For advance payment: a quotation or official estimate of cost (in certain
  circumstances a verbal confirmation is acceptable).

# Application Flow

- **Application window:** Open all year (no fixed window) — a person may apply for
  CSSA at any time. The **flat-rate school grant** is normally paid **before the
  commencement of the school year**; if the family becomes eligible after the
  school year starts, the grant is adjusted accordingly. [3]
- **How to apply / submission channel:** Apply directly to the **social security
  field unit** for your place of residence — by phone, fax, e-mail, post, online
  form, or in person; or via referral from another government department / NGO.
  All application procedures are processed in Hong Kong. [3][5] School-related
  special grants are requested through the **same** field unit handling the CSSA
  case (no separate SFO application). [4][6]
- **Review process:** SWD staff conduct investigation and verification through
  office interview and home visit, and may seek data from the Immigration
  Department, banks, Hospital Authority, WFSFAA, Education Bureau and others via
  data matching. Under normal circumstances the process is completed **within
  four weeks** if all necessary information is produced. [3][5]

# Approval Flow

- **Approval workflow:** After investigation, a formal **notification letter** is
  sent. If successful, it sets out a breakdown of the monthly payment components,
  pay-day, payment method and period of payment. If unsuccessful, the reason(s)
  for rejection are given. [3]
- **Additional-information requests:** SWD may ask the applicant/family to produce
  further documents and relies on the applicant's cooperation; it may make
  enquiries to the Department of Health / Hospital Authority (medical reports),
  employers, and other departments/organizations. [3]
- **Special-grant claims:** For special grants (incl. school grants), applicants
  should seek the field-unit staff's advice **before** incurring the expense; all
  **reimbursement claims must be submitted within six months** immediately
  following the date of payment, with all relevant receipts. Advance payment can
  be made on production of a quotation / official estimate. [3]
- **Appeal:** An applicant who disagrees with a decision (refusal of payment,
  amount offered, date from which payment was offered, or payment to a person
  other than the applicant) may appeal to the independent **Social Security Appeal
  Board** within **four weeks** of the notification date. The Board's decision is
  final. [3]

# Payment

CSSA (including the standard rate, supplements and special grants that form the
household's recognized needs) is usually credited to the
applicant's/guardian's/appointee's designated Hong Kong bank account (joint
accounts not accepted) **once a month**. Under exceptional circumstances special
cash delivery can be arranged; for urgent cases cash may be paid at the field
unit. [3][5]

For the **flat-rate grant for selected items of school-related expenses**,
payment is normally made **before the school year begins** (adjusted if
eligibility starts later); additional payment can be considered if actual
expenses exceed the grant (keep receipts). [3] Where the household pays PRH/LPH
rent, the CSSA rent allowance is normally transferred directly to the Housing
Authority. [3][5]

# Renewal

CSSA is an ongoing monthly payment subject to continuous eligibility rather than a
year-by-year re-application. Recipients must **report any change in
circumstances** (see Exceptions), including changes in **schooling conditions**
(commencement of schooling, change of school, cease schooling) and **school-fee /
school-fare** expenditure, which may affect entitlement. The annual flat-rate
school grant recurs each school year for eligible students while on CSSA. [3]

⚠️ **Needs Manual Review:** Confirm whether the flat-rate school grant is paid
automatically each school year or requires an annual declaration to the field
unit. [3]

# Exceptions

- **Mutual exclusivity with SFO student grants (no double-claim):** Families in
  receipt of CSSA **should apply direct to SWD** for school-related assistance for
  their children's examination fees, textbook and stationery expenses, transport
  fares, home Internet access charges, etc., and **do not need to submit an
  application for student financial assistance to the Student Finance Office (SFO)
  separately**. In other words, CSSA households do not double-claim the equivalent
  SFO/WFSFAA student grants. [6]
  - ⚠️ **Needs Manual Review:** Confirm the exact scope of which SFO schemes are
    replaced by CSSA school grants (e.g. School Textbook Assistance, Student Travel
    Subsidy, Internet Access charges, KCFRS / Grant-KG) and the precise
    "no double-benefit" wording on the WFSFAA scheme pages. [6]
- **Portable CSSA (PCSSA):** Elderly recipients retiring to Guangdong/Fujian
  receive only the standard rate and long-term supplement — **no special grants**
  (so school grants do not apply to PCSSA). [3][5]
- **Overpayment recovery / cancellation:** Providing false statements or failing
  to report changes to obtain assistance by deception is an offence under the
  **Theft Ordinance (Cap. 210)** (up to 14 years' imprisonment); any overpaid
  assistance must be refunded. Non-compliance with the Support for Self-reliance
  Job Seeker's Undertaking can lead to cessation/termination of payment and
  recovery of overpaid amounts. [3]
- **Absence from Hong Kong:** Temporary absence beyond the permissible limit (60
  days/year for most recipients, extendable to 90 at discretion; 180 days/year for
  old or disabled recipients) may affect assistance. [3][5]

# FAQ

**Q:** 攞緊綜援，仔女讀書要用嘅錢（學費、車費、書簿）點申請？
**A:** 直接向處理你綜援個案嘅**社會保障辦事處（social security field unit）**申請，
唔使再去學生資助處（SFO）另外申請。學費、上學車費、午膳、公開試考試費等屬綜援
「特別津貼」；書簿、文具、校服等就有一筆過嘅「選定就學開支項目津貼」。[3][4][6]

**Q:** 綜援家庭仲可唔可以再攞學生資助處（SFO）嘅書簿津貼、車船津貼？
**A:** 一般唔可以重複攞——綜援家庭嘅就學相關開支由社署直接資助，所以毋須另外向SFO
申請學生資助。[6]

**Q:** 特別津貼幾時要交單申請？
**A:** 所有實報實銷（reimbursement）嘅特別津貼申請，必須喺付款日期後**六個月內**提交，
並附上所有相關單據。買嘢或安排服務前，最好先問社會保障辦事處職員。[3]

**Q:** 「選定就學開支項目津貼」係咪每年都有？
**A:** 係，全日制學生由幼兒中心至高中程度，每學年可獲一筆過的定額津貼，用於書簿、文具、
校服及其他雜項/一次性開支；通常喺開學前發放。[3]

# Important Definitions

- **Special grant（特別津貼）** — one-off / monthly / yearly payments to meet
  specific needs on top of the standard rate. School grants are a category of
  special grant. [3][4]
- **School grants（學校費用津貼 / 就學相關開支）** — CSSA sub-category covering:
  school fees, meal allowance, fares to/from school, HKDSE examination fee, JUPAS
  application fee, and the flat-rate grant for selected items of school-related
  expenses. [3]
- **Grant for selected items of school-related expenses（選定就學開支項目津貼）**
  — flat-rate grant for books, stationery, school uniforms, miscellaneous and
  minor one-off expenses, for full-time students up to upper-secondary level. [3]
- **Child (for standard-rate purposes)** — aged below 15, or aged 15–21 in
  full-time education (post-secondary students are normally not eligible under the
  Scheme). [3]
- **Recognized needs / Assessable income / household** — see `glossary.md`. [3]

# Important Dates

- **Special-grant reimbursement claims:** within **six months** of the date of
  payment. [3]
- **Flat-rate school grant:** normally paid **before the commencement of the
  school year**. [3]
- **Appeal to Social Security Appeal Board:** within **four weeks** of the
  notification date. [3]
- **Processing time:** normally within **four weeks** of receiving all necessary
  information. [3][5]
- ⚠️ **Needs Manual Review:** current-year (2025/26 / 2026/27) school-grant
  amounts and CSSA rate-adjustment effective date. [3][5]

# Examples

*Illustrative only — based on the worked examples in the October 2023 Guide;
figures are 2023/24 and not a guarantee of entitlement.* [3]

- **Single-parent family (mother + two secondary students aged 14 & 12, no
  income):** monthly CSSA includes standard rates, single-parent supplement, and
  special grants for rent, water & sewage, telephone-related grant, **meal
  allowance for the two students ($340 × 2 = $680)** and **travelling expenses to
  and from school ($450)**; total assistance ≈ $13,608/month. The children are
  also eligible for the **annual flat-rate grant for selected items of
  school-related expenses**. [3]
- **Flat-rate school-expenses grant (2023/24 school year, per student per school
  year):** Child Care Centre $1,910; Kindergarten (Nursery/Lower/Upper) $4,370;
  Primary (P1–6) $5,905; Lower secondary (S1–3) $7,840; Upper secondary
  (SS1–3/technical/commercial) $6,945. [3] ⚠️ Confirm current-year amounts.
- **School fee grant:** actual fees, except kindergarten fees capped at the
  Kindergarten Education Scheme maximum — half-day **$11,280** / full-day
  **$29,330** per school year (2023/24 Guide). [3] ⚠️ Confirm current amounts.
- **Meal allowance:** **$340/month** for a student in full-day education who must
  take lunch away from home (2023/24 Guide). [3] ⚠️ Confirm current amount.

# Edge Cases

- **Students aged 15–21 vs post-secondary:** a "child" for standard-rate purposes
  is under 15, or 15–21 in full-time education; **post-secondary students are
  normally not eligible under the Scheme.** The flat-rate school-expenses grant
  covers full-time students **up to upper-secondary level** only. Tertiary
  students are generally directed to WFSFAA/SFO tertiary schemes instead. [3]
  ⚠️ **Needs Manual Review:** confirm exactly how a household member who enters
  post-secondary education is treated for CSSA (standard rate and grants). [3]
- **New arrivals / under-18:** HK residents aged below 18 are exempted from the
  one-year status and one-year residence requirements, so children in a household
  can be covered even if newly arrived. [2][3]
- **Split / single-parent families:** single-parent supplement and extended asset
  grace period for owner-occupied property (youngest child below 15) may apply. [3]
- **HKDSE / JUPAS costs:** covered by specific school grants (actual fee) rather
  than the flat-rate grant. [3]
- **Home Internet access charges:** listed (via 1823/WFSFAA) among school-related
  assistance CSSA families claim from SWD. ⚠️ **Needs Manual Review:** the October
  2023 Guide's School grants list does not itemise "home Internet access charge"
  separately — confirm the exact CSSA grant item / rate for home Internet access.
  [3][6]
