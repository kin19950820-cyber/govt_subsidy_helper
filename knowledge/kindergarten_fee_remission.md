# Overview

幼稚園及幼兒中心學費減免計劃（KCFRS）由學生資助處（SFO）管理，用「學費減免」的方式，
幫助有需要的清貧家庭減輕子女接受學前教育的學費負擔。合資格家庭可獲**全額（100%）、
四分三（75%）或半額（50%）**的學費減免。 [1][2][4]

同一份「學生資助計劃住戶申請表」同時申請 KCFRS 及「幼稚園學生就學開支津貼」
（Grant-KG，幼稚園學生就學開支津貼）。KCFRS 減免學費／幼兒中心費用；Grant-KG 則是
一筆現金津貼，用來支付幼稚園學生的就學相關開支。幼稚園學生若合資格，可同時獲得兩項資助；
入讀**全日制幼兒中心**的兒童只獲學費減免，不獲 Grant-KG。 [1][2][6]

> Structural facts and current-cycle (2026/27) figures below are drawn from the
> official SFO KCFRS pages. Items still marked **`⚠️ Needs Manual Review`** are:

# Official Purpose

**KCFRS:** "To provide needy families with financial assistance in the form of
fee remission to alleviate their financial burden of providing pre-primary
education for their children." [2]

**Grant-KG (linked scheme):** "To provide a grant to needy families to defray
school-related expenses incurred from the students' kindergarten education." [2]

# Eligibility

**Objective:** Reduce the pre-primary education fee burden of needy families via
fee remission. [2]

**Who can apply (applicant):** The **parent or guardian** (as recognised under
the Guardianship of Minors Ordinance, Cap. 13) of the student-applicant(s).
Where the applicant is not the parent/guardian, they must explain in detail with
proof why this cannot be complied with; if the reason is acceptable SFO will
continue to process the application. [3]

**Who can apply (student-applicant):** A student-applicant must:
- be an **unmarried Hong Kong Resident**;
- have the **right of abode, right to land, or valid permission to remain
  without any condition of stay** (other than the limit of stay) in Hong Kong,
  with a copy of the supporting document; and
- attend an eligible pre-primary institution:
  - **KCFRS / Grant-KG (kindergarten):** a **non-profit-making kindergarten**
    offering local curriculum under the Education Bureau's Kindergarten
    Education Scheme (KG scheme); for the **2026/27** school year the student
    must be **at least 2 years and 8 months old as at 31 August 2026 (i.e. born
    on or before 31 December 2023)** and attending nursery (K1), lower (K2) or
    upper (K3) class. [2][3]
  - **KCFRS (child care centre):** groups **aged 0-2 and 2-3** receiving
    **whole-day** care services in a child care centre (day crèche, day nursery
    or kindergarten-cum-child care centre) **registered with the Social Welfare
    Department (SWD)**. Grant-KG does not apply to child care centre students. [2][3]

**Who cannot apply:**
- Students holding **visitor visas, two-way exit permits, student visas only, or
  who are dependents of student-visa / visitor-visa holders** are not eligible. [3]
- Children **born on or after 1 January 2024** are not eligible for KCFRS
  kindergarten classes in 2026/27 even if they turn 3 during the year; parents
  should apply in 2027/28 instead. [2]
- Children receiving **half-day** child care services are **not eligible** for
  KCFRS. [4]
- Families **currently receiving CSSA** should generally apply to the **SWD**
  (Social Security Field Unit) for school-related special grants under the CSSA
  Scheme instead of applying to SFO — see Exceptions. [3][7]

**Eligibility criteria:** Unmarried HK Resident student with the required right
to remain + enrolled in an eligible KG / child care centre + household passes the
SFO **means test** (and, for whole-day child care ages 0-3, also the "social
needs" test). [3][4]

**Household definition:** "The members of a family normally refer to the
applicant, his/her spouse, unmarried child/children residing with the family and
the dependent parent(s) who are supported by the applicant and/or his/her
spouse." Brothers, sisters and other relatives are **not** counted. Ex-spouse
(divorced/separated) is **not** counted. Married children residing with the
family are **not** counted as members (but any contribution they make is counted
as family income). See `glossary.md#household`. [4][7]

**Dependent parent:** Parent of the applicant or spouse who, at the time of
application, is **not a CSSA recipient**; and throughout the assessment year
(1 April 2025 – 31 March 2026) was **not in employment** and met, for **at least
6 months**, one of: (A) resided with the applicant's family; (B) resided in
premises owned/rented by the applicant or spouse; or (C) resided in an elderly
home with expenses fully paid / totally supported by the applicant or spouse.
Support must continue in 2026/27 in a similar form. Documentary proof is
required. [7]

**Income definition:** "Gross annual income of the family includes the annual
income of the applicant and his/her spouse; **30% of the annual income of
unmarried child/children residing with the family** if applicable; and the
contribution from relatives/friends if applicable." All salary, allowances and
fringe benefits of both spouses, full-time and part-time, monthly or hourly
paid, during the assessment period must be reported. Rent/mortgage repayment is
**not** deductible. Alimony/maintenance received is reported under "Other
income". [4][7]

**Assets definition:** The means test is the **income-based Adjusted Family
Income (AFI)** mechanism; the official Method of Assessment page describes **no
separate asset test** — eligibility and level depend on income and family size,
not on assets. [4]

**Residency requirement:** Student-applicant must be an unmarried **Hong Kong
Resident** with **right of abode, right to land, or valid permission to remain
without any condition of stay** (other than the limit of stay). [3]

# Means Test

All applications are subject to the means test. SFO uses the **Adjusted Family
Income (AFI)** mechanism: [4]

```
AFI = Gross annual income of the family / (Number of family members + 1)
```

- For **single-parent families of 2 to 3 members**, the "plus 1 factor" in the
  divisor is **increased to 2**. Such 2-member single-parent families are
  treated as 3-member families, and 3-member single-parent families as 4-member
  families, for the full-level thresholds and AFI calculation. [4]
- **Medical expense deduction:** For necessary medical expenses of a family
  member who is chronically ill or permanently incapacitated, SFO may deduct
  relevant expenses (with medical certificates and receipts for 1 April 2025 –
  31 March 2026); the deductible ceiling is **$24,250 per family member per year
  in the 2026/27 school year**. [4]

**Social needs test:** To qualify for fee remission for children receiving
**whole-day child care services (groups aged 0-2 and 2-3)**, the applicant must
**also pass the "social needs" test** in the same assessment period, using the
"Social Needs" Assessment Form (SFO 235A). Categories include continuous
employment (normally covering 1 April 2025 – 31 March 2026, e.g. 120/104 hours
criteria), children born out of wedlock not under both parents' care, orphans/
semi-orphans, a parent/guardian in prison or long absent, special conditions of
other family members, large families, and other cases recommended by social
workers. [3][4][7]

**AFI thresholds and level of assistance — 2026/27 school year:** [4]

| 2026/27 AFI between (HK$) | Level of assistance |
| --- | --- |
| 0 – 46,292 | Full (100%) * |
| 46,293 – 56,707 | 3/4 level (75%) |
| 56,708 – 89,515 | Half (50%) |
| > 89,515 | Ineligible |

\* The AFI thresholds for **full** assistance are higher for larger families:
**3-member family $56,044**; **4-member family $51,561** (2026/27). Single-parent
families of 2 / 3 members are treated as 3 / 4-member families respectively. [4]

# Required Documents

**Required:**
- Completed **"Household Application Form for Student Financial Assistance
  Schemes" (SFO 75B(2))** — one per household — or the **Electronic Application
  Form (SFO 75B(E))**. [4][6][8][9]
- Completed **School Certificate (SC)** — student-based; applicant completes
  Parts I–III and submits to the attending school, which certifies Part IV and
  returns it to SFO. [7]
- Copy of the student-applicant's document proving right of abode / right to
  land / permission to remain without condition of stay. [3]
- Income proof for family members for the assessment period (1 April 2025 –
  31 March 2026). [4][7]

**Supporting (if applicable):**
- **"Social Needs" Assessment Form (SFO 235A)** — only for whole-day child care
  fee remission for children aged 0-3. [3][10]
- Documentary proof of support for **dependent parents** (tenancy agreement,
  residential address proof, receipt of the home for the elderly, bank
  transaction records, self-explanatory letter signed by the parent, IRD salaries
  tax / personal assessment notes showing dependent parent allowance, etc.). [4][7]
- Proof of latest income / working hours where circumstances changed; self-
  declaration of income where income proof cannot be produced (SFO reserves the
  right to accept or not). [7]
- For CSSA households with children not covered by CSSA: a copy of the Annex of
  the SWD "Notification of Successful Application" / "Notification of Revision of
  Assistance", if requested. [3]

# Application Flow

- **Application window (2026/27):** SFO distributes application forms and School
  Certificates through kindergartens / child care centres (and District Offices)
  from around **early July 2026**. New applicants should return the completed
  application form to SFO online or by post **as soon as possible**, and submit
  the completed SC to the attending school **by 31 August 2026** (or as soon as
  possible). **Fresh applications must be made every year.** [6][7]
- **How to apply / submission channel:**
  - **Online E-Form (E-Submission)** via the WFSFAA electronic application system
    (can save and retrieve an unfinished form), **or** a paper-based Household
    Application Form returned to SFO by post (Tsimshatsui Post Office Box No.
    96824). [6]
  - Whether paper or electronic, the paper-based **SC** must be submitted to the
    school, which certifies and returns it to SFO. [6][7]
  - **One combined application per household** covers KCFRS and Grant-KG (single
    "wish"), and also covers primary/secondary schemes on the same household
    form — no separate application for younger kindergarten children if the
    household form was already submitted for older primary/secondary children in
    2026/27. [1][6][7]
- **Review process:** SFO conducts the means test (AFI) and, for whole-day child
  care ages 0-3, the social needs test, within the same assessment period. During
  vetting, SFO may request supplementary documents; where a sworn declaration is
  needed, SFO provides a declaration service by appointment at 5/F, Trade and
  Industry Tower, 3 Concorde Road, Kai Tak, Kowloon or 12/F, One Mong Kok Road
  Commercial Centre, 1 Mong Kok Road, Kowloon. [4][6]

# Approval Flow

- **Approval workflow:** SFO assesses AFI (and social needs where applicable),
  determines the level of assistance (full / 3/4 / half), and, upon receipt of
  the school-verified SC, arranges disbursement. [4][6][7]
- **Additional-information requests:** Applicants may be notified during vetting
  to provide supplementary documents; SFO may project annual income pro-rata if
  employment details are incomplete. [6][7]
- **Appeal:** ⚠️ **Needs Manual Review** — no appeal / review-of-decision
  mechanism is stated on the KCFRS overview, eligibility, assessment, procedures
  or FAQ pages. Confirm the appeal channel with SFO / the general SFO terms. [3][4][6][7]

# Payment

- **KCFRS fee remission** is disbursed **through the attending schools** (the
  school reduces the fee / passes on the remission) after SFO receives the
  school-verified SC. [7]
- **Grant-KG** is disbursed by **autopay** to the applicant (only for eligible
  applicants with children studying in kindergartens). [7]
- **Effective month:** Fee remission commences from the month the Household
  Application Form is submitted **or** the month the student is admitted to the
  kindergarten / child care centre, **whichever is the later**. If a student
  already received fee remission for a given month from a previous school, the new
  school will not provide remission for the same month. [6][7]
- Each eligible kindergarten student can receive **Grant-KG only once** in the
  same school year (no double disbursement on transfer). [5]

# Renewal

- **Fresh applications must be made every year.** [6]
- **Continuing applicants** — those who successfully applied for pre-primary
  financial assistance in 2025/26 **on or before 1 May 2026** — follow the
  "Application Processing Timetable – Continuing Applicants" and generally receive
  pre-filled forms; households should not submit the form again unless advised by
  SFO. [6][7]
- **Other / new applicants** — including new applicants and those who had NOT
  received 2025/26 pre-primary assistance on or before 1 May 2026 — follow the
  "Application Processing Timetable – Other Applicants". [6]
- Applicants with a child entering **Primary 1** in 2026/27, or who successfully
  applied for **primary/secondary** assistance in 2025/26, follow the
  primary/secondary continuing-applicant timetable instead. [6]
- **Cancellation / change of circumstances:** ⚠️ Needs Manual Review — confirm
  overpayment recovery and cancellation rules; the KCFRS pages state disbursement
  and effective-month rules but not a formal cancellation procedure.

# Exceptions

- **CSSA households:** Families currently receiving CSSA with children in an
  eligible KG (or with social needs for whole-day child care) should apply to the
  **SWD Social Security Field Unit** for **school-related special grants under the
  CSSA Scheme** and are **not required** to apply to SFO. If a CSSA family has
  children **not covered by CSSA**, they may apply to SFO (and may be asked for
  proof the student-applicant is not a CSSA recipient). [3][7]
- Families with members receiving **Old Age Living Allowance, Old Age Allowance
  or Disability Allowance** can still submit the Household Application Form to SFO. [3]
- **Half-day child care** service recipients cannot apply for KCFRS. [4]
- **Kindergarten withdrawing from the KG scheme:** Eligible students admitted
  before 2026/27 and remaining in K2/K3 in 2026/27 and K3 in 2027/28 continue to
  be eligible until all eligible classes cease operating or the student leaves
  (whichever is earlier). [2]
- **Overpayment recovery / cancellation:** ⚠️ Needs Manual Review.

# FAQ

**Q:** 我已經幫讀緊小學／中學嘅仔女申請咗資助，仲要唔要幫讀緊幼稚園嘅細仔女再申請？
**A:** 唔使另外申請。用同一份「學生資助計劃住戶申請表」，只需在表上表明想為該名子女申請
KCFRS 及 Grant-KG 即可。 [7]

**Q:** 我一家攞緊綜援，仲需唔需要向學生資助處申請 KCFRS／Grant-KG？
**A:** 一般唔需要。攞緊綜援而子女入讀合資格幼稚園（或有社會需要入讀全日制幼兒中心）的
家庭，應向社會福利署社會保障辦事處申請綜援下的與學校有關的特別津貼，唔使再向學生資助處
申請。 [7]

**Q:** 兄弟姊妹或其他親戚算唔算家庭成員？
**A:** 唔算。只有申請人、配偶、與家庭同住的未婚子女，以及申請人或配偶供養的受供養父母
先算家庭成員。 [7]

**Q:** 全日制幼兒中心（0-3 歲）嘅減免有咩額外要求？
**A:** 除咗要通過入息審查（AFI），仲要喺同一評估期內通過「社會需要」評估（SFO 235A）。
半日制幼兒服務唔可以申請 KCFRS。 [3][4]

**Q:** 減免由邊個月開始計？
**A:** 以申請人交表嗰個月，或學生入學嗰個月，兩者中較遲者為準。 [6][7]

# Important Definitions

- **AFI (Adjusted Family Income / 經調整家庭收入):** Gross annual family income ÷
  (number of family members + 1); the divisor "+1" becomes "+2" for 2-3-member
  single-parent families. [4]
- **Level of assistance:** Full (100%), 3/4 (75%), Half (50%). [4]
- **Social needs test:** Additional test for whole-day child care (ages 0-3),
  using SFO 235A. [4]
- **School Certificate (SC):** Student-based certificate; applicant completes
  Parts I–III, the school certifies Part IV. [7]
- **Grant-KG:** Grant for School-related Expenses for Kindergarten Students /
  幼稚園學生就學開支津貼 — a cash grant paid alongside KCFRS for KG students. [2][5]
- See also `glossary.md` for household, means test, assessment year.

# Important Dates

- **Assessment year (income / social needs reference period):** 1 April 2025 –
  31 March 2026. [4][7]
- **Forms distributed:** from around **early July 2026** (2026/27 cycle). [6][7]
- **School Certificate submission deadline:** **31 August 2026** (or as soon as
  possible). [6][7]
- **Continuing-applicant reference date:** successfully applied for 2025/26
  pre-primary assistance **on or before 1 May 2026**. [6]
- **2026/27 fee remission ceilings / meal allowance:** to be promulgated around
  **mid-/end September 2026** — ⚠️ Needs Manual Review until published. [5]
- Note: SFO KCFRS pages do **not** display an explicit "last updated" date;
  currency is inferred from the 2026/27 content and 2026/27 form references. [1][2][3][4][5][6][7]

# Examples

*Illustrative only — not a guarantee; actual result depends on the means test.*

- **Four-member two-parent family, one K2 child (half-day KG).** If the family's
  AFI is $40,000 (below the $46,292 full-level cut-off), the child may qualify
  for **full (100%)** fee remission — up to the **2025/26 half-day ceiling of
  $11,690 per annum** (latest published; 2026/27 ceiling pending) — plus the
  **2026/27 Grant-KG full rate of $4,650**. [4][5]
- **Family with an AFI of $60,000** falls in the $56,708–$89,515 band, so the
  child would receive **half (50%)** assistance. [4]
- **Family with an AFI of $95,000** exceeds $89,515 and would be **ineligible**. [4]

# Edge Cases

- **New-born after the assessment period:** Report family members present at the
  time of application. [7]
- **Divorced / separated / single-parent:** Ex-spouse income and the ex-spouse
  are not counted; the AFI divisor uses "+2" for 2-3-member single-parent
  families; maintenance received is reported as "Other income". [4][7]
- **Employment secured only after the assessment period:** Applicants may apply
  for whole-day fee remission on latest circumstances with proof of current income
  and working hours; SFO reassesses AFI and social needs on latest circumstances. [7]
- **Cash / irregular income, no fixed employer:** Provide bank passbook income
  records or a signed self-declared statement of income; SFO decides acceptance. [7]
- **Kindergarten leaving the KG scheme:** Existing K2/K3 (and following-year K3)
  students remain eligible until eligible classes cease or the student leaves. [2]
- **Transfer of school mid-year:** No double fee remission for the same month;
  Grant-KG only once per school year. [5][6]
- **Child turning 3 during 2026/27 but born on/after 1 Jan 2024:** Not eligible
  for 2026/27 KG classes; apply in 2027/28. [2]
