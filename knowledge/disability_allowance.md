# Overview

傷殘津貼（Disability Allowance，DA）是社會福利署「公共福利金計劃（社會保障津貼，
SSA）」下的一項**按月津貼**，發給**嚴重殘疾（severely disabled）的香港居民**，用來
幫補因傷殘而引起的特別需要。傷殘津貼分兩級：**普通傷殘津貼（Normal Disability
Allowance，NDA）** 同 **高額傷殘津貼（Higher Disability Allowance，HDA）**。 [1][8]

傷殘津貼**毋須經過入息及資產審查（non-means-tested）**——即係唔會查你有幾多錢或者
身家；但申請人必須經**衞生署署長或醫院管理局行政總裁證明為「嚴重殘疾」**，並符合
居港年期等條件。如果除咗嚴重殘疾之外，仲**需要他人經常照顧（constant attendance）**，
而且冇喺受政府資助的院舍／公立醫院接受照顧，就可以攞金額較高的高額傷殘津貼。 [2][3][8][9]

# Official Purpose

"Old Age Allowance and Disability Allowance are designed to provide a monthly
allowance on a non-means-tested basis to Hong Kong residents who are 70 years of
age or above or who are severely disabled to meet their special needs arising
from old age or disability respectively." [9]

The objective of the SSA Scheme is "to provide a monthly allowance to Hong Kong
residents who are severely disabled or who are 65 years of age or above to meet
special needs arising from disability or old age." [1]

# Eligibility

**Objective:** Provide a non-means-tested monthly allowance to Hong Kong
residents who are **severely disabled** to meet their special needs arising from
disability. [9][1]

**Who can apply:** A Hong Kong resident who satisfies the general SSA conditions
**and** the specific medical criterion for Normal or Higher Disability
Allowance. General conditions (apply to all SSA allowances): [8][9]
- **Residence requirement** — (1) has been a **Hong Kong resident for at least
  seven (7) years**; and (2) has **resided in Hong Kong continuously for at least
  one (1) year immediately before the date of application** (absence up to a
  maximum of **90 days** during that one-year period is treated as residence in
  Hong Kong). **Exception:** Hong Kong residents **aged below 18** applying for
  Disability Allowance are **exempted from both residence requirements (1) and
  (2)**. [8]
- **Continues to reside in Hong Kong** during receipt of payment. [8]
- Is **not** in receipt of any other allowance under the SSA Scheme **or**
  assistance under the Comprehensive Social Security Assistance (CSSA) Scheme. [8]
- Is **not** detained in legal custody or admitted to a penal institution. [8]

**Normal Disability Allowance (NDA)** — in addition to the general conditions the
applicant: [2][8]
- is **certified by the Director of Health or the Chief Executive, Hospital
  Authority to be severely disabled** (see definition in *Important
  Definitions*); **and**
- has a **disabling condition that will persist for at least 6 months**.

**Higher Disability Allowance (HDA)** — in addition to meeting the NDA criteria
the applicant: [3][8]
- is **certified by the Director of Health or the Chief Executive, Hospital
  Authority to be in need of constant attendance from others in his/her daily
  life**; **and**
- is **not receiving care in residential institutions subsidised by the
  government** (including subsidised places in subvented/contract homes and
  residential care homes under various bought place schemes) **or all public
  hospitals and institutions under the Hospital Authority, or boarding in special
  schools under the Education Bureau**.

**Who cannot apply / cannot continue:** A person whose presence in Hong Kong is
unlawful, or who is permitted to stay in Hong Kong for a purpose other than
residence (e.g. imported workers or visitors); a person already receiving another
SSA allowance or CSSA; a person detained in legal custody or in a penal
institution; a person who does not continue to reside in Hong Kong during receipt
of payment. [8]

**Eligibility criteria:** (Residence rule, subject to the under-18 exemption)
**and** the medical certification of "severely disabled" (NDA) **and**, for HDA,
certification of need for constant attendance plus not receiving subsidised
institutional/hospital/boarding care **and** non-concurrency with other SSA/CSSA.
[2][3][8]

**Household definition:** **Not applicable in the OALA sense.** Disability
Allowance is assessed on the **individual applicant** and is **non-means-tested**,
so there is **no household income/asset assessment**. Where the applicant is
aged below 18, a parent/guardian handles the application and provides their own
identity/relationship documents (see *Required Documents*). See
`glossary.md#household`. [6][9]

**Income definition:** **Not applicable** — Disability Allowance does **not**
apply an income test. [1][9]

**Assets definition:** **Not applicable** — Disability Allowance does **not**
apply an asset test. [1][9]

**Residency requirement:** ≥ 7 years a Hong Kong resident **and** continuous
residence ≥ 1 year immediately before application (up to 90 days absence in that
year treated as residence); **applicants aged below 18 are exempted** from both.
The recipient must **continue to reside in Hong Kong** during receipt of payment
(see *Payment* for the permissible absence limits). [8]

# Means Test

**Disability Allowance is NON-means-tested.** "Applicants of Disability Allowance
and Old Age Allowance under the Scheme are not required to go through a means
test." [1] The Guidance Notes confirm the allowance is provided "on a non-means-
tested basis". [9] There is therefore **no income limit, no asset limit, and no
full/half-rate distinction based on means** — eligibility turns on residence and
the medical certification, and the two rates (Normal vs Higher) depend on the
**degree of disability / need for constant attendance**, not on finances. [1][2][3][9]

# Required Documents

Supporting documents for a Disability Allowance application (from the SWD
"Supporting documents" page; requirements differ for applicants aged below 18 vs
18 or above): [6][8]

**Required (applicant aged 18 or above):**
- Any documents confirming the applicant's **identity, age and residence** in
  Hong Kong (e.g. Hong Kong Identity Card, Birth Certificate, travel documents
  with printed copy of e-Visa if applicable).
- The pages showing **personal particulars in all the applicant's travel
  documents** (Passports, HKSAR Document of Identity for Visa Purposes, travel
  documents issued by other countries) **and** the pages showing the **absence
  record during the one-year period immediately before the application**.
- **Document of the applicant's bank account** (e.g. first page of passbook or a
  bank statement) clearly showing the applicant's name and account number —
  **only a sole account is acceptable; a joint account is not accepted** for
  payment of the allowance.
- The applicant's **hospitalisation records or follow-up appointment slips in
  public hospitals/clinics** to facilitate the arrangement of a **medical
  assessment**.

**Required (applicant aged below 18):**
- Documents confirming the applicant's identity, age and residence (as above).
- Pages of the applicant's travel documents showing particulars and absence
  record (as above).
- The applicant's hospitalisation records / follow-up appointment slips to
  facilitate the medical assessment.
- The **identity document of the applicant's parent/guardian and proof of
  relationship** with the applicant (e.g. Birth Certificate).
- Document of the **parent's/guardian's bank account** (sole account only; joint
  account not accepted) for payment purposes.
- *(A bank account of the below-18 applicant is **not** required — payment is
  made to the parent/guardian's account.)*

**Note:** If an applicant is aged below 18 without a parent or legal guardian, or
is an adult aged 18 or above medically certified as mentally unfit to make a
statement (and so unable to apply personally), an **appointee** is appointed by
the Director of Social Welfare to act on his/her behalf. [6][8]

# Application Flow

- **Application window:** **Year-round** (no fixed window). Applications are
  accepted at any time; there is no advance-application arrangement specific to
  Disability Allowance (the "one month before qualifying age" arrangement applies
  to age-based OAA/OALA, not to DA). [5][8]
- **How to apply / submission channel:** The applicant, or a relative/friend, may
  apply directly to the **social security field unit for the applicant's place of
  residence** — **by phone, fax, e-mail, post, online form, or in person**. An
  application may also be made through a **referral** to SWD by another government
  department or a non-governmental organisation. The "Social Security Allowance
  Scheme Application Form" and "Application for Social Security Allowance Guidance
  Notes" can be downloaded from the SWD website (https://www.swd.gov.hk) and
  returned, with photocopies of supporting documents, to the field unit by post
  or in person. [5][8]
- **Review process:** As soon as an application is received, an SWD officer
  arranges an **interview** with the applicant. For Disability Allowance, SWD
  arranges a **medical assessment**; the applicant is certified as "severely
  disabled" (and, for HDA, as needing constant attendance) by the **Director of
  Health or the Chief Executive, Hospital Authority** (or, in exceptional
  circumstances where a private-hospital applicant cannot be brought to a public
  hospital/clinic, by the attending registered medical practitioner of that
  private hospital, subject to other prescribed conditions). After completing the
  investigation, SWD sends a **formal notification letter**. SWD may ask for
  supplementary information. All procedures are processed in Hong Kong. [2][8]

# Approval Flow

- **Approval workflow:** SWD verifies residence and non-concurrency, arranges the
  **medical assessment/certification** of severe disability (and constant
  attendance for HDA), then approves NDA or HDA (or refuses) and issues a formal
  notification. Payment is calculated from the date of receipt of application (or
  referral date) or the date of eligibility, whichever is later. [5][8]
- **Additional information requests:** SWD may request supplementary information
  or documents (identity, travel documents/absence record, bank account,
  hospitalisation/appointment records, and parent/guardian documents for below-18
  applicants). SWD conducts **data matching** with other bodies (Immigration
  Department, Treasury, Correctional Services Department, Land Registry, Companies
  Registry, Hospital Authority, Transport Department, HKMC Annuity Limited, etc.)
  and periodic **reviews** (including home visits or by post) to cross-check
  information. [8][9]
- **Appeal:** An applicant who disagrees with SWD's decision may lodge an appeal
  with the **Social Security Appeal Board** — an independent body of non-officials
  appointed by the Chief Executive. An appeal **must be lodged within four (4)
  weeks** immediately following the date of notification of the Director of Social
  Welfare's decision. Enquiries about appeal procedures can be made with the
  social security field unit concerned. [8][11]

# Payment

- **Amount (Normal Disability Allowance):** **$2,140 per month** (with effect from
  **1 February 2026**). [10]
- **Amount (Higher Disability Allowance):** **$4,280 per month** (with effect from
  **1 February 2026**). [10]
- **Transport Supplement:** A monthly **Transport Supplement of $345** is payable
  to eligible Disability Allowance recipients **aged 12 to 64**, to encourage them
  to participate more in activities away from home. It is paid at a flat rate
  **together with** the allowance. [10][4][8]
  - *Note:* The SWD "Amount Payable" web page [4] and the SSA pamphlet [8] state
    the allowance is paid "at a flat rate" and describe the Transport Supplement
    but **do not publish the dollar figures**; the current figures above are
    quoted from the official GovHK/1823 Disability Allowance page [10] (revised
    18 Mar 2026, stating "effective from 1 February 2026").
- **Payment method:** Allowance is usually **credited monthly** to the
  applicant's / guardian's / appointee's **designated sole bank account in Hong
  Kong** (a **joint bank account is not accepted**). In exceptional circumstances,
  special delivery of cash directly to the applicant can be arranged. [8][6]
- **Effective date of payment:** Payment is calculated from the **date of receipt
  of the application** by SWD (or the date of application/referral if referred by
  another organisation) **or the date of eligibility, whichever is later**. [5][8]
- **Permissible absence during receipt of allowance:** If a recipient has resided
  in Hong Kong for **not less than 60 days in a payment year**, temporary absences
  (leaving Hong Kong for the Mainland, Macao or overseas) do **not** affect payment
  provided total absence in the year does **not exceed 305 days (306 in a leap
  year)** — i.e. a recipient satisfying the 60-day minimum is entitled to a full
  year's allowance. If the recipient resides in Hong Kong for **less than 60 days**
  in a payment year, he/she is entitled to allowance **only for the periods
  residing in Hong Kong**; any overpayment must be refunded. Subject to sufficient
  documentary proof, absences arising from **paid work** may be disregarded, and a
  **Disability Allowance recipient's absences arising from full-time study** may
  also be disregarded. A "payment year" is the 12-month period from the date the
  recipient met the eligibility criteria. [8]

# Renewal

Disability Allowance does **not** require a fixed periodic re-application;
recipients continue to receive the allowance so long as they remain eligible
(including continuing to reside in Hong Kong and, for HDA, not being admitted to
subsidised institutional/hospital/boarding care). SWD conducts periodic
**case reviews** (home visits or by post) and data matching. A medical review of
the disabling condition may be required where the condition is not permanent.
An applicant previously found ineligible may **re-apply** at any time. [8][9]

# Exceptions

- **Cannot be held with other SSA allowances or CSSA.** A person receiving Old
  Age Allowance, Old Age Living Allowance, another SSA allowance, or CSSA is
  **not** eligible for Disability Allowance (and vice versa). [8]
- **Admission to institutional/hospital/boarding care (HDA):** If an HDA applicant
  has, at the time of application, been admitted to the disqualifying residential
  institutions / public hospitals and institutions for care, or to boarding
  service in Education Bureau special schools, he/she is given **only Normal
  Disability Allowance**. An **existing HDA recipient's allowance is adjusted down
  to NDA** if he/she has been so admitted for **more than 29 days**. Recipients
  must report such admissions/discharges immediately. [8]
- **Private-hospital medical assessment:** In exceptional cases where a DA
  applicant staying in a private hospital is considered by the attending
  registered medical practitioner unsuitable to be brought to a public
  hospital/clinic for assessment, SWD can accept the private practitioner's
  medical assessment, subject to other prescribed conditions. [2][8]
- **Detention / leaving Hong Kong:** A person detained in legal custody or in a
  penal institution is not eligible; a recipient must continue to reside in Hong
  Kong (subject to the permissible absence limits above). [8]
- **Overpayment / fraud (cancellation):** Providing false, incorrect or
  incomplete information, or failing to report a change of circumstances that
  reduces or disqualifies the allowance, is a criminal offence; any resulting
  overpayment must be **refunded to SWD**. Recipients must report changes (e.g.
  change of address, imprisonment/detention, admission to or discharge from
  subsidised institutions / public hospitals, boarding or ceasing to board in
  special schools (HDA only), and absences exceeding the permissible limit)
  immediately to the social security field unit. [8][9]

# FAQ

**Q:** 傷殘津貼使唔使查我有幾多錢或者資產（入息／資產審查）？
**A:** 唔使。傷殘津貼**毋須經過入息及資產審查**（non-means-tested）。 [1][9]

**Q:** 普通同高額傷殘津貼有咩分別？
**A:** 兩者都要被證明為「嚴重殘疾」。**高額**傷殘津貼要**額外**被證明**日常生活需要
他人經常照顧**，而且**唔喺**政府資助的院舍、公立醫院或（教育局）特殊學校寄宿接受
照顧。 [2][3][8]

**Q:** 每月有幾多錢？
**A:** 普通傷殘津貼每月 **$2,140**、高額傷殘津貼每月 **$4,280**（**2026年2月1日**
起生效）。12至64歲合資格人士另有每月 **$345** 交通津貼（Transport Supplement）。 [10]

**Q:** 未滿18歲都要住夠香港7年先申請到？
**A:** 唔使。**未滿18歲**申請傷殘津貼的香港居民，**豁免**七年居港及申請前一年連續
居港的規定。 [8]

**Q:** 攞緊傷殘津貼可唔可以同時攞綜援或長者生活津貼？
**A:** 唔可以。攞傷殘津貼期間**唔可以**同時領取綜援或計劃下其他津貼。 [8]

# Important Definitions

- **"Severely disabled"** — A person is considered severely disabled within the
  meaning of the Scheme if he/she is certified by the **Director of Health or the
  Chief Executive of the Hospital Authority** (or, in exceptional circumstances,
  by a registered medical practitioner of a private hospital) as falling into one
  of the following categories: **(a) Disabling Physical or Mental Condition** —
  (1) loss of functions of two limbs; (2) loss of functions of both hands or of
  all fingers and both thumbs; (3) loss of functions of both feet; (4) total loss
  of sight; (5) total paralysis (quadriplegia); (6) paraplegia; (7) illness,
  injury or deformity resulting in being bedridden; (8) any other condition
  (including visceral diseases) producing a degree of disablement broadly
  equivalent to (1)–(7), i.e. the person needs substantial help from others to
  cope with daily life; **or (b) Profoundly Deaf** — perceptive or mixed deafness
  with a hearing loss of **85 decibels or more** in the better ear for pure-tone
  frequencies of 500, 1 000 and 2 000 cycles per second, or 75–85 decibels with
  other physical handicaps including lack of speech and distortion of hearing.
  The severe disability must produce a significant restriction or lack of ability
  to perform (with substantial help from others) at least one daily-living
  activity: working in one's original/suited occupation (not for persons aged
  below 15); self-care and personal hygiene; posture/balance and transfers/travel;
  or expressing oneself, communicating, cognition and emotional/social control.
  [8][9]
- **Constant attendance (HDA)** — certification that the applicant **needs
  constant attendance from others in his/her daily life**; required in addition to
  the "severely disabled" certification for Higher Disability Allowance. [3][8]
- **Non-means-tested** — no income or asset test is applied to Disability
  Allowance. See `glossary.md#means-test`. [1][9]
- **Transport Supplement** — a flat-rate monthly supplement ($345) added to the
  allowance for DA recipients aged 12–64. [10][8]
- OAA, OALA, CSSA — see `glossary.md`.

# Important Dates

- **Allowance rates effective:** **1 February 2026** — Normal Disability Allowance
  **$2,140/month**; Higher Disability Allowance **$4,280/month**; Transport
  Supplement **$345/month** (ages 12–64). [10]
- **Disabling condition duration (NDA):** must persist for **at least 6 months**.
  [2][8]
- **HDA downgrade on institutional admission:** existing HDA recipient's allowance
  adjusted to NDA if admitted for care for **more than 29 days**. [8]
- **Residence:** ≥ 7 years resident; ≥ 1 year continuous immediately before
  application (≤ 90 days absence disregarded); **under-18 exempted**. [8]
- **Absence during receipt:** ≥ 60 days in Hong Kong per payment year → full-year
  allowance if total absence ≤ 305 days (306 in a leap year). [8]
- **Appeal deadline:** within **4 weeks** of notification of SWD's decision. [8][11]

# Examples

*Illustrative only — not a guarantee of eligibility.*

- **Likely qualifying (NDA):** A 40-year-old Hong Kong permanent resident (resident
  > 7 years, did not leave HK in the past year) who is certified by the Hospital
  Authority as severely disabled (paraplegia) with the condition expected to
  persist over 6 months, not on CSSA or another SSA allowance — meets residence and
  the "severely disabled" certification, so is eligible for Normal Disability
  Allowance ($2,140/month) plus the $345 Transport Supplement (aged 12–64). [2][8][10]
- **Likely qualifying (HDA):** A 55-year-old resident certified as severely
  disabled **and** as needing constant attendance from others, living at home (not
  in a subsidised care home or public hospital) — is eligible for Higher Disability
  Allowance ($4,280/month) plus the $345 Transport Supplement. [3][8][10]
- **Under-18 example:** A 10-year-old Hong Kong resident child certified as severely
  disabled qualifies for Disability Allowance even without 7 years' residence,
  because under-18 applicants are exempt from the residence requirements; payment is
  made to the parent/guardian's sole bank account. [6][8]
- **Likely not qualifying:** A visitor or imported worker (permitted to stay for a
  purpose other than residence), or a person already receiving CSSA, is not
  eligible. [8]

# Edge Cases

- **New arrivals / short residence (adults):** An adult who has not been a HK
  resident for ≥ 7 years, or not resided continuously for the year before
  application (> 90 days absent, absent for reasons other than the disregarded
  full-time study / paid work / medical-treatment discretion), does not meet the
  residence rule. Under-18 applicants are exempt. [8]
- **Medical treatment abroad > 90 days:** Where an applicant was absent from HK for
  more than 90 days during the pre-application year to receive **medical treatment
  outside Hong Kong**, the Director of Social Welfare may exercise discretion to
  disregard the excess absence, subject to established reasons and evidence. [8]
- **Full-time study abroad:** Absences from Hong Kong arising from full-time study
  may be disregarded for **Disability Allowance** applicants/recipients (with
  sufficient documentary proof) — a relaxation specific to DA. [8]
- **HDA recipient admitted to institution/hospital:** If admitted to subsidised
  residential care, a public hospital/institution, or Education Bureau special-
  school boarding for **more than 29 days**, HDA is downgraded to NDA; recipients
  must report the admission. [8]
- **Private-hospital patient unable to attend public assessment:** SWD may accept
  the private hospital practitioner's medical assessment in exceptional cases. [2][8]
- **Applicant unable to apply personally:** An appointee is appointed by the
  Director of Social Welfare for a below-18 applicant without parent/legal guardian,
  or for an adult medically certified as mentally unfit to make a statement. [6][8]
