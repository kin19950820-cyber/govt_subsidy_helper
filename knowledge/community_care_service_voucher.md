# Overview

「長者社區照顧服務券計劃」（英文簡稱 **CCSV**，下稱「社區券」）是社會福利署（社署）
一項**資助長者購買社區照顧服務**的計劃。它用「**錢跟人走**」的方式，直接資助合資格的
長者，讓長者可以自己揀選、更換認可的服務單位（Recognised Service Provider，RSP），
按自己不同時候的需要調整服務項目和數量。 [1][3][4]

簡單講：這**不是**一項「政府直接派現金」的津貼。長者要先經社署的「**安老服務統一評估
機制**」評估，確認有長期護理（LTC）需要並在「中央輪候冊」上輪候資助社區照顧或住宿照顧
服務。社署會**分批寄邀請信**給合資格長者。長者收到邀請後填表（或網上申請），獲批後就會
收到一張電子社區券。長者每月用這張券去認可服務單位「買」日間照顧或家居照顧服務，政府
補貼大部分費用，長者只需按自己家庭收入付一個**共同付款**（co-payment）比例。 [1][2][4][5]

The CCSV subsidises eligible frail elderly persons — who have been assessed as
needing long term care and are on the Central Waiting List — to purchase
community care services (day care and/or home care) from Recognised Service
Providers. It follows a "money-following-the-user" model plus an "affordable
users pay" co-payment: the less the elderly person and co-living family can
afford, the more the Government pays. The Scheme was regularised (made
permanent) on 1 September 2023. [1][4][10]

# Official Purpose

Official wording (leaflet, April 2025): the Scheme's characteristics are
"**Money-following-the-user**: Elderly persons can choose the community care
services that suit their individual needs with the use of community care service
vouchers" and "**Affordable users pay**: The less that elderly persons and the
family members living with them can afford, the more the Government will
subsidise." [4]

Press release (1 Sep 2023): the Scheme "adopts a 'money-follows-the-user'
approach, allowing eligible elderly persons to choose suitable community care
services that suit their individual needs." [10]

中文原文（服務單張）：「錢跟人走：長者可運用社區照顧服務券，選擇切合個人需要的社區
照顧服務」；「能者多付：長者及與其同住的家人越是負擔不起，政府資助越多。」 [4]

# Eligibility

**Objective:** Enable frail elderly persons who need long term care and are
waitlisting for subsidised services to receive community care (day care / home
care) in their familiar home and community, and to maintain their maximum level
of functioning while on the waiting list. [4][8]

**Who can apply:** Elderly persons who satisfy **all** of the following (verbatim
from the leaflet and main page):
"Elderly persons who have been **assessed and recommended for Community Care
Services (CCS) or Residential Care Services (RCS) under the Standardised Care
Need Assessment Mechanism for Elderly Services**, and are **waitlisting for
subsidised CCS and/or RCS on the Central Waiting List (CWL) for subsidised Long
Term Care (LTC) services** **without any kind of RCS or subsidised CCS being
received**." [1][4]

So the three conditions are:
1. Assessed and recommended for CCS or RCS under SCNAMES; **and**
2. Currently on the Central Waiting List for subsidised CCS and/or RCS; **and**
3. Not currently receiving any RCS or any subsidised CCS. [1][4][5]

**Who cannot apply:** Elderly persons who are **already receiving** residential
care service or any **subsidised** community care service. The FAQ lists
subsidised CCS that make a person ineligible: Integrated Home Care Services
(Ordinary Cases and Frail Cases), Home Care Service for Persons with Severe
Disabilities, Integrated Support Service for Persons with Severe Physical
Disabilities, Integrated Discharge Support Programme for Elderly Patients, Pilot
Scheme on Support for Elderly Persons Discharged from Public Hospitals after
Treatment, etc. Users of these services are ineligible for voucher services. [5]

**Eligibility criteria (age):** ⚠️ **Needs Manual Review.** The CCSV scheme page,
leaflet, FAQ and application form define eligibility **solely** by the SCNAMES
assessment result and CWL waitlisting status; **none of the CCSV official
documents states a standalone minimum age** (e.g. "65 or above"). Related
subsidised community/home-based care services on SWD's site are described as for
persons "aged 60 or above," but this is **not** stated as a CCSV criterion.
The exact minimum age tied to the SCNAMES/CWL entry point should be confirmed.
[1][4][5] · check [8][9] and the home-based CCS page [13].

**Household definition:** For the co-payment financial assessment, "household
members" means the elderly person's **father, mother, son(s), daughter(s), and
husband/wife residing with him/her** in Hong Kong (legally recognised adoptive
parents/children, or illegitimate children with proof of parentage, are also
included). Incomes of siblings, daughter-in-law, son-in-law, grandchildren and
domestic helpers residing with the elderly person are **not** counted and need
not be reported. [5][6]

**Income definition:** "Average monthly income for the 3 months prior to the
month of application submission" of the elderly person and specified co-living
household members. Includes employment earnings (salary, double pay/leave pay,
work allowance, bonuses/commission/tips, income from services, business
profits), contributions from children, financial assistance from relatives/
friends, alimony, pensions / widow's & orphans' payments, investment profits,
interest from fixed deposits, dividends, annuity, and rental income. **Excluded**
from income: employees' mandatory MPF contributions, wages in lieu of notice,
employees' compensation, reverse mortgage payments, Government financial
programmes, CSSA, Social Security Allowance (Disability Allowance, Old Age
Allowance, Old Age Living Allowance), Criminal and Law Enforcement Injuries
Compensation, Traffic Accident Victims Assistance, charitable donations, and
assistance under the Community Care Fund. [4][6]

**Assets definition:** **Assets are NOT assessed.** "The family's assets will not
be assessed." Only household income is used to set the co-payment category. [4][5]

**Residency requirement:** Eligibility flows from SCNAMES assessment and the CWL
for subsidised LTC services; the co-payment assessment counts only household
members **residing with the elderly person in Hong Kong**. A specific residency
rule beyond this (e.g. HK ID / years of residence) is not separately stated in
the CCSV documents — ⚠️ **Needs Manual Review** if a hard residency criterion is
required. [5][6]

# Means Test

The Scheme is **partially means-tested for the co-payment amount only** — the
means test does **not** decide whether you are eligible (eligibility is set by
SCNAMES + CWL), it decides **how much you pay**. Under the "affordable users pay"
principle there are **six co-payment categories**, set at **5%, 8%, 12%, 16%,
25% and 40%** of the service package value; the Government pays the rest. [4][5]

The CCSV Office determines the category from the **average monthly income of the
elderly person and specified co-living household members (parents, children,
spouse) over the 3 months before application**, compared against the **Median
Monthly Domestic Household Income (MMDHI)** published by the Census and
Statistics Department, by household size. **Assets are not assessed.** [5][6][7]

Two special rules:
- **CSSA recipients** are automatically placed in the **5% (Category I)**
  co-payment category, so they do not need to apply to a Social Security Field
  Unit to reimburse the co-payment. [5]
- Applicants who **do not wish to disclose** household income may still apply,
  but must accept the **maximum 40% (Category VI)** co-payment. [4][5][6]

**Current co-payment table — 2026-27 price level, effective 1 April 2026.** The
income-band limits below are adjusted annually and were set with reference to the
**Q3 2025 MMDHI**. Bands are expressed as a percentage of the MMDHI for the
household size; dollar figures are the official limits: [7]

Household size **1 person** (bands: ≤75% / >75%–100% / >100%–150% / >150%–175% / >175% of MMDHI):
| Category | II (8%) | III (12%) | IV (16%) | V (25%) | VI (40%) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly household income | ≤ $7,875 | > $7,875 – $10,500 | > $10,500 – $15,750 | > $15,750 – $18,375 | > $18,375 |

Household size **2+ persons** (bands: ≤50% / >50%–75% / >75%–125% / >125%–150% / >150% of MMDHI):
| Household size | II (8%) | III (12%) | IV (16%) | V (25%) | VI (40%) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2 persons | ≤ $10,950 | > $10,950 – $16,425 | > $16,425 – $27,375 | > $27,375 – $32,850 | > $32,850 |
| 3 persons | ≤ $18,150 | > $18,150 – $27,225 | > $27,225 – $45,375 | > $45,375 – $54,450 | > $54,450 |
| 4 persons | ≤ $25,050 | > $25,050 – $37,575 | > $37,575 – $62,625 | > $62,625 – $75,150 | > $75,150 |
| 5 persons | ≤ $33,700 | > $33,700 – $50,550 | > $50,550 – $84,250 | > $84,250 – $101,100 | > $101,100 |

**Category I (5%)** applies to CSSA recipients regardless of household size. [7]
⚠️ **Needs Manual Review:** the official 2026-27 table lists household sizes up to
5 persons; the limit for households **larger than 5 persons** is not shown in the
extracted table and should be confirmed on the current co-payment table PDF. [7]

Co-payment amount = service package value × co-payment %. Example from the FAQ:
a $5,000 package at 8% → co-payment $400 ($5,000 × 8%). [5] The co-payment
amounts corresponding to the floor–ceiling voucher range ($4,526–$10,824) are: [7]
| Category | 5% | 8% | 12% | 16% | 25% | 40% |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Co-payment ($4,526 floor → $10,824 ceiling) | $226 → $541 | $362 → $866 | $543 → $1,299 | $724 → $1,732 | $1,131 → $2,706 | $1,810 → $4,330 |

**Voucher value (2026-27):** the elderly person may choose any package value
between the **floor of $4,526** and the **ceiling of $10,824 per month**
(both inclusive). Services exceeding the ceiling value are not subsidised and the
difference must be paid by the user. [1][7]

# Required Documents

**Required:**
- Completed CCSV Application Form (Form 1), signed by the elderly person or by an
  authorised applicant acting with the elderly person's prescribed consent. [6]
- Personal particulars of the elderly person: Chinese/English name, HK Identity
  Card number, LDS serial number, date of birth, contact details, residential
  address, living condition. [6]
- Household income declaration in Part 4 (unless CSSA recipient, or unless the
  applicant elects to pay the maximum 40% co-payment and not disclose income). [6]

**Supporting (if applicable / on request):**
- Copies of household income proofs for the preceding 3 months — **only** if the
  applicant is **randomly sampled for a household means test**; the CCSV Office
  will write to request them. [5][6]
- If applying on behalf of the elderly person: applicant's particulars and, where
  relevant, proof of being a "relevant person" under the Personal Data (Privacy)
  Ordinance / Mental Health Ordinance for an incapacitated elderly person. [6]

# Application Flow

- **Application window:** There is **no application deadline.** SWD issues
  **invitation letters in batches** to eligible elderly persons according to their
  application dates on the CWL. After receiving an invitation, the elderly person
  "may decide when to submit their applications based on their own preferences and
  service needs" and may keep the form and apply when a service need arises. [4][5]
- **How to apply / submission channel:**
  1. **Online** via the Voucher Information System for the Elderly (VISE):
     https://vise.swd.gov.hk/vise/en/ep/appForm ; or [5][6]
  2. **Fax** to 2117 1264; or [6]
  3. **Post** to the Community Care Service Voucher Office, Rooms 2601-05A, 26/F,
     Two Chinachem Exchange Square, 338 King's Road, North Point, Hong Kong. [6]
  Elderly persons who never received (or lost) an invitation/form may contact
  their responsible worker or the CCSV Office (3107 3013 / ccsv@swd.gov.hk) or
  download the form from the SWD website. [5]
- **Review process:** In general the CCSV Office completes vetting and approval
  and issues the voucher **within two to four weeks** of receiving an application.
  For applicants randomly sampled for a household means test, the Office notifies
  them by letter to submit income proofs and informs them of the result **within
  10 working days of receiving all necessary documents.** [5]

# Approval Flow

- **Approval workflow:** After eligibility is confirmed, the CCSV Office issues a
  **notification of issuance of voucher**, which states the approved co-payment
  category. **Paper voucher certificates are no longer issued** — the voucher is
  electronic; holders log into VISE with their HK Identity Card number to verify
  eligibility and check their voucher amounts. The notification includes a list of
  RSPs serving the elderly person's district. [5]
- **Additional-information requests:** Where sampled for means testing, or where
  SWD conducts a comprehensive review, the elderly person and co-living household
  members must fully co-operate and provide detailed financial and other
  information; otherwise SWD may disqualify the person and/or require refund of
  subsidies paid. [5][6]
- **Appeal (assessment result):** The eligibility assessment is done through
  SCNAMES. For applicants (or service providers) who disagree with the SCNAMES
  **assessment result and services matched**, there are **pre-appeal mediation and
  appeal channels** under SCNAMES, and re-assessment may be arranged. [8]
  A dedicated appeal channel against the CCSV **co-payment category** decision is
  not separately described in the CCSV documents; an elderly person who cannot
  afford the assessed rate may ask the CCSV Office to review and, on a
  **discretionary** basis, lower the rate. ⚠️ **Needs Manual Review** for a formal
  co-payment appeal mechanism. [5]

# Payment

The subsidy is **not paid as cash to the elderly person.** The voucher is an
electronic entitlement: each month the elderly person buys a service package from
up to **any two RSPs in a calendar month**, pays only the co-payment portion to
the RSP, and the Government pays the remaining (subsidised) portion of the package
value (up to the ceiling). Service items above the ceiling value, or extra
non-recognised items (e.g. traditional Chinese medicine, acupuncture,
aromatherapy), are **not** subsidised and are paid by the user. RSPs generally do
**not** refund co-payment already received. [4][5][7]

# Renewal

The voucher is ongoing while the elderly person remains eligible and continues to
use the service; there is no fixed "renewal" application. However:
- **Reassessment of co-payment category:** When there is a change in household
  size or financial situation that invalidates the approved category, the elderly
  person **should apply to the CCSV Office as soon as possible** for a
  reassessment, providing income proofs for the preceding 3 months (Application
  Form for Change of Co-payment Category, available from RSPs or the CCSV
  Office). [5]
- **SCNAMES assessment validity:** SCNAMES assessment results are valid for **12
  months**; re-assessment can be arranged if the elderly person's health condition
  or circumstances change significantly. [8]
- **Service suspension:** An elderly person who **stops using** voucher services
  for **more than one month** should be discharged from the RSP to free the place;
  the RSP may seek CCSV Office permission to reserve a place where justified, but
  no Government subsidy is paid during the reserved period. [5]

# Exceptions

- **Cancellation / withdrawal:** The elderly person may **withdraw from the Scheme
  at any time**; the co-payment already paid for that month is **not refunded**.
  Withdrawal is arranged via the CCSV Office (in person), the responsible worker,
  or an RSP, and the voucher is destroyed afterwards. [5]
- **Overpayment recovery / false information:** SWD may conduct a comprehensive
  review; if data are found false or the person is ineligible, SWD may disqualify
  the person and **request refund** of subsidies, and any verified overpayment
  must be refunded. Knowingly making a false statement or withholding information
  to obtain the subsidy is a **criminal offence** — on conviction, imprisonment
  for a maximum of **14 years** under the Theft Ordinance (Cap. 210). [6]
- **Interaction with the Central Waiting List:** On issuance of the voucher, the
  elderly person's LTC application on the CWL becomes an **"inactive case"**; it
  can be **reactivated** to "active" at any time through the responsible worker,
  and the **original application date (waitlisting position) is preserved** — using
  the voucher does not harm the person's LTC waitlisting position. [5]
- **CSSA recipients:** Placed in the 5% co-payment category; no separate special
  grant reimbursement application is needed. [5]
- **Mixed / two-provider use:** An elderly person can use both day care and home
  care at the same time, and up to **two RSPs per calendar month**; switching to a
  new RSP/package while already using two RSPs in the same month takes effect the
  following month at the earliest. [5]

# FAQ

**Q:** Is there a deadline to apply after receiving the invitation letter?
**A:** No. Elderly persons may decide when to apply based on their own preferences
and service needs, and may keep the form and apply when a need arises. [5]

**Q:** How long does approval take?
**A:** Generally the CCSV Office completes vetting and issues the voucher within
**two to four weeks** of receiving the application. If randomly sampled for a
means test, the result is given within **10 working days** of receiving all
required income proofs. [5]

**Q:** Can I apply without disclosing my household income?
**A:** Yes, but you must accept the **maximum 40% co-payment** (Category VI). [5][6]

**Q:** Are my assets assessed?
**A:** No. Only household income is assessed; the family's assets are not
assessed. [4][5]

**Q:** I can't afford the assessed co-payment rate — what can I do?
**A:** Contact your responsible worker or the CCSV Office. The Office will review
your household's financial situation and consider, on a **discretionary basis**,
whether to lower your co-payment rate. [5]

**Q:** Will using the voucher affect my place on the LTC waiting list?
**A:** No. Your CWL application becomes an "inactive case" but can be reactivated
anytime, keeping your original application date and waitlisting position. [5]

**Q:** How many RSPs can I use, and what if I stop using the service?
**A:** Up to two RSPs per calendar month. If you stop using voucher services for
more than one month, you should be discharged to free the place (the RSP may seek
permission to reserve it, but with no subsidy during that period). [5]

# Important Definitions

- **CCS (Community Care Services):** day care and home care services (e.g.
  rehabilitation exercise, nursing care, personal care, home making, escort, meals
  delivery, elder sitting, carer training, home modification). [4][8]
- **RCS (Residential Care Services):** care-and-attention homes and nursing homes
  under SWD. [8]
- **SCNAMES (Standardised Care Need Assessment Mechanism for Elderly Services):**
  single-entry assessment; **accredited assessors** (social workers, nurses,
  occupational therapists, physiotherapists) use the internationally recognised
  **interRAI-Home Care version 9.3 (interRAI-HC 9.3)** tool — updated in July 2021
  from the earlier Minimum Data Set–Home Care 2.0 (MDS-HC 2.0) — to assess care
  needs and match services. Assessment is **free** and results are valid **12
  months**. [8]  *Note: the task brief refers to this as "EACS"; the current
  official name/tool is SCNAMES / interRAI-HC 9.3.* [8]
- **CWL (Central Waiting List for Subsidised Long Term Care Services):** the single
  waiting list for subsidised CCS and RCS. [8][9]
- **RSP (Recognised Service Provider):** NGOs receiving SWD subventions, non-profit
  organisations/social enterprises, private organisations, medical/rehabilitation
  operators and licensed private hospitals recognised under the Scheme. [4]
- **Co-payment:** the percentage of the service package value the elderly person
  pays (5/8/12/16/25/40%); the Government pays the rest. [4][5]
- **VISE (Voucher Information System for the Elderly):** the online system for
  applying, checking voucher amounts and usage, calculating co-payment category,
  and searching RSPs/packages. [1][5][12]
- **MMDHI (Median Monthly Domestic Household Income):** the Census and Statistics
  Department figure, by household size, used to set the co-payment income bands. [5][7]
- See also `knowledge/glossary.md` for CSSA, Social Security Allowance, MPF, etc.

# Important Dates

- **1 September 2023** — the Scheme was **regularised** (made a permanent
  service), with expanded service scope (assistive-technology rental), use of two
  RSPs simultaneously, and launch of VISE. [2][10]
- **Voucher quota:** to increase from **12,000 (present)** to **16,000 in
  2026-27** (per the main scheme page). Earlier targets: ~7,377 recipients (Jul
  2023), 10,000 in 2023/24, 12,000 in 2025/26. [1][10]
- **Co-payment table effective 1 April 2026** (2026-27 price level), income bands
  set with reference to **Q3 2025 MMDHI**. [7]
- **2026-27 voucher values:** floor **$4,526** / ceiling **$10,824** per month. [1][7]
- ⚠️ **Needs Manual Review:** these figures (quota, voucher floor/ceiling, income
  bands) change over time — reconfirm the current values on the main scheme page
  and current co-payment table at each verification. [1][7]

# Examples

*Illustrative only — not a guarantee of eligibility or of the exact co-payment.*

- **Example A (qualifying, low income):** An elderly person assessed under SCNAMES
  as needing CCS, on the CWL, not yet receiving any subsidised CCS/RCS, living
  alone with monthly income of $7,000 (1-person household, ≤ $7,875 = ≤75% MMDHI)
  → **Category II, 8% co-payment**. If they buy a $6,000 package, they pay
  $6,000 × 8% = $480; the Government pays $5,520. [5][7]
- **Example B (CSSA recipient):** An elderly CSSA recipient assessed and
  waitlisted → automatically **Category I, 5%**; no special-grant reimbursement
  application is needed. On a $5,000 package they pay $250. [5][7]
- **Example C (non-qualifying):** An elderly person already receiving Integrated
  Home Care Services (a subsidised CCS) is **not eligible** for the voucher until
  they cease that subsidised service. [5]
- **Example D (declines to disclose income):** An elderly person who does not want
  to report household income can still get a voucher but pays the **maximum 40%
  (Category VI)** co-payment. [5][6]

# Edge Cases

- **Applying on behalf of the elderly person:** A family member/carer with the
  elderly person's prescribed consent may apply and sign. For an elderly person
  incapable of managing their own affairs or mentally incapacitated (s.2, Mental
  Health Ordinance Cap. 136), a "relevant person" under the Personal Data
  (Privacy) Ordinance (Cap. 486) may give consent on their behalf. [6]
- **Health deterioration while on CCS waiting list:** If first assessed for CCS by
  interRAI-HC 9.3 and later re-assessed as matching RCS, the **original CCS
  application date is used** to waitlist for RCS. [8]
- **Package exceeding the ceiling / extra services:** Any amount above the ceiling
  voucher value, and extra non-recognised items, are **not** subsidised and must
  be paid by the user; RSPs generally do not refund co-payment. [4][5]
- **Switching RSPs mid-month:** If already using two RSPs in the same calendar
  month, a switch to a new RSP/package takes effect the **following month** at the
  earliest. [5]
- **Stopping service temporarily:** Stopping for more than one month normally means
  discharge from the RSP; place reservation requires CCSV Office permission and
  attracts no subsidy during the reserved period. [5]
- **Minimum age not stated in CCSV documents:** see ⚠️ Needs Manual Review under
  Eligibility. [1][4]
