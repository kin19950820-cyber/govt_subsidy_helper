# Overview

「離院長者綜合支援計劃」（英文簡稱 **IDSP**，本文稱「支援計劃」）為**離院後有較高
風險再次緊急入院的 60 歲或以上住院病人**提供**過渡性綜合支援服務**，目的是減低他們
再次入院的風險，幫助他們**居家安老**。 [1]

簡單講：呢個唔係一項「自己去申請、政府派錢」嘅現金津貼。你唔使自己填表申請。當一位
**60 歲或以上**嘅長者喺公立醫院準備出院時，醫院嘅「**出院規劃隊伍**」會評估佢出院後
會唔會好高風險再次緊急入院；如果係，隊伍就會**主動轉介**佢去合適嘅過渡性離院支援
服務（例如老人科日間醫院、由社區機構提供嘅家居支援等）。 [1]

支援計劃**由勞工及福利局撥款，並由醫院管理局（醫管局）營運**。 [1]

The IDSP provides transitional and integrated support services for discharged
patients aged 60 or above who are at a higher risk of emergency re-admission,
with a view to reducing their risk of readmission. It is **referral-based** —
patients are assessed and referred by the hospital's Discharge Planning Team at
the point of discharge, not by a self-submitted application. [1]

# Eligibility

**Objective:** Reduce the risk of emergency (unplanned) re-admission for
high-risk elderly patients leaving public hospitals, so they can age in place. [1]

**Who is served:** "Discharged patients **aged 60 or above** who are at a higher
risk of emergency re-admission **assessed by the Hospital Authority (HA)'s
Discharge Planning Teams (DPTs)**." [1] The 2019 official reply describes the
target as "medical patients aged 60 or above who are discharged from hospitals
and have higher risk of unplanned re-admission to hospital" (e.g. elderly with
multiple and/or chronic illnesses). [4][5]

**Who is not served:** Persons who are **not** inpatients of a participating
public (HA) hospital; persons **under 60**; or patients **not assessed by the
DPT as higher-risk for emergency re-admission**. There is no direct public
sign-up channel — patients not identified/referred by a DPT are not enrolled. [1]
(Elderly who cannot benefit from IDSP may be covered by a **separate** SWD/Care
scheme — see Exceptions.) [5]

**Eligibility criteria:** (a) inpatient of a participating HA hospital; (b) aged
**60 or above**; and (c) **assessed by the hospital's Discharge Planning Team as
having a higher risk of emergency re-admission** after discharge. [1]

**Household definition:** Not applicable in the CSSA/allowance sense — IDSP is a
**service** targeted at the individual patient (with support and training also
extended to the patient's **carer**). There is no household-based unit of
assessment. [1] See `glossary.md#household`.

**Income definition:** Not applicable — IDSP is **not means-tested**; enrolment
turns on clinical/functional risk assessment by the DPT, not on income. [1]
⚠️ Needs Manual Review: confirm whether any downstream referred service (e.g.
home support) applies a fee/means element to the patient.

**Assets definition:** Not applicable — no asset test is stated for IDSP. [1]

**Residency requirement:** Not separately stated; eligibility is by virtue of
being an inpatient of a participating HA public hospital who meets the age and
risk criteria. [1] ⚠️ Needs Manual Review: confirm any HK-residency/eligible-
person requirement inherited from HA public-hospital eligibility rules.

# Means Test

IDSP is **not a means-tested cash grant**. Enrolment is determined by the
Discharge Planning Team's clinical assessment of the patient's **risk of
emergency re-admission**, combined with the age (60+) and inpatient criteria —
**not** by income or assets. [1][4]

Whether any **fee** is payable for the transitional support services that a
patient is referred to (e.g. HA geriatric day hospital care, community
home-support services) is **not stated** in the IDSP sources reviewed.
⚠️ Needs Manual Review: confirm fees/co-payment (if any) for IDSP referrals and
whether these follow standard HA public-service charges. [1]

# Required Documents

There is **no application form** for the patient to complete for IDSP; enrolment
is by DPT referral during the hospital stay. [1]

**Required:** None stated for the patient to submit — the Discharge Planning
Team formulates the discharge plan and makes referrals internally. [1]

**Supporting (if applicable):** ⚠️ Needs Manual Review — the IDSP sources
reviewed do not list any patient-submitted documents; confirm whether the
downstream community home-support provider requests any documents (e.g.
identity, address) at intake. [1]

# Application Flow

- **Application window:** Not applicable in the fixed-window sense. Enrolment
  happens **during the hospital stay, around the time of discharge**, whenever a
  60+ inpatient is assessed by the DPT as higher-risk. There is **no annual
  application period**. [1]
- **How to apply / submission channel:** **Referral-based, not self-apply.** The
  "Discharge Planning Team" (comprising doctors, nurses, occupational therapists,
  physiotherapists, etc.) set up by the participating public hospital assesses
  the patient, formulates a discharge plan, and **actively refers** the patient
  to suitable transitional post-discharge support services according to care
  needs. [1]
- **Review process:** The DPT carries out the risk assessment and care-needs
  assessment; suitable patients are referred either to HA **geriatric day
  hospitals** (continued nursing/rehabilitation) and/or to **Home Support Teams**
  operated by commissioned community service providers. [1]

# Approval Flow

- **Approval workflow:** The **Discharge Planning Team** decides enrolment and
  the specific referrals as part of the discharge plan; the **Home Support Team**
  (mainly social workers and care workers, run by a community service provider
  commissioned by the HA) then provides the post-discharge support. [1]
- **Additional information requests:** Not specified in the sources reviewed.
  ⚠️ Needs Manual Review. [1]
- **Appeal:** No formal appeal mechanism for IDSP enrolment is stated in the
  sources reviewed (this is a clinician-led referral, not a benefits
  adjudication). ⚠️ Needs Manual Review. [1]

# Payment

IDSP does **not pay cash to patients**. It is a **service programme** — patients
receive support services (discharge planning, geriatric day hospital
care/rehabilitation, transitional home support such as personal care, meals,
home modification and cleaning, escort, etc.) and carers receive training. [1]

Programme funding: IDSP **is funded by the Labour and Welfare Bureau and operated
by the Hospital Authority**. [1] For scale, the annual total expenditure of IDSP
ranged from **$171.6 million (2014-15)** to a revised estimate of **$192.4
million (2018-19)** per the 2019 LegCo reply. [4]

# Renewal

There is no annual re-application. Support under IDSP is **transitional** (time-
limited). The 2011 LegCo paper reported that the **average duration of home
support service was about nine (9) weeks** during the pilots. [3] ⚠️ Needs Manual
Review: confirm the **current** standard/maximum service duration, as this
figure is from 2011 and the programme has since been regularised and expanded.

The DPT arranges **continued care** for patients after they leave IDSP — e.g.
referring those in need to the HA's **geriatric day hospitals** to continue
nursing care and rehabilitation. [1] Carers are trained so they can **continue
to provide care to the elderly patient after leaving the IDSP**. [1]

# Exceptions

- **Not for under-60s or non-referred patients:** Enrolment requires the DPT's
  risk assessment; patients not identified as higher-risk are not enrolled. [1]
- **Separate SWD/Community Care scheme for those who cannot benefit from IDSP:**
  The Government also ran a **separate** pilot — "支援在公立醫院接受治療後離院的
  長者試驗計劃 / Support Programme for Elderly Persons Discharged from Public
  Hospitals" — targeting discharged elderly **unable to benefit from IDSP**,
  which involved **case management by SWD and co-payment levels (co-payment
  ranging across levels)**. This is a **distinct scheme from IDSP** and its fees
  must **not** be attributed to IDSP. [5] ⚠️ Needs Manual Review: confirm the
  current status/name of this separate SWD scheme (reported extended to
  30 Sep 2023 in official replies).
- **Cancellation:** No cancellation/overpayment-recovery mechanism applies, since
  IDSP is a service (no cash paid to the patient). A patient may decline or
  discontinue the referred support services. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review: confirm the
  formal discharge-from-programme process. [1]

# FAQ

**Q:** 我要唔要自己填表申請「離院長者綜合支援計劃」？
**A:** 唔使。呢個唔係自己申請嘅計劃。當你（60 歲或以上）喺公立醫院準備出院時，醫院
嘅「出院規劃隊伍」會評估你出院後係咪有較高風險再次緊急入院，如果係，佢哋會**主動
轉介**你去合適嘅支援服務。 [1]

**Q:** 幾多歲先合資格？
**A:** **60 歲或以上**、由醫院「出院規劃隊伍」評估為離院後有較高風險再次緊急入院嘅
住院病人。 [1][4]

**Q:** 支援計劃提供啲乜嘢服務？
**A:** 包括制訂離院計劃、轉介去醫管局老人科日間醫院繼續護理及康復、或由社區服務機構
提供過渡性家居支援（個人護理、膳食服務、家居改裝和清潔、護送服務等），亦為照顧者
提供護理培訓。 [1]

**Q:** 使唔使畀錢？
**A:** IDSP 嘅官方資料**冇列明**病人需要繳付嘅費用。⚠️ 需人手覆核 — 請向醫院／
服務機構確認個別轉介服務（例如家居支援）是否收費。 [1]

**Q:** 邊個部門負責？
**A:** **由勞工及福利局撥款，並由醫院管理局（醫管局）營運。** [1]

# Important Definitions

- **Discharge Planning Team (DPT) / 出院規劃隊伍** — a team set up by a
  participating public hospital, comprising **doctors, nurses, occupational
  therapists, physiotherapists, etc.**, that formulates discharge plans and
  refers patients to suitable transitional post-discharge support. [1]
- **Home Support Team / 家居支援隊伍** — operated by **community service
  providers commissioned by the HA**, mainly comprising **social workers and
  care workers**, providing post-discharge support to enable ageing in place. [1]
- **Geriatric day hospital / 老人科日間醫院** — an HA facility to which DPTs may
  refer patients for continued nursing care and rehabilitation. [1]
- **Transitional support / 過渡性支援** — time-limited support after discharge
  (e.g. ~9 weeks average home support in the pilots). [1][3]
- See `glossary.md` for household/means-test terms (not applicable to IDSP).

# Important Dates

- **2008** — IDSP started in pilot mode (Labour and Welfare Bureau / SWD with HA);
  three pilots launched **March 2008, August 2008 and July 2009** in Kwun Tong,
  Kwai Tsing and Tuen Mun. [3]
- **2011-12** — Programme **regularised and expanded territory-wide** with
  additional recurrent funding of **$148 million**; participating hospitals
  increased to **15**; projected annual beneficiaries increased from **8,000 to
  33,000**; a **transitional residential care** element added. [3]
- **27 February 2019** — LCQ17 reply: IDSP operated across HA hospital groupings
  with **13 partnering Home Support Teams run by NGOs**; annual expenditure
  ranged from **$171.6M (2014-15) to $192.4M (2018-19 revised estimate)**. [4]
- **Third quarter of 2023** — Under the 2022 Policy Address, IDSP expanded by
  one-third: annual beneficiaries **from about 33,000 to 45,000**, and home
  support service referrals **from about 9,000 to 11,000**. [6]
- ⚠️ Needs Manual Review: confirm the **current** number of participating
  hospitals and the current standard service duration.

# Examples

*Illustrative only — not a guarantee of enrolment; enrolment is decided by the
hospital's Discharge Planning Team.*

- **Likely enrolled:** A 78-year-old with heart failure and diabetes, admitted to
  a participating HA hospital's medical ward, assessed by the DPT before discharge
  as having a high risk of unplanned re-admission — the DPT formulates a discharge
  plan and refers her to a Home Support Team for transitional personal care and
  meals, and provides caring training to her daughter. [1]
- **Likely not enrolled:** A 55-year-old discharged after a minor procedure, not
  aged 60+ and not assessed as high-risk — outside the IDSP target group. [1]

# Edge Cases

- **Elderly who cannot benefit from IDSP:** may fall under a **separate** SWD
  pilot/community-care scheme with case management and co-payment — a distinct
  scheme; IDSP itself has no stated fee. [5] ⚠️ Needs Manual Review on that
  scheme's current status.
- **After IDSP ends:** DPTs arrange continued care (e.g. referral to HA geriatric
  day hospitals); carers trained to continue caring for the patient. [1]
- **Non-participating hospital:** Patients in hospitals not in the programme are
  not enrolled via IDSP. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review: confirm the current list of
  participating hospitals/clusters. [4]
