# Overview

長者醫療券計劃（醫療券）由**衞生署醫療券組**負責，係一項政府津貼，幫**65歲或以上**
嘅香港長者，用嚟俾私營嘅**基層醫療（primary healthcare）服務**費用，例如睇私家
醫生、牙醫、中醫、物理治療、驗眼配鏡等。目的係鼓勵長者揀選最切合自己健康需要嘅私營
服務，並同醫護建立長期關係（「家庭醫生」概念）。醫療券**唔使審查入息或資產**，只要
夠年齡同持有有效身份證明就可以用。 [1][2][13]

簡單講：夠65歲、有**有效香港身份證**（或入境處發出嘅**豁免證明書**）就得，唔使入息
審查、唔使預先登記、唔使領取實體券。政府每年1月1日會自動存入一筆醫療券金額落你嘅
戶口。現時每年金額係 **$2,000**，可累積上限係 **$8,000**。 [1][6]

# Official Purpose

"The Government launched the Elderly Health Care Voucher Scheme … to provide
subsidies for eligible elderly to receive private primary healthcare services
that best suit their health needs." The Scheme "aims to supplement existing
public healthcare services … by providing financial incentive for elderly
persons to choose private healthcare services that best suit their health needs,
including preventive care", and "helps promote the concept of family doctor."
[2][13]

# Eligibility

**Objective:** Subsidise elderly persons aged 65 or above to use private primary
healthcare services that best suit their health needs, in addition to (not
replacing) existing public healthcare services. [1][2]

**Who can apply / who can use vouchers:** An elderly person who is: [1][11]
- **aged 65 or above**; and
- **holding a valid Hong Kong Identity Card (HKIC)** within the meaning of the
  Registration of Persons Ordinance (Cap. 177), **or a valid Certificate of
  Exemption (CoE)** within the meaning of the Immigration Ordinance (Cap. 115),
  issued by the Immigration Department.

The person **need not be a permanent resident** — non-permanent residents who
hold a valid HKIC/CoE are eligible. [11 · Q1]

**Who cannot use vouchers:** A person who "**obtained a HKIC by virtue of a
previous permission to land or remain in Hong Kong granted to him/her and such
permission has expired or ceased to be valid**" is **not** eligible. A person who
cannot present a valid HKIC/CoE cannot use vouchers. [1][6][11 · Q1]

**Eligibility criteria (summary):** Age 65+ **AND** valid HKIC/CoE (not falling
within the excluded category above). There is **no income test, no asset test,
and no residence-year requirement**. [1][11]

**Household definition:** Not applicable — vouchers are issued to the **individual
elderly person**, not to a household. (Spouses may optionally *pair up* their
accounts to share use — see Exceptions.) [1][9]

**Income definition:** Not applicable — the Scheme is **not means-tested**. [1]

**Assets definition:** Not applicable — the Scheme is **not means-tested**. [1]

**Residency requirement:** No minimum-years-of-residence requirement is stated;
eligibility is based on age plus holding a valid HKIC or CoE. Permanent
residence is **not** required. [1][11 · Q1]

# Means Test

**The Elderly Health Care Voucher Scheme is NOT means-tested.** There is no
income limit and no asset limit; every eligible elderly person aged 65 or above
holding a valid HKIC/CoE receives the same annual voucher amount regardless of
financial circumstances. Elderly persons receiving CSSA or Disability Allowance
(DA) remain eligible to use health care vouchers. [1][11 · Q2]

# Required Documents

**Required (to create an account / to use vouchers):**
- **Original, valid Hong Kong Identity Card**, or a valid **Certificate of
  Exemption** (if applicable). A **photocopy is not accepted**, and a Senior
  Citizen Card **cannot** be used in its place. [6][11 · Q13, Q14]
- An **SMS-enabled mobile phone number** (may be the number of the elderly
  person, or of a family member / carer) so the system can send an SMS
  confirming the voucher amount used. [6]

**Supporting (if applicable):**
- For a **mentally incapacitated** elderly person represented by a guardian: the
  guardian confirms via the paper "Consent of Voucher Recipient", and the
  "Consent of Witness/Guardian to Transfer Personal Data". [11 · Q25]
- For an **illiterate** elderly person: a mark or fingerprint on the paper
  consent form in the presence of an adult witness (who records name, HKIC no.,
  date and signs). [11 · Q26, Q27]
- For **spousal account pairing**: both spouses present their valid HKIC/CoE and
  declare their spousal relationship; supporting documents of the marriage are
  **not** required at pairing but the Government may later request them for
  verification. [9]

# Application Flow

- **Application window:** **No application and no pre-registration are required.**
  Eligible elderly persons need not pre-register, need not pre-collect and need
  not carry any physical voucher. Vouchers are issued and used **electronically
  through the eHealth System (Subsidies)** and are **not** issued in paper form.
  [4][6]
- **How to use / submission channel (step by step at an enrolled provider):** [6]
  1. Browse the **List of Enrolled Healthcare Service Providers** (Scheme logo
     displayed at the clinic); optionally check voucher balance (website, hotline
     **2838 0511**, or eHealth App) and ask about the service and charging items.
  2. **Receive the healthcare service in person** (vouchers can only be used
     after receiving service in person).
  3. **Present a valid HKIC or CoE** to the enrolled provider and indicate the
     wish to use vouchers. For a first-time user, the provider **creates a
     personal voucher account free of charge** and deposits the relevant voucher
     amount based on the year the person became eligible.
  4. **Give consent electronically (e-consent)** to the voucher amount to be used
     (insert HKIC into the Smart ID card reader). A paper "Consent of Voucher
     Recipient" is used only in exceptional cases (e.g. CoE holder, damaged card
     chip, or guardian consent).
  5. Provider submits the claim and gives a **"Notice on Use of Health Care
     Voucher"**; an SMS is sent to the designated phone number.
- **Review process:** No means-test review; the provider verifies identity via
  the HKIC and the eHealth System (Subsidies) confirms the balance and records
  the transaction. The Department of Health runs monitoring and audit checks. [6][9]

# Approval Flow

- **Approval workflow:** There is **no approval decision** for the elderly person
  — eligibility is automatic on age plus valid HKIC/CoE, and the annual amount is
  deposited automatically on 1 January. The individual does not submit an
  application to be "approved". (Approval/enrolment applies to **healthcare
  service providers**, not to voucher recipients.) [1][4][6]
- **Additional information requests:** The Government (DH) may **directly request
  supporting documents** for verification when necessary — e.g. to verify a
  declared spousal relationship for account pairing. [9]
- **Appeal:** No means-test appeal mechanism is documented for voucher recipients
  (there is no eligibility determination to appeal). General enquiries: Scheme
  hotline **2838 2311**; balance enquiry hotline **2838 0511**. **⚠️ Needs Manual
  Review** — whether any formal complaint/appeal channel exists for a
  recipient-side dispute is not stated on the public pages read. [4][7]

# Payment

- Payment is **in kind (a voucher subsidy), not a cash payment** to the elderly
  person, and vouchers **cannot be redeemed for cash**. [5]
- The **annual voucher amount is deposited automatically into the individual's
  voucher account on 1 January** each year, from the year in which the person
  becomes eligible (i.e. the year they turn 65). Vouchers are used to pay the
  enrolled provider directly; the provider claims reimbursement from the Scheme.
  [1][6][11 · Q9, Q10]
- Current amounts (see Important Dates for the full historical table): [1]
  - **Annual voucher amount: $2,000** (for 2020 and onwards).
  - **Accumulation limit: $8,000** (with effect from 26 June 2019).
- **No advance** of a voucher amount not yet issued is allowed. [5][11 · Q15]
- Per-visit use: there is **no cap per visit** except that the total used cannot
  exceed the actual service fee, and there must be **no handling/administrative
  fee** for using vouchers, and no higher charge than a non-voucher patient. The
  exception is the **optometry cap** (see below). [5][11 · Q17, Q18]

# Renewal

- **No renewal application is needed.** The annual amount is credited
  automatically every 1 January while the person remains eligible. [1][6]
- **Carrying forward:** Any **unused amount can be retained for use in following
  years**, with **no limit on the number of years** it may be carried forward —
  **but** the cumulative total in the account as at 1 January each year (that
  year's new amount plus all carried-forward balance) **cannot exceed the
  accumulation limit** (currently **$8,000**). Amount above the limit on that
  day is **forfeited**. [1][5][11 · Q16]

# Exceptions

- **Optometry sub-cap:** With effect from **26 June 2019**, the voucher amount
  that can be spent on **optometry services is capped at $2,000 every two years**
  (a "Quota"). Unused Quota **lapses at the end of each 2-year cycle** and cannot
  be carried forward. For persons born **1954 or before**, the first cycle ran
  26 Jun 2019 – 31 Dec 2020, the second 1 Jan 2021 – 31 Dec 2022, and so on; for
  persons born **1955 or after**, the 2-year cycle counts from 1 January of the
  year they become eligible. [5][11 · Q17, Q19, Q20]
- **What vouchers CANNOT pay for:** solely purchasing products (e.g. medication,
  spectacles, dried seafood, personal-care/food products, medical equipment)
  without an associated service; government-subsidised public healthcare
  (including services HA purchases from the private sector, e.g. GOPC
  Public-Private Partnership, unless otherwise specified); **in-patient services,
  pre-paid healthcare services and day-surgery procedures** (e.g. cataract
  surgery, endoscopy); and services from providers **not enrolled** in the
  Scheme. [5][11 · Q3–Q8]
- **Shared use between spouses:** Two elderly persons **both aged 65+ and both
  eligible**, in a **monogamous marriage recognised by Hong Kong law**, may pair
  up their voucher accounts (in person at an enrolled provider, or via the
  "Consent of Sharing Health Care Vouchers …" form if one spouse is immobile).
  After pairing, either party may use the spouse's balance **once their own
  balance is exhausted**. **No prior application** is needed. Either party may
  opt out later by submitting the designated form. (Effective 28 July 2023.)
  [2][9][11 · Q28, Q29]
- **Death of a recipient:** When a recipient dies, the voucher balance is **not
  transferred** to anyone, including a paired spouse. **Using a deceased person's
  HKIC / account to spend the remaining vouchers may constitute fraud** and
  criminal liability. [9][11 · Q23]
- **Cancellation / recovery / misuse:** The Department of Health operates
  "stringent monitoring and audit mechanisms, including regular and sampling
  checks, routine inspections, and detection of aberrant patterns of
  transactions." False declaration of a spousal relationship, or misuse of a
  deceased's account, is referred to law-enforcement agencies and may lead to
  criminal prosecution and imprisonment. [9]

# FAQ

**Q: Do I need to apply or pre-register for the voucher?**
**A:** No. Eligible elderly persons need not register in advance, need not
pre-collect and need not carry vouchers; just present a valid HKIC/CoE at an
enrolled provider after receiving service. [4][11 · Q11]

**Q: Am I eligible if I am not a permanent resident, or if I receive CSSA / DA?**
**A:** Yes to both — any elderly resident aged 65+ holding a valid HKIC/CoE (and
not in the excluded expired-permission category) is eligible, including
non-permanent residents and CSSA/DA recipients. [11 · Q1, Q2]

**Q: Must I wait until my exact 65th birthday to use vouchers?**
**A:** No. Vouchers are provided from the **year** in which you reach 65. For
example, a person turning 65 in October 2023 is provided with vouchers usable
from 1 January 2023. [11 · Q9]

**Q: When is the annual amount deposited?**
**A:** In general, **1 January** of each year during the Scheme period. [11 · Q10]

**Q: What happens to unused vouchers?**
**A:** They carry forward to later years with no year limit, but the total on
1 January each year cannot exceed the accumulation limit (currently $8,000);
excess on that day is forfeited. [1][11 · Q16]

**Q: Can I get cash, or a top-up in advance?**
**A:** No. Vouchers cannot be redeemed for cash, and no advance of a not-yet-
issued amount is allowed. [5][11 · Q15]

**Q: Can I use vouchers for day surgery (e.g. cataract) or in-patient care?**
**A:** No — the Scheme is for primary healthcare; in-patient services, pre-paid
services and day-surgery procedures are excluded. Out-patient A&E that involves
no in-patient service can be paid. [5][11 · Q5, Q6, Q8]

**Q: Will I be charged a handling fee for using vouchers?**
**A:** No. Providers must not charge handling/administrative fees, and must not
charge a voucher user more than a non-voucher patient for equivalent services.
[11 · Q18]

# Important Definitions

- **eHealth System (Subsidies) / Voucher account (eHealth (Subsidies) Account):**
  the electronic system through which vouchers are issued, stored and used; a
  personal voucher account is created (free) by an enrolled provider on first
  use. [6]
- **Enrolled healthcare service provider:** a Hong-Kong-registered healthcare
  professional who has enrolled in the Scheme (Scheme logo displayed). Only
  enrolled providers can accept vouchers. Eligible professions: medical
  practitioners, Chinese medicine practitioners, dentists, chiropractors,
  registered/enrolled nurses, physiotherapists, occupational therapists,
  radiographers, medical laboratory technologists, optometrists (Part I of the
  register), and — under the Accredited Registers Scheme — audiologists,
  dietitians, clinical psychologists and speech therapists. **Note:** medical
  practitioners not enlisted in the **Primary Care Directory (PCD)** are
  ineligible from 6 Dec 2023; dentists from 1 Mar 2025; occupational therapists
  and physiotherapists from 2 Sep 2025. Use of allied-health and laboratory-test
  services is subject to the current referral arrangement. [3][5]
- **Accumulation limit:** the maximum cumulative voucher balance an account may
  hold as at 1 January each year (currently $8,000); amounts above it on that day
  are forfeited. [1][5]
- **Optometry Quota:** the $2,000-per-2-years sub-limit on spending vouchers on
  optometry services. [5][11 · Q17]
- **Certificate of Exemption (CoE):** a document issued by the Immigration
  Department under the Immigration Ordinance (Cap. 115); a valid CoE is accepted
  in place of an HKIC for the Scheme. [1][11 · Q1]

# Examples

*Illustrative only — not a guarantee of eligibility; confirm on the official
site.*

- **Qualifying:** Mr Chan, aged 68, holds a valid HKIC. On 1 January he has
  $2,000 credited plus $3,500 carried forward = $5,500 balance (within the
  $8,000 limit). He visits an enrolled private GP, receives a consultation, and
  consents (via Smart ID card reader) to use $400 of vouchers. He pays nothing
  extra; the provider claims the $400. [1][6]
- **Accumulation-limit forfeiture:** Mrs Wong has an unused balance of $7,500 on
  31 December. On 1 January the new $2,000 would make $9,500, exceeding the
  $8,000 limit, so **$1,500 is forfeited** and her balance becomes $8,000. [1][5]
- **Optometry cap:** Mr Lee wants new glasses. Even with an $8,000 balance, he
  may spend **at most $2,000 on optometry within his current 2-year cycle**; the
  rest of an optometry bill must be paid by other means. Unused optometry Quota
  does not carry to the next cycle. [5][11 · Q17, Q19]
- **Not qualifying (this use):** Mr Ho, 66, wants to use vouchers for a private
  hospital **day-surgery cataract operation** — **not allowed** (day-surgery
  procedures are excluded), though he remains an eligible voucher holder for
  other primary-care services. [5][11 · Q8]

# Edge Cases

- **Turns 65 mid-year:** eligible from the **year** of turning 65 (vouchers
  usable from 1 January of that year), not from the exact birthday. [11 · Q9]
- **Non-permanent resident / CoE holder:** eligible if holding a valid HKIC or
  CoE (CoE holders generally use the paper consent, as the card reader may not
  apply). [6][11 · Q1]
- **Expired "permission to land/remain" HKIC:** a person who obtained a HKIC by a
  previous permission to land/remain that has since expired or ceased to be valid
  is **not** eligible. [1][11 · Q1, Q9]
- **Invalid old-format HKIC:** the Immigration Department announced that old-form
  smart ID cards (issued before 26 Nov 2018) bearing a year of birth in or before
  1969 became **invalid on 12 October 2025**; an invalid HKIC cannot be used —
  the person must replace it first. [11 · Note to Q1 etc.]
- **Bedridden / in convalescent home:** may use vouchers if an enrolled provider
  delivers the service in person at that location. [11 · Q22]
- **Mentally incapacitated:** guardian consents via paper "Consent of Voucher
  Recipient" and "Consent of Witness/Guardian to Transfer Personal Data". [11 · Q25]
- **Illiterate:** mark/fingerprint on the paper consent form in front of an adult
  witness (who may be clinic staff such as a nurse). [11 · Q26, Q27]
- **Spouse dies after account pairing:** the deceased's balance is **not** passed
  to the survivor; using the deceased's account may be fraud. [9][11 · Q23]
- **Using vouchers in the Mainland:** possible only at the designated **HKU–SZ
  Hospital** / its Health Centre and at the **GBA Pilot Scheme** designated
  medical institutions, for designated out-patient services only; e-consent is
  **not** available for these Mainland uses. [5][9][12]
