# Overview

呢個唔係「派錢」嘅現金津貼，而係政府（社會福利署）資助嘅**上門／社區照顧服務**，
幫助長者「**居家安老**」（留喺屋企住，唔使入住院舍）。服務由社署資助非政府機構嘅
服務隊提供，並會按長者嘅**家庭經濟狀況收取費用**（收費有分等級）。 [1][2][3][5]

**重要更新（2026 年 4 月 1 日生效）：** 「**綜合家居照顧服務（體弱個案）**」
〔IHCS(FC)〕同「**改善家居及社區照顧服務**」（EHCCS）已**合併改名**為一項服務，叫
「**體弱長者家居照顧服務 / Home Care Services (HCS) for Frail Elderly Persons**」。
同時：「改善家居及社區照顧服務隊（EHCCS Teams）」改名為「**家居照顧服務隊
（HCSTs）**」；「綜合家居照顧服務隊（IHCS Teams）」改名為「**家居照顧及支援服務隊
（HCSSTs）**」。 [1][2]

至於原本嘅「**綜合家居照顧服務（普通個案）**」〔IHCS(OC)〕—— 服務對象係缺損程度較
輕（或只需個人照顧、家居清潔等）嘅長者、殘疾人士，以及有社會需要嘅個人及家庭 ——
現時對應「**家居支援服務 / Home Support Services (HSS)**」。 [1][2][6]

In short: these are **subsidised, referral-based home-care services** (not a cash
grant) run by SWD-funded NGO service teams to help frail or mildly-impaired
elderly age in place. Access is by **assessment/referral**, and **fees are charged
according to the service user's family household financial status.** [1][3][5]

# Official Purpose

**Home Care Services (HCS) for Frail Elderly Persons** — "Home Care Services for
Frail Elderly Persons are home-based community care services providing a range of
care and support services to frail elderly persons aged 60 or above living in the
community and who are assessed and recommended for Community Care Services or
Residential Care Services under the Standardised Care Need Assessment Mechanism
for Elderly Services. The services aim at facilitating service users to continue
ageing in place for as long as possible and maintaining their optimal level of
functioning, while providing various kinds of support and assistance to carers." [3]

**Home Support Services (HSS)** — "Home Support Services (HSS) provide home-based
care and support services to people in the community to facilitate service users
to continue aging in place for as long as possible and achieve or maintain their
optimal level of functioning. It also provides support to their carers to
alleviate their stress." [4]

**IHCS (Ordinary Cases), original objective** — the service teams provide a
flexible range of services for elderly persons, people with disabilities and
individuals and families with social need, to "(a) stay in the community; (b)
achieve and maintain an optimal level of functioning and independence; (c) acquire
the necessary skills to adapt to changing health status; and (d) prevent premature
and inappropriate admission to hospital and residential care." [6]

# Eligibility

**Objective:** Help frail or mildly-impaired elderly (and, for HSS, also persons
with disabilities and families with social need) **age in place** through
home-based/community care and support, delaying or avoiding hospital or
residential-care admission, and support their carers. [3][4][6]

**Who can apply / target service users:**

- **Home Care Services (HCS) for Frail Elderly Persons** (前 IHCS 體弱個案 + EHCCS):
  Elderly persons "**Aged 60 or above living in the community and not receiving
  institutional service**; and **Assessed and recommended for Community Care
  Services or Residential Care Services under the Standardised Care Need Assessment
  Mechanism for Elderly Services.**" Carers of these service users are also served. [3]
- **Home Support Services (HSS)** (前 IHCS 普通個案):
  - "**Elderly persons**: refer to people aged **60 or above** residing in the
    community and not receiving institutional services, and are assessed to be at
    the level of **mild impairment or requiring a higher level of care** under the
    interRAI™ Check-up (CU) assessment tool or the Standardised Care Need
    Assessment Mechanism for Elderly Services." [4]
  - "**Persons with disabilities or individuals and families with social needs**:
    application for HSS under these categories should be **referred by social
    workers of social welfare service units**." [4]
- **IHCS (Ordinary Cases) original wording** (for reference / pre-April-2026):
  "(a) Elderly persons: refer to people aged **65 or above**. Persons aged between
  **60 and 64 may receive the service if there is proven need**; (b) People with
  disabilities …; (c) Individuals and families with social need …" [6]

**Who cannot apply:** Persons **receiving institutional (residential) service** are
outside the target group for the community/home services (HCS: "not receiving
institutional service") — although IHCS(OC) could, exceptionally and irregularly,
provide escort for home leave to residential-care users. [3][6] An elderly person
who is **not assessed/recommended** for the relevant service under the assessment
mechanism is not admitted. [3][4][7]

**Eligibility criteria (HCS for Frail Elderly Persons):** (a) aged **60+**; (b)
**living in the community, not in institutional care**; and (c) **assessed and
recommended for Community Care Services or Residential Care Services** under the
Standardised Care Need Assessment Mechanism for Elderly Services (SCNAMES). [3]

**Eligibility criteria (HSS):** aged **60+**, residing in the community, **not in
institutional care**, and assessed at **mild impairment / higher level of care**
under the interRAI™ Check-up (a simplified, standardised tool) or SCNAMES. Non-
elderly categories (PWD, families with social need) require a **social worker
referral**. [4]

**Household definition:** No CSSA-style "household unit of assessment" is used for
eligibility; eligibility is based on the individual's age, community-dwelling
status and care-need assessment. **Fees**, however, are set according to the
service user's **"family household financial status."** [3][4][5] The official
sources do not define exactly whose income/assets make up that "family household"
— ⚠️ Needs Manual Review. See `glossary.md#household`.

**Income definition:** Not used for **eligibility** (eligibility is need-based). It
is used only to set the **fee tier** ("family household financial status"). The
precise income measure and thresholds are **not published on these pages**. ⚠️
Needs Manual Review. [3][4][5]

**Assets definition:** Not stated as an eligibility factor. Whether assets form
part of the "family household financial status" fee assessment is not specified. ⚠️
Needs Manual Review. [5]

**Residency requirement:** Target users are elderly "**living / residing in the
community**" in Hong Kong; the services are geographically organised by service-team
boundaries. The pages do not separately state a fixed "years-of-HK-residence"
rule. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review — confirm any HK-resident / eligible-person
requirement inherited from subsidised LTC eligibility. [1][3][4]

# Means Test

These services are **need-assessed** for eligibility (via SCNAMES / interRAI CU),
**not** means-tested for admission. However, **fees are means-related**: "**Fees
are determined according to the service users' family household financial status,
and may be adjusted.**" [3][4]

The current fee tiers (April 2026) show multiple price levels that correspond to
financial status (see Payment). The **income/asset thresholds** that map a service
user to a particular fee level are **not published** on these SWD pages. ⚠️ Needs
Manual Review — obtain the current fee-assessment (financial-status) criteria and
whether CSSA recipients are fully fee-exempt. [5]

# Required Documents

The service briefs and fee schedules **do not list a document checklist** for the
service user. Access is by referral/assessment rather than a self-submitted form. [3][4]

**Likely required (not explicitly listed in the reviewed sources — ⚠️ Needs Manual
Review):** proof of identity/age, address, and financial-status documents needed
to set the fee tier. [3][4][5]

**Supporting (if applicable):** For HSS applications by **persons with disabilities
or families with social need**, a **referral by a social worker** of a social
welfare service unit is required. [4]

# Application Flow

- **Application window:** **No fixed annual window** — applications/referrals are
  accepted **on an ongoing basis** subject to assessment and service availability;
  subsidised long-term-care services operate a **Central Waiting List (CWL)**. [7]
  ⚠️ Needs Manual Review — current waiting time / quota is not stated on the page.
- **How to apply / submission channel (referral & assessment route):** Service
  users or their family can approach the nearest **Integrated Family Service
  Centres / Integrated Services Centres**, **Medical Social Services Units**,
  **District Elderly Community Centres / Neighbourhood Elderly Centres / Social
  Centres for the Elderly**, or the **Home Care Services Teams (HCSTs)** and **Home
  Care and Support Services Teams (HCSSTs)** for enquiry/referral. [1][3][4] For
  subsidised LTC (frail cases), "**new applicants must approach referring offices**
  including **District Elderly Community Centres, Medical Social Services Units, or
  Integrated Family Service Centres**" to raise a request for subsidised LTC
  services. [7] SWD hotline: **2343 2255**; Designated Hotline for Carer Support:
  **182 183**. [3][4]
- **Review process (assessment):** Under the **Standardised Care Need Assessment
  Mechanism for Elderly Services (SCNAMES)**, an assessor "**carries out the
  assessment through home visit and face-to-face interview**", using the interRAI
  **MDS-HC** tool; the result **matches the elderly person to Community Care
  Services or Residential Care Services** according to level of impairment, and
  registers them on the **Central Waiting List**. [7] For HSS mild-impairment
  cases, the **interRAI™ Check-up (CU)** simplified tool is used. [4]

# Approval Flow

- **Approval workflow:** Eligibility/recommendation is decided by the **care-need
  assessment** (SCNAMES / interRAI CU). Following assessment and recommendation,
  the service team admits the user (subject to the Central Waiting List for
  subsidised frail-case services) and the **Home Care (and Support) Services Team
  designs the scope and frequency of services according to the individual's
  needs**, with care management and periodic review. [3][4][7]
- **Additional-information requests:** Not specified in the reviewed sources. ⚠️
  Needs Manual Review. [3][4]
- **Appeal:** No appeal mechanism is stated on the reviewed service briefs/pages.
  ⚠️ Needs Manual Review — confirm any re-assessment / appeal route under SCNAMES/CWL. [7]

# Payment

This programme **does not pay cash to the service user** — it delivers **services**
(personal care, nursing, rehabilitation, meals, cleaning, escort, respite, carer
support, etc.), and the **user pays a subsidised fee** to receive them. Fees are
"**determined according to the service users' family household financial status,
and may be adjusted.**" [3][4]

**Current Fee Schedule — Home Care Services for Frail Elderly Persons (April 2026):** [5]
- Meal service: **$13 / $16 / $19 per meal**
- Home care services: **$5.5 / $12 / $19 per hour**
- Laundry: **$0.7 (light) / $0.9 (medium) / $1.8 (heavy) per piece**

**Current Fee Schedule — Home Support Services (April 2026):** [5]
- Meal service: **$13 / $16 / $19 / $24.4 / $27.5 per meal**
- Home care services: **$5.5 / $12 / $19 / $30.9 / $39.7 per hour**
- Laundry: **$0.7 (light) / $0.9 (medium) / $1.8 (heavy) per piece**

(The multiple price points within each row correspond to different financial-status
tiers; the mapping of financial status to tier is not published on these pages —
⚠️ Needs Manual Review.) [5]

Service funding: SWD subvents the NGO service operators on the **Lump Sum Grant
(LSG)** mode (paid monthly); this is a funding mechanism to the operator, not a
payment to service users. [6]

# Renewal

There is no annual re-application in the cash-grant sense. Service is provided **on
a needs basis** with **periodic review**: for IHCS(OC)/HSS, "the service needs of
individuals should be reviewed **at least half-yearly** to ascertain their
continuing need and eligibility." [6] The MDS-HC assessment result is **valid for
12 months**. [7] Frail-case (HCS) users receive ongoing **care management and
assessment** by the team. [3]

**Cancellation / discharge:** "Service users should be **discharged if they no
longer have service need upon periodic review**." [6] IHCS(OC)/HSS priority also
covers "**discharged cases from Home Care Services for Frail Elderly Persons**"
(i.e. frail-case users stepped down to ordinary/home-support). [6]

# Exceptions

- **Not for those in institutional care:** HCS target users are "not receiving
  institutional service." For residential-care residents, IHCS(OC) may be provided
  **only irregularly and exceptionally** (mainly escort for home leave); primary
  care responsibility rests with the home operator. [3][6]
- **People with dementia:** Operators must ensure staff have **special training** in
  communicating with and handling service users with mood/behavioural symptoms of
  dementia (poor temper, unrealistic fears, agitation, wandering, aggression, etc.). [6]
- **Persons with mental illness (IHCS/HSS non-elderly category):** must be
  "**certified as mentally stable and free from aggressive/violent act**." [6]
- **CSSA / low-income priority (IHCS Ordinary Cases):** priority is given to
  individuals who "have no or poor support from friends or the community and are
  **financially disadvantaged, e.g. low-income working families [income level up to
  1.5 Comprehensive Social Security Assistance (CSSA) level], recipients of CSSA**",
  particularly where a carer's role must be relieved for them to return to work. [6]
  ⚠️ Needs Manual Review — confirm whether CSSA recipients receive a fee waiver/reduction. [5]
- **Urgent/social-need HSS cases:** urgent elderly cases where an interRAI™ CU
  cannot be completed before admission, or elderly who do not pass the CU **but have
  strong social needs**, "**may be accepted with justifications**." [4]

# FAQ

**Q:** 呢個係咪派錢畀我？
**A:** 唔係。呢個係政府資助嘅**上門／社區照顧服務**（例如個人照顧、護理、復康運動、
送飯、家居清潔、護送、日間／院舍暫託、照顧者支援等），你係**收服務、畀資助後嘅
費用**，唔係攞現金。 [3][4]

**Q:** 幾多歲先申請得？
**A:** 「體弱長者家居照顧服務」同「家居支援服務」都係**60 歲或以上**、住喺社區、
未入住院舍嘅長者。（原本 IHCS 普通個案嘅寫法係 65 歲或以上，60–64 歲有實際需要
亦可。） [3][4][6]

**Q:** 點樣申請／點評估？
**A:** 聯絡就近嘅**綜合家庭服務中心、醫務社會服務部、長者地區中心／長者鄰舍中心／
長者活動中心**，或**家居照顧服務隊／家居照顧及支援服務隊**。體弱個案會經「**安老服務
統一評估機制（SCNAMES）**」由評估員**上門面談評估**（用 interRAI MDS-HC 工具），
評估你適唔適合社區照顧或院舍照顧服務。 [1][3][7]

**Q:** 使唔使畀錢？幾錢？
**A:** 要畀**資助後嘅費用**，按你**家庭經濟狀況**分等級。以 2026 年 4 月收費表為例：
送飯每餐 $13／$16／$19（家居支援服務另有 $24.4／$27.5）；家居照顧服務每小時
$5.5／$12／$19（家居支援服務另有 $30.9／$39.7）；洗衣每件 $0.7／$0.9／$1.8。 [5]

**Q:** 2026 年 4 月改咗啲乜？
**A:** 由 2026 年 4 月 1 日起，「綜合家居照顧服務（體弱個案）」＋「改善家居及社區
照顧服務（EHCCS）」合併改名做「**體弱長者家居照顧服務**」；EHCCS 隊改名「家居照顧
服務隊」，IHCS 隊改名「家居照顧及支援服務隊」。 [1][2]

# Important Definitions

- **Home Care Services (HCS) for Frail Elderly Persons / 體弱長者家居照顧服務** —
  from 1 Apr 2026, the merged service formerly called **IHCS (Frail Cases)** and
  **EHCCS**. For frail elderly aged 60+ recommended for CCS/RCS under SCNAMES. [1][3]
- **Home Support Services (HSS) / 家居支援服務** — home-based support for
  mild-impairment elderly (60+) and, by referral, PWD and families with social
  need. Corresponds to the former **IHCS (Ordinary Cases)** scope. [1][4][6]
- **HCST — Home Care Services Teams / 家居照顧服務隊** — the renamed **EHCCS Teams**
  (from 1 Apr 2026). [1]
- **HCSST — Home Care and Support Services Teams / 家居照顧及支援服務隊** — the
  renamed **IHCS Teams** (from 1 Apr 2026). [1]
- **SCNAMES / 安老服務統一評估機制** — Standardised Care Need Assessment Mechanism
  for Elderly Services; single-entry assessment (via home visit/interview using the
  interRAI **MDS-HC** tool) that recommends an elderly person for Community Care
  Services or Residential Care Services; result valid **12 months**. [7]
- **interRAI™ Check-up (CU) / 簡易標準評估工具** — simplified standardised tool used
  to identify **mild impairment** for HSS. [4]
- **CWL — Central Waiting List / 中央輪候冊** — central registration/waiting list for
  subsidised long-term-care services. [7]
- See `glossary.md` for CSSA, household, means-test terms.

# Important Dates

# Examples

*Illustrative only — not a guarantee of admission; admission depends on the
official care-need assessment and service availability.*

- **Likely eligible (HCS for Frail Elderly Persons):** An 82-year-old living alone
  at home, assessed under SCNAMES (MDS-HC) as needing Community Care Services — the
  HCST/HCSST provides personal care, nursing care, rehabilitation exercises, meals
  and carer support; fees are set by the family household's financial status. [3][5]
- **Likely eligible (HSS):** A 68-year-old with mild impairment (identified by the
  interRAI CU) who mainly needs meal delivery, household cleaning and escort — served
  by a Home Care and Support Services Team under HSS. [4][5]
- **Referral needed (HSS non-elderly):** A 45-year-old person with a disability who
  needs home support — must be **referred by a social worker** of a social welfare
  service unit. [4]
- **Likely not admitted here:** An elderly person already living in a subsidised
  residential care home (institutional service) — outside the community/home target
  group (save for exceptional home-leave escort). [3][6]

# Edge Cases

- **Frail case stepping down:** A frail-case (HCS) user whose needs reduce may be
  discharged and given **priority** for ordinary/home-support (HSS) service. [6]
- **Urgent / strong-social-need HSS elderly:** may be **accepted with
  justifications** even if the interRAI CU is not completed or not passed. [4]
- **Dementia / mental-illness cases:** served with trained staff; mental-illness
  (non-elderly) cases must be certified mentally stable and non-violent. [6]
- **60–64 age band (former IHCS OC rule):** could receive service "if there is
  proven need." Under the April-2026 HSS brief the elderly threshold is stated as
  **60+**. ⚠️ Needs Manual Review — confirm the exact current age rule for HSS elderly. [4][6]
- **Boundaries:** services are delivered within defined district/team service
  boundaries (see the "service boundary" links on the main page). [1]
